讀者，也許你以為我在這場場風雲變幻中忘記了羅徹斯特先生。一刻也沒有。他的意念仍與我同在，因為那不是陽光能驅散的水汽，也不是風暴能洗去的沙痕；那是刻在石碑上的名字，注定要與刻在大理石上的名字一樣長久。想知道他的下落的渴望无处不在地追随着我；当我在莫顿时，我每天晚上都会回到我的小屋去想这件事；而现在在摩尔庄园，我每晚都会到我的卧室去想这件事。

In the course of my necessary correspondence with Mr . Briggs about the will , I had inquired if he knew anything of Mr . Rochester ’ s present residence and state of health ; but , as St . I had calculated with certainty on this step answering my end ; I felt sure it would elicit an early answer . I was astonished when a fortnight passed without reply ; but when two months wore away , and day after day the post arrived and brought nothing for me , I fell a prey to the keenest anxiety .

在與布里格斯先生就遺囑進行必要的通信過程中，我曾詢問他是否知道羅徹斯特先生目前的住處和健康狀況，但正如聖約翰所猜測的那樣，他對羅徹斯特先生的一切都一無所知。於是我寫信給費爾法克斯夫人，請她提供這方面的信息。我曾满怀信心地指望这一步能达到我的目的；我觉得这一定能早日得到答复。两星期过去了，没有回信，我很吃惊；但两个月过去了，一天又一天，邮递员来了，却什么也没给我带来，我陷入了极度的焦虑之中。

I wrote again ; there was a chance of my first letter having missed . Renewed hope followed renewed effort ; it shone like the former for some weeks , then , like it , it faded , flickered ; not a line , not a word reached me . When half a year wasted in vain expectancy , my hope died out ; and then I felt dark indeed .

我又写了一封信；我的第一封信有可能被漏掉了。重新努力之后，我又燃起了希望；它像前者一样闪耀了几个星期，然后又像它一样消逝、闪烁；没有一行字、一个词到达我的手中。当我在徒劳的期盼中浪费了半年时间时，我的希望破灭了；这时，我确实感到了黑暗。

I know no medium ; I never in my life have known any medium in my dealings with positive hard characters , antagonistic to my own , between absolute submission and determined revolt . The breeze was from the west ; it came over the hills , sweet with scents of heath and rush ; the sky was of stainless blue ; the stream descending the ravine , swelled with past spring rains , poured along plentiful and clear , catching golden gleams from the sun , and sapphire tints from the firmament . As we advanced and left the tract , we trod a soft turf , mossy , fine , and emerald green , minutely enamelled with a tiny white flower , and spangled with a star - like yellow blossom ; the hills , meantime , shut us quite in ; for the glen , toward its head , wound to their very core .

微风从西边吹来，掠过山丘，带着沼泽和溪流的清香；天空湛蓝如洗；溪流顺着峡谷而下，春雨滋润了溪水，溪水潺潺，清澈见底，在阳光的照耀下闪烁着金色的光芒，在苍穹的映衬下呈现出蓝宝石般的色彩。当我们前进并离开小道时，我们踏上了一片柔软的草皮，长满了青苔，细细的，翠绿的，上面点缀着一朵朵白色的小花，还有星星点点的黄色小花。

“ Let us rest here ,” said St . John , as we reached the first stragglers of a battalion of rocks , guarding a sort of pass , beyond which the beck rushed down , a waterfall , and where , still a little further , the mountain shook off turf and flower , had only heath for raiment , and crag for gem — where it exaggerated the wild to the savage , and exchanged the fresh for the frowning — where it guarded the forlorn hope of solitude , and a last refuge for silence .

圣约翰说："让我们在这里休息一下吧。"这时，我们走到了一排岩石的最前端，那里有一个山口，溪水从山口奔流而下，形成了一个瀑布，再往前走一点，山就不再有草皮和鲜花了，只有石楠做衣服，峭壁做宝石--在这里，它把野性夸张成了野蛮，把清新换成了皱眉--在这里，它守护着孤独的希望，也是寂静的最后庇护所。

I took a seat — St . John stood near me ; he looked up the pass and down the hollow ; his glance wandered away with the stream , and returned to traverse the unclouded heaven which colored it ; he removed his hat , let the breeze stir his hair and kiss his brow ; he seemed in communion with the genius of the haunt ; with his eye he bade farewell to something .

我找了个座位坐下，约翰站在我身边；他仰望着山口，俯视着山谷；他的目光随着溪流游走，又回到溪流上，穿过为溪流增添色彩的澄净的天空；他摘下帽子，让微风吹动他的头发，亲吻他的眉毛；他似乎在与这个鬼地方的天才交流；他用目光向某些东西告别。

“ And I shall see it again ,” he said aloud , “ in dreams , when I sleep by the Ganges ; and again , in a more remote hour — when another slumber overcomes me — on the shore of a darker stream .”

他大声说道："我还会再见到它的，""在梦中，当我睡在恒河边时；在更遥远的时刻，当我再次沉睡时，在更黑暗的溪流岸边，我还会再见到它的"。

Strange words of a strange love ! An austere patriot ’ s passion for his fatherland ! He sat down ; for half an hour we never spoke — neither he to me nor I to him : that interval past , he recommenced :

奇怪的话语，奇怪的爱情！一个朴素的爱国者对祖国的热情！他坐了下来；半小时里，我们都没有说话--他对我，我对他都没有：间隔过去了，他又开始了：

“ Jane , I go in six weeks ; I have taken my berth in an East Indiaman which sails on the twentieth of June .”

"简，我六周后就走，我已经在一艘东印度群岛的船上订好了船位，6月20日启航"。

"上帝会保佑你的，因为你承担了他的工作，"我回答道。

“ Yes ,” said he , “ there is my glory and joy . "是的，"他说，"这是我的荣耀和喜悦。我是一个无懈可击的主人的仆人；我不是在人类的指导下出征，受制于有缺陷的法律和我那些软弱无能的同胞们的错误控制；我的国王、我的立法者、我的船长，是无所不能的；我觉得奇怪的是，我周围的所有人都不愿意在同一面旗帜下应征入伍，加入同一项事业。"

"所有人都没有你的能力，弱者想与强者并肩作战是愚蠢的"。

"我不对弱者说话，也不考虑他们；我只对那些有价值、有能力完成这项工作的人说话。"

"这些人为数不多，而且难以发现。"

"你说得没错；但一旦发现了，就应该激励他们--敦促和劝勉他们努力--向他们展示他们的天赋是什么，以及为什么赋予他们天赋--在他们耳边传达上天的信息--向他们提供上帝直接赐予的一席之地，让他们加入上帝的选民行列。"

"如果他们真的能胜任这项任务，他们自己的内心难道不会首先告诉他们吗？"

我感到仿佛有一种可怕的魔力在我身上环绕、聚集；我颤抖着，生怕听到某个致命的字眼，它会立刻宣告并拴住我的心。

"你的心怎么说？"圣约翰问道。

He had calculated on these first objections ; he was not irritated by them . "我不理解传教士的生活，我从未研究过传教士的工作。

"在那里，我，谦卑的我，可以给你你想要的帮助；我可以每时每刻给你安排任务；永远站在你身边；每时每刻帮助你。一开始我可以这样做；很快（因为我知道你的能力），你就会像我一样强壮、灵巧，不需要我的帮助了。"

"但我的能力--它们在哪里？我感觉不到它们。你说话的时候，我什么也没说，什么也没动。我感觉不到光明的照耀--没有生命的复苏--没有劝慰或鼓舞的声音。哦，我真希望我能让你看到，此时此刻我的心灵多么像一个没有光线的地牢，在它的深处束缚着一种畏缩的恐惧--害怕被你说服去尝试我无法完成的事情！"

"会的，"我有些不屑地肯定道，"完全会的。我有一颗女人心，但与你无关；对你，我有的只是一个同志的坚定；一个同袍的坦率、忠诚、博爱，如果你喜欢的话；一个新兵对他的长官的尊敬和服从；仅此而已--别害怕。"

“ It is what I want ,” he said , speaking to himself ; “ it is just what I want . And there are obstacles in the way ; they must be hewn down . Jane , you would not repent marrying me ; be certain of that ; we must be married . I repeat it , there is no other way ; and undoubtedly enough of love would follow upon marriage to render the union right even in your eyes .”

"这就是我想要的，"他自言自语地说，"这就是我想要的。路上有障碍，必须把它们凿开。简，你不会后悔嫁给我的，这一点你一定要相信，我们必须结婚。我再说一遍，别无他法；毫无疑问，婚后的爱情足以让我们的结合在你眼里也是正确的。

“ I scorn your idea of love ,” I could not help saying , as I rose up and stood before him , leaning my back against the rock . “ I scorn the counterfeit sentiment you offer ; yes , St . John, and I scorn you when you offer it .”

"我蔑视你的爱情观，"我站起来，站在他面前，背靠着岩石，情不自禁地说。"我蔑视你提供的虚假情感；是的，圣约翰，当你提供这种情感时，我也蔑视你。"

He looked at me fixedly , compressing his well - cut lips while he did so . Whether he was incensed or surprised , or what , it is not easy to tell ; he could command his countenance thoroughly .

他定定地看着我，同时紧紧抿着他那剪裁得体的嘴唇。他是被激怒了，还是感到惊讶，或者是什么原因，这不好说；他可以完全控制自己的表情。

“ I scarcely expected to hear that expression from you ,” he said ; “ I think I have done and uttered nothing to deserve scorn .”

他说："我几乎没想到会从你嘴里听到这种说法，""我想我没有做过什么，也没有说过什么值得被蔑视的话。"

I was touched by his gentle tone , and overawed by his high , calm mien .

我被他温和的语气所感动，也被他高大沉稳的神情所震慑。

“ Forgive me the words , St . John ; but it is your own fault that I have been roused to speak so unguardedly . You have introduced a topic on which our natures are at variance — a topic we should never discuss ; the very name of love is an apple of discord between us — if the reality were required , what should we do ? How should we feel ? My dear cousin , abandon your scheme of marriage — forget it .”

"请原谅我的话，圣约翰；但这是你自己的错，我被激怒了，才会如此毫无顾忌地说话。你提出了一个我们天性相悖的话题--一个我们永远不该讨论的话题；爱的名字本身就是我们之间不和的苹果--如果需要现实，我们该怎么办？我们该作何感想？我亲爱的表弟，放弃你的结婚计划吧。

“ No ,” said he ; “ it is a long cherished scheme , and the only one which can secure my great end ; but I shall urge you no further at present . To - morrow I leave home for Cambridge ; I have many friends there to whom I should wish to say farewell . I shall be absent a fortnight ; take that space of time to consider my offer ; and do not forget that if you reject it , it is not me you deny , but God . Through my means , He opens to you a noble career ; as my wife only can you enter upon it . Refuse to be my wife , and you limit yourself forever to a track of selfish ease and barren obscurity . Tremble , lest in that case you should be numbered with those who have denied the faith and are worse than infidels !” 100

"不，"他说，"这是一个珍视已久的计划，也是唯一能实现我的伟大目标的计划；但我现在不会再劝你了。明天我就要离家去剑桥了；我在那里有许多朋友，我想向他们道别。我将离开两星期，请利用这段时间考虑我的提议；不要忘记，如果你们拒绝我的提议，你们拒绝的不是我，而是上帝。上帝通过我为你开辟了崇高的事业；只有作为我的妻子，你才能进入这一事业。拒绝做我的妻子，你就会永远局限于自私自利的安逸和贫瘠晦暗的生活。颤抖吧，免得那样的话，你会与那些否认信仰、比异教徒还坏的人同列！"100

He had done . Turning from me , he once more but this time his feelings were all pent in his heart ; I was not worthy to hear them uttered . As I walked by his side homeward , I read well in his iron silence all he felt toward me : the disappointment of an austere and despotic nature , which has met resistance where it expected submission ; the disapprobation of a cool , inflexible judgment , which has detected in another feelings and views in which it has no power to sympathize ; in short , as a man , he would have wished to coerce me into obedience ; it was only as a sincere Christian he bore so patiently with my perversity , and allowed so long a space for reflection and repentance .

他已经这样做了。他再次从我身边转过身去，但这一次，他的感情全部压抑在心底，我不配听到他说出这些话。当我从他身边回家时，我从他铁青的沉默中读出了他对我的全部感情：他的天性严厉而专横，在期望我顺从的地方却遇到了阻力；他的判断力冷酷而僵硬，在别人身上发现了他无法同情的感情和观点；总之，作为一个男人，他本想强迫我顺从；只有作为一个真诚的基督徒，他才会如此耐心地忍受我的反常，给我留出如此长的反思和忏悔的空间。

That night , after he had kissed his sisters , he thought proper to forget even to shake hands with me , but left the room in silence . I — who , though I had no love , had much friendship for him — was hurt by the marked omission , so much hurt that tears started to my eyes .

那天晚上，他亲吻了他的姐妹们之后，认为应该忘记和我握手，而是默默地离开了房间。我--虽然对他没有爱情，却有很多友情--被这明显的疏忽伤害了，伤害得泪水夺眶而出。

“ I see you and St . John have been quarrelling , Jane ,” said Diana , “ during your walk on the moor . But go after him ; he is now lingering in the passage , expecting you — he will make it up .”

"黛安娜说："简，我看你和圣约翰在荒野散步时一直在吵架。去追他吧；他现在正在通道里徘徊，等着你呢--他会和好的。"

I have not much pride under such circumstances ; I would always rather be happy than dignified , and I ran after him ; he stood at the foot of the stairs .

在这种情况下，我没有多少自尊心；我总是宁可快乐也不愿有尊严，于是我追了上去；他站在楼梯脚下。

“Good- night , St . John ,” said I .

"晚安，圣约翰，"我说

“ Good - night , Jane ,” he replied , calmly .

"晚安，简"他平静地回答

“ Then shake hands ,” I added .

"那就握个手吧，"我补充

What a cold , loose touch he impressed on my fingers ! He was deeply displeased by what had occurred that day ; cordiality would not warm , nor tears move him . No happy reconciliation was to be had with him — no cheering smile or generous word ; but still the Christian was patient and placid ; and when I asked him if he forgave me , he answered that he was not in the habit of cherishing the remembrance of vexation ; that he had nothing to forgive , not having been offended .

他对我手指的触感是多么冰冷、松弛！他对那天发生的事情深感不满；亲切不会温暖他，眼泪也不会打动他。我问他是否原谅了我，他回答说，他没有怀恨在心的习惯；他没有什么可原谅的，因为他没有得罪过人。

And with that answer , he left me . I would much rather he had knocked me down .

就这样，他离开了我。我宁愿他把我打倒在地。
