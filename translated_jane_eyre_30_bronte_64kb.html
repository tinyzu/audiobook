第 XXX 章

The more I knew of the inmates of Moor House , the better I liked them . In a few days I had so far recovered my health that I could sit up all day , and walk out sometimes . I could join with Diana and Mary in all their occupations ; converse with them as much as they wished , and aid them when and where they would allow me . There was a reviving pleasure in this intercourse , of a kind now tasted by me for the first time — the pleasure arising from perfect congeniality of tastes , sentiments , and principles .

我对摩尔庄园的居民了解得越多，就越喜欢他们。没过几天，我的身体就恢复得很好，可以整天坐着，有时还能走出去。我可以与黛安娜和玛丽一起从事她们的所有工作；可以随心所欲地与她们交谈，还可以在她们允许的时间和地点帮助她们。在这种交往中，我第一次尝到了一种令人兴奋的快乐，这种快乐源于品味、情感和原则的完美契合。

I liked to read what they liked to read ; what they enjoyed , delighted me ; what they approved , I reverenced . They loved their sequestered home . I , too , in the gray , small , antique structure , with its low roof , its latticed casements , its mouldering walls , its avenue of aged firs — all grown aslant under the stress of mountain winds ; its garden , dark with yew and holly — and where no flowers but of the hardiest species would bloom — found a charm , both potent and permanent . They clung to the purple moors behind and around their dwelling — to the hollow vale into which the pebbly bridle - path leading from their gate descended ; and which wound between fern - banks first , and then among a few of the wildest little pasture - fields that ever bordered a wilderness of heath , or gave sustenance to a flock of gray moorland sheep , with their little mossy - faced lambs ; they clung to this scene , I say , with a perfect enthusiasm of attachment . I could comprehend the feeling , and share both its strength and truth . I saw the fascination of the locality . I felt the consecration of its loneliness ; my eye feasted on the outline of swell and sweep — on the wild coloring communicated to ridge and dell , by moss , by heath - bell , by flower - sprinkled turf , by brilliant bracken , and mellow granite crag . These details were just to me what they were to them — so many pure and sweet sources of pleasure . The strong blast and the soft breeze ; the rough and the halcyon day ; the hours of sunrise and sunset ; the moonlight and the clouded night , developed for me , in these regions , the same attraction as for them — wound round my faculties the same spell that entranced theirs .

他们喜欢读的书，我也喜欢读；他们喜欢的东西，我也喜欢；他们赞成的东西，我也赞成。他们热爱自己隐居的家。我也一样，在这灰色的、小巧的、古色古香的建筑中，在这低矮的屋顶、格子窗、发霉的墙壁、古老的冷杉林荫道--所有这些都是在山风的吹拂下倾斜生长的；在这长满紫杉和冬青的花园中--除了最耐寒的花朵，没有其他花朵会在这里绽放--我发现了一种既强大又持久的魅力。他们留恋住宅后面和周围的紫色荒原，留恋从大门通往山谷的卵石小路；小路先是在蕨类植物丛中蜿蜒，然后又在几片最荒凉的小牧场中蜿蜒，这些牧场与荒野的石楠林相邻，或养育着一群灰色的荒原绵羊和长着青苔的小羊羔；我说，他们对这一景象充满了依恋之情。我能够理解这种感情，也能分享它的力量和真理。我看到了当地的魅力。我感受到了它的孤寂；我的眼睛尽情地欣赏着山峦起伏的轮廓，欣赏着山脊和山谷、苔藓、石楠花、洒满鲜花的草皮、灿烂的蕨类植物和温润的花岗岩峭壁所呈现出的野性色彩。这些细节对我来说就像它们对他们一样--那么多纯净而甜蜜的快乐源泉。在这些地方，强烈的暴风和柔和的微风；崎岖的山路和晴朗的白天；日出和日落的时间；月光和云雾缭绕的夜晚，对我产生了和他们一样的吸引力--我的大脑也被他们的魔力所吸引。

In - doors we agreed equally well . They were both more accomplished and better read than I was ; but with eagerness I followed in the path of knowledge they had trodden before me . I devoured the books they lent me ; then it was full satisfaction to discuss with them in the evening what I had perused during the day . Thought fitted thought ; opinion met opinion ; we coincided , in short , perfectly .

在室内，我们的意见同样一致。他们都比我更有造诣，读的书也比我多；但我还是满怀热切地追随着他们在我之前走过的知识之路。我读完了他们借给我的书，然后在晚上和他们讨论我白天阅读的内容，这让我感到非常满足。思想与思想相吻合，观点与观点相一致，总之，我们的观点完全一致。

If in our trio there was a superior and a leader , it was Diana . Physically , she far excelled me ; she was handsome ; she was vigorous . In her animal spirits there was an affluence of life and certainty of flow , such as excited my wonder , while it baffled my comprehension . I could talk a while when the evening commenced ; but the first gush of vivacity and fluency gone , I was fain to sit on a stool at Diana ’ s feet , to rest my head on her knee , and listen alternately to her and Mary ; while they sounded thoroughly the topic on which I had but touched . Diana offered to teach me German . I liked to learn of her ; I saw the part of instructress pleased and suited her ; that of scholar pleased and suited me no less . Our natures dovetailed , mutual affection of the strongest kind was the result . They discovered I could draw ; their pencils and color - boxes were immediately at my service . My skill , greater in this one point than theirs , surprised and charmed them . Mary would sit and watch me by the hour together ; then she would take lessons ; and a docile , intelligent , assiduous pupil she made . Thus occupied , and mutually entertained , days passed like hours , and weeks like days .

在我们三人中，如果说有谁比我更优秀，谁是领导者，那一定是黛安娜。在体格上，她远胜于我；她英俊潇洒，精力充沛。在她的动物精神中，有一种丰富的生命力和流动的确定性，这让我感到惊奇，同时也让我无法理解。当晚会开始时，我还能说上几句话；但当第一股活力和流利的语言消失后，我就情不自禁地坐在戴安娜脚边的凳子上，把头靠在她的膝盖上，交替听她和玛丽说话；而她们则把我刚刚触及的话题说得头头是道。戴安娜提出要教我德语。我喜欢向她学习；我看到女教师的角色很适合她，而学者的角色也同样适合我。我们的天性不谋而合，彼此产生了最强烈的好感。他们发现我会画画；他们的铅笔和颜料盒立即为我服务。我的绘画技巧在这一点上比她们更胜一筹，这让她们感到惊讶和着迷。玛丽每小时都会坐在一起看我画画，然后她会去上课，她是一个温顺、聪明、勤奋的学生。就这样，我和她一起学习，一起娱乐，一天就像几小时一样过去了，几周就像几天一样过去了。

As to Mr . St . John , the intimacy which had arisen so naturally and rapidly between me and his sisters did not extend to him . One reason of the distance yet observed between us was , that he was comparatively seldom at home ; a large proportion of his time appeared devoted to visiting the sick and poor among the scattered population of his parish .

至于圣约翰先生，我和他的姐妹们之间自然而然地迅速产生的亲密关系并没有延伸到他身上。我们之间的距离之所以如此遥远，一个原因是他很少在家；他的大部分时间似乎都用来探望教区里分散的病人和穷人。

No weather seemed to hinder him in these pastoral excursions ; rain or fair , he would , when his hours of morning study were over , take his hat , and , followed by his father’ s old pointer , Carlo , go out on his mission of love or duty — I scarcely know in which light he regarded it . Sometimes , when the day was very unfavorable , his sisters would expostulate . He would then say , with a peculiar smile , more solemn than cheerful —

无论刮风下雨，他都会在晨读时间结束后，带上帽子，在父亲的老指针卡洛的跟随下，去完成他的爱的使命或责任--我几乎不知道他是怎么看待这件事的。有时，天公不作美，他的姐姐们就会说三道四。这时，他会带着一种特殊的微笑说"如果我让一阵风或一阵雨把我从这些轻松的任务中撇开，这种懒惰对我向自己提出的未来又有什么准备呢？"

“ And if I let a gust of wind or a sprinkling of rain turn me aside from these easy tasks , what preparation would such sloth be for the future I propose to myself ?”

Diana and Mary’ s general answer to this question was a sigh , and some minutes of apparently mournful meditation .

黛安娜和玛丽对这个问题的回答一般都是叹息，以及几分钟明显哀伤的沉思。

But besides his frequent absences , there was another barrier to friendship with him ; he seemed of a reserved , an abstracted , and even a brooding nature . Zealous in his ministerial labors , blameless in his life and habits , he yet did not appear to enjoy that mental serenity , that inward content , which should be the reward of every sincere Christian and practical philanthropist . Often of an evening , when he sat at the window , his desk and papers before him , he would cease reading or writing , rest his chin on his hand , and deliver himself up to I know not what course of thought ; but that it was perturbed and exciting might be seen in the frequent flash and changeful dilation of his eye .

但是，除了他经常缺席之外，与他交朋友还有另一个障碍：他似乎生性矜持、抽象，甚至忧心忡忡。他热心于牧师的工作，生活和习惯无可指责，但他似乎并不享受精神上的宁静和内心的满足，而这种宁静和满足应该是每一个真诚的基督徒和实际的慈善家的回报。晚上，当他坐在窗前，面前摆着书桌和文件时，他常常会停止阅读或写作，用手托着下巴，不知在想些什么；但从他的眼睛经常闪烁和不断变化的涣散中，可以看出他的思绪是烦乱和激动的。

I think , moreover , that Nature was not to him that treasury of delight it was to his sisters . He expressed once , and but once , in my hearing , a strong sense of the rugged charm of the hills , and an inborn affection for the dark roof and hoary walls he called his home ; but there was more of gloom than pleasure in the tone and words in which the sentiment was manifested ; and never did he seem to roam the moors for the sake of their soothing silence — never seek out or dwell upon the thousand peaceful delights they could yield .

此外，我认为，大自然对他来说并不像对他的姐妹们那样是快乐的宝库。他曾在我的耳边表达过一次，但也仅仅是一次，他强烈地感受到了山丘崎岖不平的魅力，对他称之为家的阴暗的屋顶和苍老的墙壁有着与生俱来的感情；但在表达这种感情的语气和言辞中，更多的是阴郁而不是愉悦；他似乎从来没有为了荒野的宁静而漫游过荒野，从来没有寻找或沉浸在荒野所能带来的千百种宁静的愉悦之中。

Incommunicative as he was , some time elapsed before I had an opportunity of gauging his mind . I first got an idea of its calibre when I heard him preach in his own church at Morton . I wish I could describe that sermon ; but it is past my power . I cannot even render faithfully the effect it produced on me .

虽然他不善言辞，但过了一段时间，我才有机会了解他的想法。当我在莫顿他自己的教堂里听他布道时，我第一次对他的思想有了一个大概的了解。我希望能描述一下那次布道，但我已力不从心。我甚至无法忠实地描述它给我带来的影响。

It began calm — and indeed , as far as delivery and pitch of voice went , it was calm to the end ; an earnestly felt , yet strictly restrained zeal breathed soon in the distinct accents , and prompted the nervoushg language . This grew to force — compressed , condensed , controlled . The heart was thrilled , the mind astonished , by the power of the preacher ; neither were softened . Throughout there was a strange bitterness ; an absence of consolatory gentleness ; stern allusions to Calvinistic doctrines — election , predestination , reprobationhh — were frequent ; and each reference to these points sounded like a sentence pronounced for doom . When he had done , instead of feeling better , calmer , more enlightened by his discourse , I experienced an inexpressible sadness ; for it seemed to me — I know not whether equally so to others — that the eloquence to which I had been listening had sprung from a depth where lay turbid dregs of disappointment — where moved troubling impulses of insatiate yearnings and disquieting aspirations . I was sure St . John Rivers — pure - lived , conscientious , zealous as he was — had not yet found that peace of God which passeth all understanding ; 83 he had no more found it , I thought , than had I ; with my concealed and racking regrets for my broken idol and lost elysium — regrets to which I have latterly avoided referring ; but which possessed me and tyrannized over me ruthlessly .

讲道一开始很平静--事实上，就讲道的方式和声音的高低而言，讲道一直都很平静；一种恳切的、但严格克制的热情很快在清晰的口音中流露出来，并推动着紧张的语言。这种热情逐渐被压缩、凝练和控制。传道人的力量让人心潮澎湃，精神为之震撼；但两者都没有软化。自始至终都有一种奇怪的苦涩；没有安慰性的温柔；经常严厉地暗指加尔文教义--拣选、宿命、责备；每次提到这些观点听起来都像是在宣判末日。他讲完后，我非但没有因他的演讲而感觉好些、平静些、开明些，反而感到一种难以言表的悲伤；因为在我看来--我不知道其他人是否也这样认为--我一直在聆听的雄辩似乎是从一个深渊中涌出的，那里蕴藏着失望的浊流--那里涌动着令人不安的冲动，有贪得无厌的渴望和令人不安的愿望。我确信，圣约翰-里弗斯--生活纯洁、兢兢业业、热心肠的他--还没有找到上帝的那份超越一切理解的平安；83 我想，他和我一样，也没有找到那份平安；我对我破碎的偶像和失去的 "极乐世界 "怀有隐秘而痛苦的悔恨--我后来一直避免提及这些悔恨，但这些悔恨却无情地占据着我，支配着我。

Meantime , a month was gone . Diana and Mary were soon to leave Moor House , and return to the far different life and scene which awaited them , as governesses in a large , fashionable , south - of - England city ; where each held a situation in families , by whose wealthy and haughty members they were regarded only as humble dependents , and who neither knew nor sought one of their innate excellences , and appreciated only their acquired accomplishments as they appreciated the skill of their cook , or the taste of their waiting - woman . Mr . St . John had said nothing to me yet about the employment he had promised to obtain for me ; yet it became urgent that I should have a vocation of some kind . One morning , being left alone with him a few minutes in the parlor , I ventured to approach the window - recess - which his table , chair , and desk consecrated as a kind of study — and I was going to speak ; though not very well knowing in what words to frame my inquiry — for it is at all times difficult to break the ice of reserve glassing over such natures as his — when he saved me the trouble , by being the first to commence a dialogue .

与此同时，一个月过去了。黛安娜和玛丽很快就要离开摩尔庄园，回到等待着她们的截然不同的生活和场景中去，在英格兰南部的一个时尚的大城市里做家庭教师；在那里，每个人都在家庭中占有一席之地，那些富有而傲慢的家庭成员只把她们当作卑微的附属品，他们既不知道也不寻求她们与生俱来的优点，只欣赏她们后天的成就，就像欣赏厨师的手艺或女招待的品位一样。圣约翰先生还没有对我说过他答应为我找的工作，但我急需一份职业。一天早上，我和他在客厅里独处了几分钟，我大胆地走近窗边，他的桌子、椅子和书桌把窗边当成了书房，我正想开口说话，却不知道该用什么词来表达我的询问--在他这样的天性面前，任何时候都很难打破矜持的坚冰--这时，他省去了我的麻烦，率先开始了对话。

Looking up as I drew near —“ You have a question to ask of me ?” he said .

当我走近时，他抬起头来--"你有问题要问我？"他说。

“ Yes ; I wish to know whether you have heard of any service I can offer myself to undertake .”

"是的，我想知道你是否听说过我可以提供的服务。"

“ I found or devised something for you three weeks ago ; but as you seemed both useful and happy here — as my sisters had evidently become attached to you , and your society gave them unusual pleasure — I deemed it inexpedient to break in on your mutual comfort till their approaching departure from Marsh - End should render yours necessary .”

"三个星期前，我就为你找到或设计了一些东西；但由于你在这里似乎既有用又快乐--我的姐妹们显然已经对你产生了感情，你的社交给她们带来了非同寻常的快乐--我认为在她们即将离开沼泽地而需要你之前，不宜打扰你们的相互慰藉。"

“ And they will go in three days now ?” I said .

"她们三天后就要走了吗？"我说。

“ Yes ; and when they go , I shall return to the parsonage at Morton ; Hannah will accompany me ; and this old house will be shut up .”

"是的，他们走后，我就回莫顿的牧师住宅，汉娜会陪我一起走，这栋老房子就关起来。"

I waited a few moments , expecting he would go on with the subject first broached ; but he seemed to have entered another train of reflection ; his look denoted abstraction from me and my business . I was obliged to recall him to a theme which was of necessity one of close and anxious interest to me .

我等了一会儿，本以为他会继续刚才的话题，但他似乎进入了另一个思考的轨道；他的神情对我和我的事情都很抽象。我不得不让他重新回到一个主题上来，这个主题必然是我非常感兴趣的。

“ What is the employment you had in view , Mr . Rivers ? I hope this delay will not have increased the difficulty of securing it .”

"里弗斯先生，你打算做什么工作？我希望这次耽搁不会增加你找到工作的难度"。

“ Oh , no ; since it is an employment which depends only on me to give , and you to accept .”

"哦，不会；因为这个工作只取决于我给不给，你接受不接受。"

He again paused ; there seemed a reluctance to continue . I grew impatient ; a restless movement or two , and an eager and exacting glance fastened on his face , conveyed the feeling to him as effectually as words could have done , and with less trouble .

他又停顿了一下，似乎不愿继续说下去。我开始不耐烦了；一两个不安分的动作，以及紧盯着他的脸的急切而苛刻的目光，都向他传达了这种感觉，就像语言所能做到的那样有效，而且省事。

“ You need be in no hurry to hear ,” he said ; “ let me frankly tell you , I have nothing eligiblehi or profitable to suggest . Before I explain , recall , if you please , my notice , clearly given , that if I helped you , it must be as the blind man would help the lame . I am poor ; for I find that , when I have paid my father ’ s debts , all the patrimony remaining to me will be this crumbling grange , the row of scathed firs behind , and the patch of moorish soil , with the yew - trees and holly - bushes in front . I am obscure ; Rivers is an old name ; but of the three sole descendants of the race , two earn the dependent’ s crust among strangers , and the third considers himself an alien from his native country — not only for this life , but in death . Yes , and deems , and is bound to deem himself honored by the lot ; and aspires but after the day when the cross of separation from fleshly ties shall be laid on his shoulders , and when the Head of that church - militant of whose humblest members he is one , shall give the word , ‘ Rise , follow me !’ ”

"你不用急着听，"他说，"让我坦率地告诉你，我没有什么合乎时宜或有益的建议。在我解释之前，如果你愿意，请回想一下我已经清楚地告诉过你，如果我帮助你，就必须像盲人帮助瘸子一样。我很穷，因为我发现，等我还清了父亲的债务，我剩下的遗产就只有这个破败的庄园，后面一排残败的枞树，以及前面一片荒芜的土地，还有紫杉树和冬青树。我是个默默无闻的人，里弗斯是个古老的姓氏，但在这个种族的三个唯一后裔中，有两个是在陌生人中间挣取生活费的，第三个则认为自己是故乡的异乡人--不仅生前如此，死后也是如此。是的，他认为，而且必然会认为自己因这一命运而感到光荣；他渴望的只是有一天，当脱离肉体束缚的十字架被放在他的肩上，当他作为最卑微成员之一的教会战士的首领发出'起来，跟我走！'的口号时。"

St . John said these words as he pronounced his sermons , with a quiet , deep voice ; with an unflushed cheek , and a coruscating radiance of glance . He resumed —

圣约翰在布道时说这些话时，声音平静而低沉，脸颊不红，目光炯炯有神。他接着说--

“ And since I am myself poor and obscure , I can offer you but a service of poverty and obscurity . You may even think it degrading — for I see now your habits have been what the world calls refined ; your tastes lean to the ideal ; and your society has at least been among the educated — but I consider that no service degrades which can better our race . I hold that the more arid and unreclaimed the soil where the Christian laborer’ s task of tillage is appointed him — the scantier the meed his toil brings — the higher the honor . His , under such circumstances , is the destiny of the pioneer ; and the first pioneers of the Gospel were the Apostles — their captain was Jesus , the Redeemer himself .”

"既然我自己贫穷而默默无闻，我只能为你们提供贫穷而默默无闻的服务。你们甚至可能认为这有失身份--我现在看到你们的习惯是世人所说的高雅；你们的品味偏向理想；你们的社会至少是受过教育的社会--但我认为，没有什么服务能使我们的种族变得更好。我认为，基督徒劳动者耕作的土地越是干旱贫瘠，他的劳动所得越是微薄，他的荣誉就越高。在这种情况下，他的命运就是先驱者的命运；而福音的第一批先驱者就是使徒们，他们的队长就是耶稣，救世主本人。

“ Well !” I said , as he again paused —“ proceed .”

"嗯！"我说，他又停顿了一下，"继续"。

He looked at me before he proceeded ; indeed , he seemed leisurely to read my face , as if its features and lines were characters on a page . The conclusions drawn from this scrutiny he partially expressed in his succeeding observations .

他先看了我一眼，然后才继续说下去；事实上，他似乎在悠闲地阅读我的脸，仿佛我脸上的特征和线条就是一页纸上的字符。从这种审视中得出的结论，他在接下来的观察中作了部分表述。

“ I believe you will accept the post I offer you ,” said he , “ and hold it for a while ; not permanently though , any more than I could permanently keep the narrow and narrowing — the tranquil , hidden office of English country incumbent ; for in your nature is an alloy as detrimental to repose as that in mine ; though of a different kind .”

"我相信你会接受我给你的职位，"他说，"而且会担任一段时间；但不会长期担任，就像我无法长期担任英国乡村任职者这个狭窄、宁静、隐蔽的职位一样；因为在你的天性中，有一种合金与我的天性中的合金一样不利于安宁，尽管种类不同。"

“ Do explain ,” I urged , when he halted once more .

“ I will ; and you shall hear how poor the proposal is — how triv ial — how cramping . I shall not stay long at Morton , now that my father is dead , and that I am my own master . I shall leave the place probably in the course of a twelvemonth ; but while I do stay , I will exert myself to the utmost for its improvement . Morton , when I came to it two years ago , had no school ; the children of the poor were excluded from every hope of progress . I established one for boys ; I mean now to open a second school , for girls . I have hired a building for the purpose , with a cottage of two rooms attached to it for the mistress ’ house . Her salary would be thirty pounds a year ; her house is already furnished , very simply , but sufficiently , by the kindness of a lady , Miss Oliver , the only daughter of the sole rich man in my parish — Mr . Oliver , the proprietor of a needle factory and iron foundry in the valley . The same lady pays for the education and clothing of an orphan from the workhouse , on condition that she shall aid the mistress in such menial offices connected with her own house and the school , as her occupation of teaching will prevent her having time to discharge in person . Will you be this mistress ?”

"我会的，你会听到这个提议是多么糟糕，多么琐碎，多么令人窒息。我不会在莫顿久留，现在我父亲死了，我是自己的主人。也许十二个月后我就会离开这个地方，但在我逗留期间，我会尽最大努力改善它。两年前我来到莫顿时，这里还没有一所学校；穷人家的孩子被排除在一切进步的希望之外。我创办了一所男校，现在我打算再办一所女校。为此，我租用了一栋楼房，附带一栋两室一厅的小屋，作为女校长的住所。她的年薪是 30 英镑；她的房子已经有了家具，非常简单，但很充足，这是一位女士奥利弗小姐的好意，她是我教区唯一的富人奥利弗先生的独生女，奥利弗先生是山谷里一家针厂和铸铁厂的老板。奥利弗先生是山谷里一家针纺厂和铸铁厂的老板。这位女士资助了一名孤儿的教育和衣物，条件是她必须协助女主人处理与她自己的房子和学校有关的琐碎事务，因为她的职业是教书，没有时间亲自处理这些事务。您愿意做这个女主人吗？

He put the question rather hurriedly ; he seemed half to expect an indignant , or at least a disdainful , rejection of the offer ; not knowing all my thoughts and feelings , though guessing some , he could not tell in what light the lot would appear to me . In truth it was humble — but then it was sheltered , and I wanted a safe asylum ; it was plodding — but then , compared with that of a governess in a rich house , it was independent ; and the fear of servitude with strangers entered my soul like iron ; it was not ignoble — not unworthy — not mentally degrading . I made my decision .

他问得相当匆忙；他似乎半信半疑，以为我会愠怒地，或者至少是不屑一顾地拒绝这个提议；他不知道我的所有想法和感受，尽管猜到了一些，但他无法判断我对这份工作的看法。事实上，它是卑微的，但它又是遮风避雨的，我需要一个安全的庇护所；它是蹒跚的，但与富裕人家的家庭教师相比，它又是独立的；对陌生人奴役的恐惧像铁一样刺入我的灵魂；它并不卑微，也不不不值得，更不是精神上的堕落。我做出了决定。

“ I thank you for the proposal , Mr . Rivers ; and I accept it with all my heart .”

"感谢您的提议，里弗斯先生，我全心全意地接受。"

“ But you comprehend me ?” he said . “ It is a village school ; your scholars will be only poor girls — cottagers ’ children — at the best , farmers ’ daughters . Knitting , sewing , reading , writing , ciphering , will be all you will have to teach . What will you do with your accomplishments ? What with the largest portion of your mind — sentiments — tastes ?”

"但你能理解我吗？"他说。"这是一所乡村学校，你的学生都是穷苦的女孩，乡下人的孩子，顶多是农民的女儿。编织、缝纫、阅读、写作、密码，这就是你要教的一切。你们将如何对待自己的成就？你心灵中最重要的部分--情感--品味又该如何处理？"

“ Save them till they are wanted . They will keep .”

"留到需要的时候再用它们会保留下来的"

“ You know what you undertake , then ?”

"那你知道你要做什么吗？"

“ I do .”

He now smiled ; and not a bitter or a sad smile ; but one well pleased and deeply gratified .

他现在笑了，不是苦笑，也不是悲伤的笑，而是欣慰和深深的满足。

“ And when will you commence the exercise of your function ?”

"那你什么时候开始履行职责呢？"

“ I will go to my house to - morrow ; and open the school , if you like , next week .”

"我明天就回自己家，如果您愿意，下周就开学。"

“ Very well ; so be it .”

"很好，就这样吧。"

He rose and walked through the room . Standing still , he again looked at me . He shook his head .

他站起身，穿过房间。站定后，他又看了我一眼。他摇了摇头。

“ What do you disapprove of , Mr . Rivers ?” I asked .

"你不赞成什么，里弗斯先生？"我问道。

“ You will not stay at Morton long ; no , no !”

"你不会在莫顿久留，不会，不会！"

“ Why ? What is your reason for saying so ?”

"为什么，你这么说的理由是什么？

“ I read it in your eye ; it is not of that description which promises the maintenance of an even tenor in life .”

"我从你的眼睛里看出来了；你的眼睛并不是那种能让你保持平和心态的眼睛。"

“ I am not ambitious .”

"我没有野心。

He started at the word “ ambitious .” He repeated , “ No . What made you think of ambition ? Who is ambitious ? I know I am ; but how did you find it out ?”

他一听到 "野心 "这个词就怔住了。他重复道："不，你怎么会想到野心？谁有野心？我知道我有，但你是怎么发现的？"

“ I was speaking of myself .”

"我说的是我自己。"

“ Well , if you are not ambitious , you are —” He paused .

"好吧，如果你没有野心，那你就是--"他停顿了一下。

“ What ?”

"什么？"

“ I was going to say , impassioned ; but perhaps you would have misunderstood the word , and been displeased . I mean , that human affections and sympathies have a most powerful hold on you . I am sure you cannot long be content to pass your leisure in solitude , and to devote your working hours to a monotonous labor wholly void of stimulus , any more than I can be content ,” he added , with emphasis , “ to live here buried in morass , pent in with mountain — my nature , that God gave me , contravened ; my faculties , Heaven - bestowed , paralyzed — made useless .84 You hear now how I contradict myself . I , who preached contentment with a humble lot , and justified the vocation even of hewers of wood , and drawers of water , in God ’ s service — I , his ordained minister , almost rave in my restlessness . Well , propensities and principles must be reconciled by some means .”

"我本想说，慷慨激昂；但也许你会误解这个词，并感到不快。我的意思是，人类的情感和同情心对你有着最强大的吸引力。我相信你不会满足于在孤独中打发闲暇，把工作时间投入到完全没有刺激的单调劳动中，就像我不能满足于，"他加重语气，补充道，"生活在这里，埋没在泥沼中，被大山压抑着--我的天性，上帝赐予我的，被违背了；我的能力，上天赐予我的，被麻痹了，变得毫无用处。我曾宣扬安于卑微的命运，甚至为樵夫和汲水者为上帝服务的天职辩护，而我，他所任命的牧师，却几乎在我的焦躁不安中咆哮。好吧，倾向和原则必须通过某种方式来调和。

He left the room . In this brief hour I had learned more of him than in the whole previous month ; yet still he puzzled me .

他离开了房间。在这短短的一小时里，我对他的了解比前一个月还要多，但他仍然让我困惑。

Diana and Mary Rivers became more sad and silent as the day approached for leaving their brother and their home . They both tried to appear as usual ; but the sorrow they had to struggle against was one that could not be entirely conquered or concealed . Diana intimated that this would be a different parting from any they had ever yet known . It would probably , as far as St . John was concerned , be a parting for years — it might be a parting for life .

随着离开哥哥和家的日子一天天临近，戴安娜和玛丽-里弗斯变得更加悲伤和沉默。她们都试图表现得像往常一样；但她们不得不与悲伤作斗争，这种悲伤是无法完全战胜或掩盖的。戴安娜暗示说，这将是一次不同于他们以往任何一次的离别。就圣约翰而言，这可能是多年的离别，也可能是一生的离别。

“ He will sacrifice all to his long - framed resolves ,” she said ; “ natural affection and feelings more potent still . St . John looks quiet , Jane , but he hides a fever in his vitals . You would think him gentle , yet in some things he is inexorable as death ; and the worst of it is , my conscience will hardly permit me to dissuade him from his severe decision ; certainly I cannot for a moment blame him for it . It is right , noble , Christian ; yet it breaks my heart .” And the tears gushed to her fine eyes . Mary bent her head low over her work .

她说："他会为他的夙愿牺牲一切，"她说："自然的感情和感觉更加强烈。圣约翰看起来很平静，简，但他的内心却在发烧。你会觉得他很温和，但在有些事情上，他却像死神一样不可抗拒；最糟糕的是，我的良心几乎不允许我劝阻他放弃他的重大决定；当然，我一刻也不能责怪他。这是正确的、高尚的、基督徒的决定，但却令我心碎。泪水夺眶而出。玛丽低头伏案工作。

“ We are now without father ; we shall soon be without home and brother ,” she murmured .

"她喃喃地说："我们现在没有了父亲，很快就会失去家和兄弟。

At that moment a little accident supervened , which seemed decreed by fate purposely to prove the truth of the adage that “ Misfortunes never come singly ”; and to add to their distresses the vexing one of the slip between the cup and the lip . St . John passed the window reading a letter . He entered .

就在这时，一个小小的意外发生了，这似乎是命运特意安排的，以证明 "不幸从来都不是单独发生的 "这句格言的真谛；而且，在他们的苦恼中，又多了一个令人烦恼的杯弓蛇影。圣约翰从窗前经过，正在读一封信。他进来了。

“ Our Uncle John is dead ,” said he .

"他说："我们的约翰叔叔死了

Both the sisters seemed struck ; not shocked or appalled ; the tidings appeared in their eyes rather momentous than afflicting .

姐妹俩似乎都吓了一跳，但并没有震惊或骇然；在她们眼里，这个消息与其说是痛苦，不如说是重大的消息。

“ Dead ?” repeated Diana .

"死了？"黛安娜重复道。

“ Yes .”

"是的。"

She riveted a searching gaze on her brother ’ s face . “ And what then ?” she demanded , in a low voice .

她探究地注视着弟弟的脸。"然后呢？"她低声问道。

“ What then , Di ?” he replied , maintaining a marble immobility of feature . “ What then ? Why — nothing . Read .”

"然后呢，黛安娜？"他回答道，保持着大理石般的不动声色。"然后呢？没什么。读吧。"

He threw a letter into her lap . She glanced over it , and handed it to Mary . Mary perused it in silence , and returned it to her brother . All three looked at each other , and all three smiled — a dreary , pensive smile enough .

他把一封信扔到她腿上。她扫了一眼，然后递给玛丽。玛丽默默地看了看，又还给了她哥哥。三个人互相看了一眼，都笑了--一个沉闷而深思的微笑。

“ Amen ! We can yet live ,” said Diana , at last .

"阿门！我们还能活下去，"戴安娜最后说。

"玛丽说："不管怎么说，我们的处境不会比以前更糟。

里弗斯先生说："只是这让我们的脑海中浮现出一幅可能的画面，"他说，"与现在的情况形成了鲜明的对比。"

他把信叠好，锁在书桌里，然后走了出去。

“ Jane , you will wonder at us and our mysteries ,” she said ; “ and think us hard - hearted beings not to be more moved at the death of so near a relation as an uncle ; but we have never seen him or known him . He was my mother ’ s brother . My father and he quarrelled long ago . It was by his advice that my father risked most of his property in the speculation that ruined him . Mutual recriminations passed between them ; they parted in anger , and were never reconciled . My uncle engaged afterward in more prosperous undertakings ; it appears he realized a fortune of twenty thousand pounds . He was never married , and had no near kindred but ourselves , and one other person , not more closely related than we . My father always cherished the idea that he would atone for his error by leaving his possessions to us . That letter informs us that he has bequeathed every penny to the other relation , with the exception of thirty guineas , to be divided between St . John , Diana , and Mary Rivers , for the purchase of three mourning rings . He had a right , of course , to do as he pleased ; and yet a momentary damp is cast on the spirits by the receipt of such news . "简，你一定会对我们的神秘感到惊讶，"她说，"你一定会认为我们是铁石心肠的人，不会因为舅舅这样的近亲去世而更加感动；但我们从未见过他，也不认识他。他是我母亲的哥哥。很久以前，我父亲和他吵过架。正是在他的建议下，我父亲冒着失去大部分财产的风险进行投机，结果毁了自己。他们相互指责，愤然分手，从此再无和好。我叔叔后来从事了更成功的事业；他似乎获得了两万英镑的财富。他终身未娶，没有近亲，只有我们和另外一个比我们关系不近的人。父亲一直认为，他将把财产留给我们，以弥补他的过错。那封信告诉我们，他把所有的钱都留给了另一位亲属，只有三十几尼除外，这三十几尼将由圣约翰、戴安娜和玛丽-里弗斯三人平分，用于购买三个孝戒。当然，他有权这样做；然而，听到这样的消息，我们的心情瞬间变得沮丧。如果玛丽和我每人能得到一千英镑，我们都会觉得自己很富有；而对圣约翰来说，这笔钱也是很有价值的，因为它能让他做一些好事"。

This explanation given , the subject was dropped , and no further reference made to it , by either Mr . Rivers or his sisters . The next day , I left Marsh - End for Morton .
