But at that moment the summons sounded for dinner : all reentered the house . The odor which now filled the refectory was scarcely more appetizing than that which had regaled our nostrils at breakfast ; the dinner was served in two huge tin - plated vessels , whence rose a strong steam redolent of rancid fat . I found the mess to consist of indifferent potatoes and strange shreds of rustyaj meat , mixed and cooked together . Of this preparation a tolerably abundant plateful was apportioned to each pupil . I ate what I could , and wondered within myself whether every day ’ s fare would be like this .

但就在这时，晚餐的召唤声响了起来：所有人都回到了屋子里。晚餐是用两个巨大的镀锡器皿盛的，冒着浓浓的油脂馊味。我发现饭菜是由冷淡的土豆和奇怪的锈肉丝混合在一起煮成的。在这些食物中，每个学生都分到了相当丰盛的一盘。我吃了我能吃的，心里想，是不是每天的伙食都是这样的。

After dinner we immediately adjourned to the schoolroom : lessons recommenced , and were continued till five o ‘ clock .

晚饭后，我们立即回到教室：课程重新开始，一直持续到五点钟。

The only marked event of the afternoon was , that I saw the girl with whom I had conversed in the verandah dismissed in disgrace , by Miss Scatcherd , from a history class , and sent to stand in the middle of the large school - room . The punishment seemed to me in a high degree ignominious , especially for so great a girl — she looked thirteen or upward . I expected she would show signs of great distress and shame ; but , to my surprise , she neither wept nor blushed . Composed , though grave , she stood , the central mark of all eyes . “ How can she bear it so quietly — so firmly ?” I asked of myself . “ Were I in her place , it seems to me I should wish the earth to open and swallow me up . She looks as if she were thinking of something beyond her punishment — beyond her situation ; of something not round her nor before her . I have heard of day - dreams - is she in a day - dream now ? Her eyes are fixed on the floor , but I am sure they do not see it — her sight seems turned in , gone down into her heart . She is looking at what she can remember , I believe ; not at what is really present . I wonder what sort of a girl she is — whether good or naughty .”

下午发生的唯一一件大事就是，我看到在阳台上和我交谈过的那个女孩被斯卡德小姐从历史课上不光彩地赶走了，并被罚站在大教室的中央。在我看来，这种惩罚是非常可耻的，尤其是对这么大的一个女孩来说--她看上去才十三岁上下。我本以为她会表现出非常痛苦和羞愧的样子，但令我惊讶的是，她既没有哭泣，也没有脸红。虽然表情严肃，她却镇定自若地站着，成为所有人目光的焦点。"她怎么能如此平静，如此坚定地承受这一切？我问自己。"如果我是她，我似乎希望大地裂开，把我吞没。她看起来好像在想她所受的惩罚之外的东西，她所处的境遇之外的东西，她所想的东西既不在她身边，也不在她面前。我听说过白日梦，她现在是在做白日梦吗？她的眼睛盯着地板，但我确信她的眼睛没有看到地板--她的视线似乎转了进去，进入了她的内心。我相信，她在看她能记起的东西，而不是真实存在的东西。我想知道她是个怎样的女孩，是善良还是淘气。

Soon after five P . M ., we had another meal , consisting of a small mug of coffee and half a slice of brown bread . I devoured my bread and drank my coffee with relish ; but I should have been glad of as much more — I was still hungry . Half an hour ’ s recreation succeeded , then study ; then the glass of water and the piece of oat - cake , prayers , and bed . Such was my first day at Lowood .

下午五点后不久，我们又吃了一顿饭，包括一小杯咖啡和半片黑面包。我狼吞虎咽地吃着面包，津津有味地喝着咖啡；但我还想再多吃点，因为我还饿着肚子。接着是半小时的娱乐活动，然后是学习；然后是一杯水和一块燕麦饼、祈祷和睡觉。这就是我在罗伍德的第一天。
