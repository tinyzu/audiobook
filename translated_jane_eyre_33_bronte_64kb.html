When Mr . St . John went , it was beginning to snow ; the whirling storm continued all night . The next day a keen wind brought fresh and blinding falls ; by twilight the valley was drifted up and almost impassable . I had closed my shutter , laid a mat to the door to prevent the snow from blowing in under it , trimmed my fire , and , after sitting nearly an hour on the hearth , listening to the muffled fury of the tempest , I lighted a candle , took down Marmion and , beginning —

圣约翰先生去的时候，天开始下雪；呼啸的暴风雪持续了整整一夜。第二天，一阵狂风带来了新的鹅毛大雪；到了黄昏时分，山谷里飘起了雪花，几乎无法通行。我关上百叶窗，在门上铺上垫子，以防雪从门缝里吹进来，修整好炉火，在炉台上坐了近一个小时，听着暴风雪闷闷的怒号，我点上蜡烛，取下马尔米恩，开始--

日落在诺勒姆城堡般的陡峭上

我很快就在音乐中忘记了风暴。

I heard a noise ; the wind , I thought , shook the door . No ; it was St . John Rivers , who , lifting the latch , came in out of the frozen hurricane , the howling darkness , and stood before me , the cloak that covered his tall figure all white as a glacier . I was almost in consternation , so little had I expected any guest from the blocked - up vale that night .

我听到一阵响声；我以为是风在摇门。不，是圣约翰-里弗斯，他掀开门闩，从冰冻的飓风和嚎叫的黑暗中走了进来，站在我面前，他高大的身影披着的斗篷像冰川一样洁白。我几乎惊呆了，因为我几乎没有想到那天晚上会有客人从堵塞的谷地里来。

"有什么坏消息吗？"我问道。"发生什么事了吗？"

“ No . How very easily alarmed you are !” he answered , removing his cloak and hanging it up against the door , toward which he again coolly pushed the mat which his entrance had deranged . He stamped the snow from his boots .

"没有，你真容易惊慌！"他回答，脱下斗篷，挂在门边，又冷冷地把他进来时弄乱的垫子推向门。他踩了踩靴子上的雪。

“ I shall sully the purity of your floor ,” said he , “ but you must excuse me for once .” Then he approached the fire . “ I have had hard work to get here , I assure you ,” he observed , as he warmed his hands over the flame . “ One drift took me up to the waist ; happily the snow is quite soft yet .”

"我会玷污你地板的纯洁，"他说，"但你必须原谅我这一次。"然后他走近火堆"我向你保证，我好不容易才来到这里，"他边说边把手放在火上取暖。"一次飘雪把我的腰都冻僵了，幸好雪还很松软。"

“ But why are you come ?” I could not forbear saying .

"但你为什么要来？我忍不住问道。

“ Rather an inhospitable question to put to a visitor ; but , since you ask it , I answer , simply , to have a little talk with you ; I got tired of my mute books and empty rooms . Besides , since yesterday , I have experienced the excitement of a person to whom a tale has been half told , and who is impatient to hear the sequel .”

"对一个访客来说，这是个不礼貌的问题；不过，既然你问了，我的回答很简单，就是想和你聊会儿天；我已经厌倦了我的哑巴书本和空荡荡的房间。此外，从昨天开始，我已经体验到了一个故事讲了一半就迫不及待想听续集的人的激动心情。

He sat down . I recalled his singular conduct of yesterday , and really I began to fear his wits were touched . If he were insane , however , his was a very cool and collected insanity ; I had never seen that handsome - featured face of his look more like chiselled marble than it did just now , as he put aside his snow - wet hair from his forehead , and let the fire - light shine free on his pale brow and cheek as pale ; where it grieved me to discover the hollow trace of care or sorrow now so plainly graved . I waited , expecting he would say something I could at least comprehend ; but his hand was now at his chin , his finger on his lip ; he was thinking . It struck me that his hand looked wasted like his face . A perhaps uncalled - for gush of pity came over my heart ; I was moved to say —

他坐了下来。我回想起他昨天的奇怪举动，真担心他的智力受到了触动。然而，如果他是疯了，那也是一种非常冷静和镇定的疯了；我从未见过他那英俊的脸庞看起来像大理石雕刻的一样，就像刚才那样，他拨开额前被雪打湿的头发，让火光自由地照耀着他苍白的眉毛和同样苍白的脸颊；在那里，我悲伤地发现了空洞的忧虑或悲伤的痕迹，现在却如此清晰地刻在那里。我等待着，希望他能说一些我至少能理解的话；但他的手正托着下巴，手指放在嘴唇上；他在思考。我突然发现，他的手看起来像他的脸一样消瘦。我心中涌起一股也许是无缘无故的怜悯之情；我动情地说--

“ I wish Diana or Mary would come and live with you ; it is too bad that you should be quite alone ; and you are recklessly rash about your own health .”

"我希望黛安娜或玛丽能来和你一起住；你一个人太可怜了；你对自己的健康太轻率了。"

“ Not at all ,” said he ; “ I care for myself when necessary ; I am well now . What do you see amiss in me ?”

"一点也不，"他说，"必要时我会照顾自己，我现在很好。你觉得我有什么不对劲吗？

This was said with a careless , abstracted indifference , which showed that my solicitude was , at least in his opinion , wholly superfluous . I was silenced .

这话说得漫不经心，抽象冷漠，表明我的关心，至少在他看来，完全是多余的。我沉默了。

He still slowly moved his finger over his upper lip , and still his eye dwelt dreamily on the glowing grate ; thinking it urgent to say something , I asked him presently if he felt any cold draught from the door , which was behind him .

他的手指仍然慢慢地在上唇上移动，眼睛仍然梦幻般地盯着发光的炉排；我觉得急需说点什么，于是问他是否感到身后的门有冷风吹来。

“ No , no ,” he responded , shortly and somewhat testily .

"没有，没有，"他简短而有些试探地回答道。

“ Well ,” I reflected , “ if you won ’ t talk , you may be still ; I ’ ll let you alone now , and return to my book .”

"好吧，"我想了想，"如果你不说话，那你就静静地呆着吧；我现在让你一个人呆着，回去看我的书。"

So I snuffed the candle , and resumed the perusal of Marmion . He soon stirred ; my eye was instantly drawn to his movements ; he only took out a moroccohr pocket - book , thence produced a letter which he read in silence , folded it , put it back , relapsed into meditation . It was vain to try to read with such an inscrutable fixture before me ; nor could I , in my impatience , consent to be dumb ; he might rebuff me if he liked , but talk I would .

于是，我掐灭了蜡烛，继续阅读马尔米恩的书。他很快就动了起来；我的视线立刻被他的动作吸引住了；他只是拿出一个软皮口袋书，然后拿出一封信，默默地读了一遍，折好，放回原处，重新陷入沉思。面对这样一个高深莫测的人，我想读也是白费力气；我不耐烦了，也不能同意哑巴吃黄连；如果他愿意，他可以回绝我，但我还是要说。

“ Have you heard from Diana and Mary lately ?”

"你最近有戴安娜和玛丽的消息吗？

“ Not since the letter I showed you a week ago .”

"一周前我给你看过信后就没有了"

“ There has not been any change made about your own arrangements ? You will not be summoned to leave England sooner than you expected ?”

"你自己的安排没有任何变化吗？你不会比预想的更早被召唤离开英国吧？"

“ I fear not , indeed ; such chance is too good to befall me .”

"恐怕不会，这种机会不会降临在我身上。"

Baffled so far , I changed my ground — I bethought myself to talk about the school and my scholars .

莫名其妙至此，我改变了话题，转而谈起了学校和我的学生们。

“ Mary Garrett ’ s mother is better , and Mary came back to the school this morning , and I shall have four new girls next week from the Foundry Close — they would have come today but for the snow .”

"玛丽-加勒特的母亲好些了，玛丽今天上午回到了学校，下周我将从铸造厂附近新来四个女孩，要不是因为下雪，她们今天就会来了。"

“ Indeed ?”

"真的吗？"

"奥利弗先生付了两个的学费"

"是吗？"

"他打算在圣诞节时犒劳全校师生"

“ Yes .”

停顿的空白再次出现；钟敲了八下。钟声唤醒了他；他松开双腿，坐直身子，转向我。

"他说："把你的书放一会儿，到火边来。

Wondering , and of my wonder finding no end , I complied .

“ Half an hour ago ,” he pursued , “ I spoke of my impatience to hear the sequel of a tale ; on reflection , I find the matter will be better managed by my assuming the narrator ’ s part , and converting you into a listener . Before commencing , it is but fair to warn you that the story will sound somewhat hackneyed in your ears ; but stale details often regain a degree of freshness when they pass through new lips . For the rest , whether trite or novel , it is short .

"半小时前，"他接着说，"我曾说过，我迫不及待地想听一个故事的续集；经过思考，我发现这件事最好由我来扮演叙述者的角色，把你变成一个听众。在开始之前，我必须提醒各位，这个故事在你们的耳朵里听起来会有些老套；但是陈旧的细节在经过新的嘴唇之后，往往会恢复一定程度的新鲜感。至于其他部分，无论是老套还是新奇，都很简短。

“ Twenty years ago , a poor curate — never mind his name at this moment — fell in love with a rich man ’ s daughter ; she fell in love with him , and married him , against the advice of all her friends , who consequently disowned her immediately after the wedding . Before two years passed , the rash pair were both dead , and laid quietly side by side under one slab . Charity carried the friendless thing to the house of its rich maternal relations ; it was reared by an aunt - in - law , called ( I come to names now ) Mrs . Reed of Gateshead — you start — did you hear a noise ? I dare say it is only a rat , scrambling along the rafters of the adjoining school - room : it was a barn before I had it repaired and altered , and barns are generally haunted by rats . To proceed . Mrs . Reed kept the orphan ten years ; whether it was happy or not with her , I cannot say , never having been told ; but at the end of that time she transferred it to a place you know — being no other than Lowood school , where you so long resided yourself . It seems her career there was very honorable ; from a pupil , she became a teacher , like yourself — really it strikes me there are parallel points in her history and yours — she left it to be a governess ; there again , your fates were analogous ; she undertook the education of the ward of a certain Mr . Rochester .”

"二十年前，一个穷牧师--先不管他叫什么名字--爱上了一个富人的女儿；她爱上了他，并不顾所有朋友的劝阻嫁给了他，结果婚后朋友们立刻就不认她了。还没过两年，这对草率的男女就双双死去，静静地并排躺在一块石板下。(我见过他们的坟墓；那是郡里一个杂草丛生的制造业小镇的一个巨大的教堂院子的部分路面，周围是灰黑色的老教堂。）他们留下了一个女儿，女儿刚出生，慈爱就把她抱在了怀里--就像我今晚差点陷在雪堆里一样冷。慈善家把这个没有朋友的孩子抱到了她富有的娘家，由一位姨妈抚养，这位姨妈叫（我现在说到名字了）盖茨黑德的里德太太。我敢说，那只是一只老鼠，正沿着隔壁教室的椽子爬来爬去：在我对它进行修缮和改造之前，它还是一个谷仓，而谷仓一般都有老鼠出没。继续。里德夫人把这个孤儿养了十年；孤儿和她在一起是否快乐，我无从得知；但在十年结束时，她把孤儿转到了一个你知道的地方--那就是洛伍德学校，也就是你长期居住的地方。在那里，她的职业生涯似乎非常光荣；她从一名学生成为了一名教师，就像你一样--我真的觉得她的历史和你的历史有相似之处--她离开了学校，成为了一名家庭教师；在那里，你们的命运也相似；她承担了某位罗切斯特先生的监护人的教育工作。"

“ Mr . Rivers !” I interrupted .

"里弗斯先生！我打断了他的话。

“ I can guess your feelings ,” he said , “ but restrain them for a while ; I have nearly finished : hear me to the end . Of Mr . Rochester ’ s character I know nothing , but the one fact that he professed to offer honorable marriage to this young girl , and that at the very altar she discovered he had a wife yet alive , though a lunatic . What his subsequent conduct and proposals were , is a matter of pure conjecture ; but when an event transpired which rendered inquiry after the governess necessary , it was discovered she was gone — no one could tell when , where , or how . She had left Thornfield Hall in the night ; every research after her course had been vain ; the country had been scoured far and wide ; no vestige of information could be gathered respecting her . Yet that she should be found is become a matter of serious urgency ; advertisements have been put in all the papers ; I myself have received a letter from one Mr . Briggs , a solicitor , communicating the details I have just imparted . Is it not an odd tale ?”

"我能猜到你的感受，"他说，"但请暂时克制一下，我已经快讲完了，请听我讲完。羅徹斯特先生的人品我一無所知，只知道他曾向這位年輕的姑娘提過一樁光榮的婚事，但在婚禮上，她發現他有一位妻子還活著，雖然是個瘋子。至于他后来的行为和求婚，纯属猜测；但当发生了一件事，有必要对家庭教师进行调查时，却发现她已经离开了--没有人知道她是何时、何地、如何离开的。她在夜里离开了桑菲尔德庄园；对她的行踪进行的所有调查都是徒劳的；全国各地都找遍了；没有任何关于她的信息。然而，找到她已成为一件刻不容缓的大事；所有报纸都登了寻人启事；我本人也收到了一位名叫布里格斯的律师的来信，告知了我刚才透露的细节。这难道不是一个奇怪的故事吗？

"就告诉我这个吧，"我说，"既然你知道这么多，肯定能告诉我--罗切斯特先生怎么样了？他在哪里？他在做什么？他還好嗎？

“ I am ignorant of all concerning Mr . Rochester ; the letter never mentions him but to narrate the fraudulent and illegal attempt I have adverted to . You should rather ask the name of the governess — the nature of the event which requires her appearance .”

"我對羅徹斯特先生一無所知；信中從未提及他，只是敘述了我剛才提到的詐騙和非法企圖。你應該問問家庭教師的名字--這件事的性質需要她出現。"

“ Did no one go to Thornfield Hall , then ? Did no one see Mr . Rochester ?”

"那么没有人去桑菲尔德庄园吗？沒人見到羅徹斯特先生嗎？"

“ I suppose not .”

"我想没有"

“ But they wrote to him ?”

"但他們給他寫信了？

“ Of course .”

"當然"

“ And what did he say ? Who has his letters ?”

"他怎麼說？誰有他的信？

“ Mr . Briggs intimates that the answer to his application was not from Mr . Rochester , but from a lady ; it is signed ‘ Alice Fairfax .’ ”

"布里格斯先生暗示，给他的回信不是罗切斯特先生写的，而是一位女士写的，署名是'爱丽丝-费尔法克斯'。"

I felt cold and dismayed ; my worst fears , then , were probably true ; he had in all probability left England and rushed in reckless desperation to some former haunt on the continent . And what opiate for his severe sufferings — what object for his strong passions — had he sought there ? I dared not answer the question . Oh , my poor master — once almost my husband — whom I had often called “ my dear Edward ”!

我感到又冷又沮丧；我最担心的事情可能是真的；他很可能已经离开英国，不顾一切地奔向欧洲大陆的某个旧居。他在那里寻找的是什么鸦片来缓解他剧烈的痛苦，是什么东西来满足他强烈的激情？我不敢回答这个问题。哦，我可怜的主人--曾经几乎是我的丈夫--我常常称他为 "我亲爱的爱德华"！

“ He must have been a bad man ,” observed Mr . Rivers .

"他一定是个坏人，"里弗斯先生说。

“ You don ’ t know him — don ’ t pronounce an opinion upon him ,” I said with warmth .

"你不了解他，不要对他发表意见，"我热情地说。

“ Very well ,” he answered , quietly ; “ and , indeed , my head is otherwise occupied than with him ; I have my tale to finish . Since you won ’ t ask the governess ’ name , I must tell it of my own accord — stay — I have it here — it is always more satisfactory to see important points written down , fairly committed to black and white .”

"很好，"他平静地回答，"事实上，我的脑子里想的不是他，我还有我的故事要讲完。既然你不问家庭教师的名字，那我就自己讲吧--请留步，我这里有--重要的事情写下来，白纸黑字写得清清楚楚，总是比较令人满意的。"

And the pocket - book was again deliberately produced , opened , sought through ; from one of its compartments was extracted a shabby slip of paper , hastily torn off ; I recognized in its texture , and its stains of ultra - marine , and lake , and vermilion , the ravished margin of the portrait - cover . He got up , held it close to my eyes ; and I read , traced in Indian ink , in my own handwriting , the words “ JANE EYRE ”— the work , doubtless , of some moment of abstraction .

他又特意拿出了那个小本子，打开，翻找；从其中一个夹层里取出了一张匆忙撕下的破旧纸条；我从它的质地、超深蓝、湖蓝和朱红的污渍中认出了肖像封面被蹂躏过的边缘。他站起身来，把那张纸片凑近我的眼睛，我读到了用印度墨水描摹的 "简-爱 "几个字，那是我自己的笔迹--毫无疑问，那是某个抽象时刻的杰作。

“ Briggs wrote to me of a Jane Eyre ,” he said ; “ the advertisements demanded a Jane Eyre ; I knew a Jane Elliott . I confess I had my suspicions ; but it was only yesterday afternoon they were at once resolved into certainty . You own the name , and renounce the alias ?”

他说："布里格斯写信给我，说有个叫简-爱的人，"他说："广告上说有个叫简-爱的人，我知道有个叫简-艾略特的人。我承认我有过怀疑，但就在昨天下午，我的怀疑一下子变成了肯定。你拥有这个名字，放弃了化名？"

‘ Yes — yes — but where is Mr . Briggs ? He , perhaps , knows more of Mr . Rochester than you do .”

是的，是的，但是布里格斯先生呢？也許他比你更瞭解羅徹斯特先生"

“ Briggs is in London ; I should doubt his knowing anything at all about Mr . Rochester ; it is not in Mr . Rochester he is interested . Meantime you forget essential points in pursuing trifles ; you do not inquire why Mr . Briggs sought after you — what he wanted with you .”

"布里格斯在倫敦；我懷疑他對羅徹斯特先生是否有所瞭解；他感興趣的並非羅徹斯特先生。與此同時，你在追求瑣事時忘記了要點；你沒有詢問布里格斯先生為何要找你，他找你有什麼事。"

“ Well , what did he want ?”

"他想要什么？"

“ Merely to tell you that your uncle , Mr . Eyre of Madeira , is dead ; that he has left you all his property , and that you are now rich — merely that — nothing more .”

"只是想告诉你，你的叔叔，马德拉岛的埃尔先生死了；他把他所有的财产都留给了你，你现在很富有--仅此而已--没有别的了。"

“ I ! rich ?”

"我！有钱了？"

“ Yes , you , rich — quite an heiress .”

"是的，你，富有了--相当一个女继承人。

Silence succeeded .

Besides , the entire fortune is your right ; my uncle gained it by his own efforts ; he was free to leave it to whom he would ; he left it to you . After all , justice permits you to keep it ; you may , with a clear conscience , consider it absolutely your own .”

"我确实看到了某种公正，但这有悖于所有习俗。此外，全部财产都是你的权利；我叔叔是靠自己的努力得来的；他可以自由地把它留给他愿意留下的人，但他把它留给了你。毕竟，正义允许你保留它；你可以问心无愧地认为它绝对是你自己的"。

“ With me ,” said I , “ it is fully as much a matter of feeling as of conscience . I must indulge my feelings , I so seldom have had an opportunity of doing so . Were you to argue , object , and annoy me for a year , I could not forego the delicious pleasure of which I have caught a glimpse — that of repaying , in part , a mighty obligation , and winning to myself life - long friends .”

"对我来说，"我说，"这完全是一个感情和良心的问题。我必须放纵自己的感情，因为我很少有机会这样做。即使你争论、反对、烦扰我一年，我也不能放弃我已经瞥见的那份美味的快乐--那就是部分地偿还我的大恩大德，为我自己赢得终生的朋友。"

“ You think so now ,” rejoined St . John , “ because you do not know what it is to possess , nor consequently to enjoy , wealth . You cannot form a notion of the importance twenty thousand pounds would give you ; of the place it would enable you to take in society ; of the prospects it would open to you . You cannot —”

"你现在这么想，"圣约翰反驳道，"因为你不知道什么是拥有财富，也不知道什么是享受财富。你无法想象两万英镑对你的重要性，无法想象它能让你在社会上占据怎样的地位，无法想象它能为你开辟怎样的前景。你无法--"

“ And you ,” I interrupted , “ cannot at all imagine the craving I have for fraternal and sisterly love . I never had a home , I never had brothers and sisters ; I must and will have them now . You are not reluctant to admit me and own me , are you ?”

"而你，"我打断道，"根本无法想象我对兄弟姐妹之爱的渴望。我从未有过一个家，从未有过兄弟姐妹；我现在必须也将拥有他们。你不是不愿意接纳我，拥有我吧？"

“ Jane , I will be your brother — my sisters will be your sisters — without stipulating for this sacrifice of your just rights .”

"简，我愿意做你的哥哥，我的妹妹们也愿意做你的妹妹，而不需要牺牲你的正当权利。"

“ Brother ? Yes , at the distance of a thousand leagues ! Sisters ? Yes , slaving among strangers ! I , wealthy — gorged with gold I never earned and do not merit ! You , penniless ! Famous equality and fraternization ! Close union ! Intimate attachment !”

"兄弟？是的，在千里之外！姐妹？是的，在陌生人中间做奴隶！我，富得流油，金子是我不劳而获的，也是我不配拥有的！你，身无分文！著名的平等和友爱亲密的结合！亲密无间！

“ But , Jane , your aspirations after family ties and domestic happiness may be realized otherwise than by the means you contemplate ; you may marry .”

"但是，简，你对家庭纽带和家庭幸福的渴望可以通过其他方式来实现，而不是通过你所考虑的方式；你可以结婚。"

“ Nonsense again ! Marry ! I don ’ t want to marry , and never shall marry .”

"又胡说八道！结婚！我不想结婚，也永远不会结婚"。

“ That is saying too much ; such hazardous affirmations are a proof of the excitement under which you labor .”

"你说得太多了，这种危险的断言证明了你的激动。

“ It is not saying too much ; I know what I feel , and how averse are my inclinations to the bare thought of marriage . No one would take me for love ; and I will not be regarded in the light of a mere money - speculation . And I do not want a stranger — unsympathizing , alien , different from me . I want my kindred — those with whom I have full fellow - feeling . Say again you will be my brother ; when you uttered the words , I was satisfied , happy ; repeat them , if you can , repeat them sincerely .”

"这并不过分，我知道自己的感受，也知道自己对婚姻有多么厌恶。没有人会把我当做爱情；我也不愿意被看成是单纯的金钱投机。我不想要一个陌生人--不同情我的人，异类，与我不同的人。我要的是我的同类--那些与我有深厚感情的人。再说一遍，你愿意做我的兄弟；当你说出这句话时，我很满意，很高兴；重复一遍，如果可以的话，真诚地重复一遍。

“ I think I can . I know I have always loved my own sisters , and I know on what my affection for them is grounded — respect for their worth , and admiration of their talents . You , too , have principle and mind ; your tastes and habits resemble Diana ’ s and Mary ’ s ; your presence is always agreeable to me ; in your conversation I have already , for some time , found a salutary solace . I feel I can easily and naturally make room in my heart for you as my third and youngest sister .”

"我想我可以。我知道我一直爱着自己的妹妹们，我也知道我对她们的感情是基于什么--对她们价值的尊重，对她们才华的钦佩。你也有原则和头脑；你的品味和习惯与黛安娜和玛丽相似；你的出现总是让我感到愉快；在你的谈话中，我已经有一段时间找到了有益的慰藉。我觉得我可以很容易、很自然地在心里把你当成我的三妹和小妹。

“ Thank you ; that contents me for to - night . Now you had better go ; for if you stay longer , you will , perhaps , irritate me afresh by some mistrustful scruple .”

"谢谢你，今晚我就满足了。现在你最好走吧；因为如果你再呆下去，也许你会因为一些不信任的顾虑而重新激怒我。"

“ And the school , Miss Eyre ? It must now be shut up , I suppose ?”

"学校呢，爱小姐？我想现在必须关闭了吧？"

“ No . I will retain my post of mistress till you get a substitute .”

"不，在你找到替代人选之前，我会继续担任女校长。"

He smiled approbation ; we shook hands , and he took leave .

他露出了赞许的微笑；我们握了握手，他就告辞

I need not narrate in detail the further struggles I had , and arguments I used , to get matters regarding the legacy settled as I wished . My task was a very hard one ; but , as I was absolutely resolved — as my cousins saw , at length , that my mind was really and immutably fixed on making a just division of the property — as they must in their own hearts have felt the equity of the intention ; and must , besides , have been innately conscious that , in my place , they would have done precisely what I wished to do — they yielded , at length , so far as to consent to put the affair to arbitration . The judges chosen were Mr . Oliver and an able lawyer . Both coincided in my opinion ; I carried my point . The instruments of transfer were drawn out ; St . John , Diana , Mary , and I each became possessed of a competency .

为了如愿以偿地解决遗产问题，我又进行了怎样的斗争，使用了怎样的论据，我就不必细述了。我的任务非常艰巨；但是，由于我的决心很大--我的堂兄弟们终于看出了我的心思确实是坚定不移地要对财产进行公正的分割--他们自己心里肯定也感觉到了这一意图的公平性；此外，他们心里肯定也意识到，如果换做是我，他们也会做我希望做的事情--他们终于屈服了，同意将此事提交仲裁。被选中的法官是奥利弗先生和一位能干的律师。两人的意见不谋而合，我坚持了自己的观点。转让文书已经拟好；圣约翰、戴安娜、玛丽和我各自拥有了一项权限。
