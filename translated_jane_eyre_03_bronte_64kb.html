The next thing I remember is , waking up with a feeling as if I had had a frightful nightmare , and seeing before me a terrible red glare , crossed with thick , black bars . I heard voices , too , speaking with a hollow sound , and as if muffled by a rush of wind or water ; agitation , uncertainty , and an all - predominating sense of terror , confused my faculties . Ere long , I became aware that some one was handling me ; lifting me up , and supporting me in a sitting posture ; and that more tenderly than I had ever been raised or upheld before . I rested my head against a pillow or an arm , and felt easy .

我记得的下一件事是，我醒来时感觉好像做了一场可怕的噩梦，眼前是一片可怕的红光，横着粗粗的黑杠。我还听到有人在说话，声音很空洞，好像被一阵风或一阵水冲得闷闷的；激动、不确定，还有一种无处不在的恐怖感，让我的意识混乱不堪。没过多久，我意识到有人在抚摸我，把我扶起来，让我坐着，而且比以往任何时候都温柔。我把头靠在枕头或手臂上，感觉很轻松。

In five minutes more , the cloud of bewilderment dissolved . I knew quite well that I was in my own bed , and that the red glare was the nursery fire . It was night ; a candle burned on the table . Bessie stood at the bed - foot with a basin in her hand , and a gentleman sat in a chair near my pillow , leaning over me .

又过了五分钟，迷惑的阴云消散了。我清楚地知道，我是在自己的床上，而红光就是幼儿园的火光。是夜，桌上燃着一支蜡烛。贝茜站在床边，手里端着一个脸盆，一位先生坐在我枕边的椅子上，靠在我身上。

I felt an inexpressible relief , a soothing conviction of protection and security , when I knew that there was a stranger in the room ; an individual not belonging to Gateshead , and not related to Mrs . Reed . Next day , by noon , I was up and dressed , and sat wrapped in a shawl by the nursery hearth . 我多么希望能完整地回答这个问题！但要做出任何回答又是多么困难！孩子们会感受，但他们不会分析自己的感受；如果分析部分是在思考中完成的，他们也不知道如何用语言表达这一过程的结果。然而，我害怕失去这第一次也是唯一一次通过传递悲伤来缓解我的悲伤的机会，在不安地停顿了一会儿后，我想出了一个微不足道的回答，尽管就其内容而言，它是真实的。

“ Nonsense ! And is it that makes you so miserable ? Are you afraid now , in daylight ?”

"首先，我没有父亲或母亲，也没有兄弟姐妹。"

“ No ; but night will come again before long ; and , besides , I am unhappy , very unhappy , for other things .”

"你有一个好心的姨妈和表姐妹"

“ What other things ? Can you tell me some of them ?”

我再次停顿了一下，然后笨拙地回答道：

How much I wished to reply fully to this question ! How difficult it was to frame any answer ! Children can feel , but they cannot analyze their feelings ; and if the analysis is partially effected in thought , they know not how to express the result of the process in words . Fearful , however , of losing this first and only opportunity of relieving my grief by imparting it , I , after a disturbed pause , contrived to frame a meagre , though , as far as it went , true response .

"但是约翰-里德把我打倒了 我姨妈把我关在红屋子里"

“ For one thing , I have no father or mother , brothers or sisters .”

劳埃德先生第二次拿出了他的鼻烟盒。

“ You have a kind aunt and cousins .”

"你不觉得盖茨黑德庄园是座非常漂亮的房子吗？"他问道。"能住在这么好的地方，你不觉得很感激吗？"

Again I paused ; then bunglingly enounced :

"这不是我的房子，先生；修道院长说我在这里的权利还不如一个仆人。"

“ But John Reed knocked me down , and my aunt shut me up in the red - room .”

"呸！你不会傻到想离开这么好的地方吧？"

Mr . Lloyd a second time produced his snuff - box .

"如果我有别的地方可去，我会很高兴离开这里；但我永远也离不开盖茨黑德，除非我是个女人。"

“ Don ’ t you think Gateshead Hall a very beautiful house ?” asked he . “ Are you not very thankful to have such a fine place to live at ?”

"谁知道呢？除了里德太太，你还有其他亲戚吗？"

“ It is not my house , sir ; and Abbot says I have less right to be here than a servant .”

"我想没有 先生

“ Pooh ! you can ’ t be silly enough to wish to leave such a splendid place ?”

"没有你父亲的亲戚吗？"

“ If I had anywhere else to go , I should be glad to leave it ; but I can never get away from Gateshead till I am a woman .”

"我不知道我曾经问过里德姨妈，她说我可能有一些叫埃尔的穷亲戚，但她对他们一无所知。

“ Perhaps you may — who knows ? Have you any relations besides Mrs . Reed ?”

"如果你有这样的亲戚，你愿意去找他们吗？"

“ I think not , sir .”

我思考着。贫穷对成年人来说是可怕的，对孩子们来说更是如此：他们对勤劳、工作、体面的贫穷没有什么概念；他们认为贫穷这个词只与衣衫褴褛、食物匮乏、炉灶无火、举止粗鲁和堕落的恶习联系在一起；对我来说，贫穷就是堕落的代名词。

“ None belonging to your father ?”

"我回答说："不，我不喜欢属于穷人。

“ I don ’ t know . I asked Aunt Reed once , and she said possibly I might have some poor , low relations called Eyre ; but she knew nothing about them .”

"即使他们对你好也不行吗？"

“ If you had such , would you like to go to them ?”

我摇了摇头。我不明白穷人怎么会有办法对你好；然后学他们说话，学他们的举止，不接受教育，长大后像我有时在盖茨黑德村的茅屋门前看到的那些照看孩子或洗衣服的贫穷妇女一样；不，我还没有英雄气概到用种姓的代价来换取自由。

I reflected . Poverty looks grim to grown people ; still more so to children : they have not much idea of industrious , working , respectable poverty ; they think of the word only as connected with ragged clothes , scanty food , fireless grates , rude manners , and debasing vices ; poverty for me was synonymous with degradation .

"但是，你的亲戚就这么穷吗？他们是劳动人民吗？

“ No ; I should not like to belong to poor people ,” was my reply .

"我说不清楚。里德姨妈说，如果我有亲戚，他们一定是乞丐；我不想去乞讨。

“ Not even if they were kind to you ?”

"你想去上学吗？"

I shook my head . I could not see how poor people had the means of being kind ; and then to learn to speak like them , to adopt their manners , to be uneducated , to grow up like one of the poor women I saw sometimes nursing their children or washing their clothes at the cottage doors of the village of Gateshead ; no , I was not heroic enough to purchase liberty at the price of caste .

我再次思考起来。我几乎不知道学校是什么。约翰-里德讨厌他的学校，还辱骂他的主人；但约翰-里德的口味不适合我的口味，如果说贝西对学校纪律的描述（从她来盖茨黑德之前住过的一个家庭的年轻女士那里收集的）有些骇人听闻，那么她对这些年轻女士所取得的某些成就的详细描述，我觉得同样很有吸引力。她夸耀说，她们画了很多美丽的风景画和花卉画；她们会唱歌，会弹奏乐曲，会编钱包，会翻译法文书籍；听得我心潮澎湃，跃跃欲试。此外，上学将是一个彻底的改变；它意味着一次长途旅行，意味着与盖茨黑德的彻底分离，意味着进入一种新的生活。

“ But are your relatives so very poor ? Are they working people ?”

"我确实想去上学，"这是我思考后得出的结论。

“ I cannot tell . Aunt Reed says if I have any , they must be a beggarly set ; I should not like to go a - begging .”

"好吧，好吧，谁知道会发生什么呢？"劳埃德先生站起来说。"孩子应该换换空气和环境，"他自言自语地补充道，"神经状态不好。"

“ Would you like to go to school ?”

贝茜现在回来了；就在这时，听到马车驶上墓道的声音。

Again I reflected . I scarcely knew what school was . Bessie sometimes spoke of it as a place where young ladies sat in the stocks , p wore back - boards , q and were expected to be exceedingly genteel and precise ; John Reed hated his school , and abused his master ; but John Reed’ s tastes were no rule for mine , and if Bessie’ s accounts of school -discipline (gathered from the young ladies of a family where she had lived before coming to Gateshead ) were somewhat appalling , her details of certain accomplishments attained by these same young ladies were , I thought , equally attractive . She boasted of beautiful paintings of landscapes and flowers by them executed ; of songs they could sing , and pieces they could play , of purses they could net , of French books they could translate ; till my spirit was moved to emulation as I listened . Besides , school would be a complete change ; it implied a long journey , an entire separation from Gateshead , an entrance into a new life .

"是你的女主人吗，护士？"劳埃德先生问道，"我想在走之前跟她说几句话。"

“ I should , indeed , like to go to school ,” was the audible conclusion of my musings .

贝茜请他走进早餐室，并带路走了出去。在接下来他和里德太太的谈话中，我想，从他的言谈举止中，我们可以看出他对里德太太的关心。从后来发生的事情来看，我猜想药剂师大胆地建议把我送去上学，而这个建议无疑很容易就被采纳了；有一天晚上，我上床睡觉后，艾博特和贝茜坐在育儿室里做针线活，她们都以为我睡着了，艾博特和贝茜在讨论这个问题时说："小姐说，她很高兴能摆脱这样一个令人讨厌、不懂事的孩子，她看起来总是在监视每个人，在暗地里搞阴谋诡计。"我想，方丈认为我是一个幼稚的盖伊-福克斯。

“ Well , well ; who knows what may happen ?” said Mr . Lloyd , as he got up . “ The child ought to have change of air and scene ,” he added , speaking to himself ; “ nerves not in a good state .”

就在那次，我第一次从艾博特小姐给贝茜的信中得知，我的父亲是个穷牧师；我的母亲违背了朋友们的意愿嫁给了他，朋友们认为这门亲事有失她的身份；我的祖父里德对她的忤逆非常恼火，不给她一先令就把她赶出了家门；我母亲和父亲结婚一年后，父亲在一个制造业大城镇的穷人家做客时染上了斑疹伤寒，他在那里当牧师，当时这种病在那里很流行；我母亲被他传染了，两个人在一个月内相继去世。

Bessie now returned ; at the same moment the carriage was heard rolling up the gravel - walk .

贝茜听了这段叙述，叹了口气说："可怜的简小姐也真可怜，方丈。"

“ Is that your mistress , nurse ?” asked Mr . Lloyd : “ I should like to speak to her before I go .”

"是的，"方丈回答道，"如果她是个漂亮的好孩子，人们也许会同情她的不幸；但像这样的小癞蛤蟆，人们真的无法关心。"

Bessie invited him to walk into the breakfast - room , and led the way out . In the interview which followed between him and Mrs . Reed , I presume , from after - occurrences , that the apothecary ventured to recommend my being sent to school ; and the recommendation was no doubt readily enough adopted ; for , as Abbot said , in discussing the subject with Bessie , when both sat sewing in the nursery one night , after I was in bed , and , as they thought , asleep , “ Missis was , she dared say , glad enough to get rid of such a tiresome , ill - conditioned child , who always looked as if she were watching everybody , and scheming plots underhand .” Abbot , I think , gave me credit for being a sort of infantine Guy Fawkes .

"肯定不是，"贝茜同意道，"无论如何，像乔治亚娜小姐这样的美人在同样的情况下会更动人。

On that same occasion I learned , for the first time , from Miss Abbot ’ s communications to Bessie , that my father had been a poor clergyman ; that my mother had married him against the wishes of her friends , who considered the match beneath her ; that my grandfather Reed was so irritated at her disobedience , he cut her off without a shilling ; that after my mother and father had been married a year , the latter caught the typhus fever while visiting among the poor of a large manufacturing town where his curacy was situated , and where that disease was then prevalent ; that my mother took the infection from him , and both died within a month of each other .

"是的，我很喜欢乔治亚娜小姐！"修道院长热切地喊道。"小可爱！她长长的卷发，蓝蓝的眼睛，肤色甜美，就像画出来的一样！贝茜，我想吃威尔士兔子做晚餐。

Bessie , when she heard this narrative , sighed and said , “ Poor Miss Jane is to be pitied , too , Abbot .”

"我也想，再加上烤洋葱。来吧，我们下去。"她们走了。

“ Yes ,” responded Abbot ; “ if she were a nice , pretty child , one might compassionate her forlornness ; but one really cannot care for such a little toad as that .”

“ Not a great deal , to be sure ,” agreed Bessie ; “ at any rate , a beauty like Miss Georgiana would be more moving in the same condition .”

“ Yes , I dote on Miss Georgiana !” cried the fervent Abbot . “ Little darling ! with her long curls and her blue eyes , and such a sweet color as she has ; just as if she were painted ! Bessie , I could fancy a Welsh rabbit for supper .”

“ So could I — with a roast onion . Come , we ’ ll go down .” They went .
