﻿ Chapter XXVIII

第二十八章

Two days are passed . It is a summer evening ; the coachman has set me down at a place called Whitcross ; he could take me no further for the sum I had given , and I was not possessed of another shilling in the world . The coach is a mile off by this time ; I am alone . At this moment I discover that I forgot to take my parcel out of the pocket of the coach , where I had placed it for safety ; there it remains , there it must remain ; and now I am absolutely destitute .

两天过去了。这是一个夏日的傍晚；马车夫把我送到了一个叫惠特克罗斯的地方；我给了他一笔钱，他不能再送我了，而我在这个世界上又没有一先令。这时马车已经走了一英里，只有我一个人。这时，我发现我忘了从马车的口袋里取出我的包裹，为了安全起见，我把它放在了口袋里。

Whitcross is no town , nor even a hamlet ; it is but a stone pillar set up where four roads meet ; white - washed , I suppose , to be more obvious at a distance and in darkness . Four arms spring from its summit ; the nearest town to which these point is , according to the inscription , distant ten miles ; the furthest , above twenty . From the well-known names of these towns I learn in what county I have lighted ; a north - midland shire , dusk with moorland , ridged with mountain ; this I see . There are great moors behind and on each hand of me ; there are waves of mountains far beyond that deep valley at my feet . The population here must be thin , and I see no passengers on these roads ; they stretch out east , west , north , and south — white , broad , lonely ; they are all cut in the moor , and the heather grows deep and wild to their very verge . Yet a chance traveller might pass by ; and I wish no eye to see me now ; strangers would wonder what I am doing , lingering here at the sign - post , evidently objectless and lost . I might be questioned ; I could give no answer but what would sound incredible and excite suspicion . Not a tie holds me to human society at this moment — not a charm or hope calls me where my fellow - creatures are — none that saw me would have a kind thought or a good wish for me . I have no relative but the universal mother , Nature ; I will seek her breast and ask repose .

怀特克罗斯不是一个小镇，甚至也不是一个村落；它不过是四条路交汇处的一根石柱；我想，它被粉刷成白色，是为了在远处和黑暗中更显眼。从石柱顶端伸出四条臂膀；根据碑文，四条臂膀指向的最近的城镇距离石柱十英里，最远的超过二十英里。从这些城镇众所周知的名字中，我了解到我所处的郡县；我看到的是一个中北部郡县，荒原昏暗，山峦起伏。我的身后和两侧都是广袤的荒原；我脚下的深谷远处山峦起伏。这里的人口一定很稀少，我在这些道路上看不到乘客；它们向东西南北延伸--白色、宽阔、孤独；它们都在荒原上开凿，石南花生长得很深很茂盛，一直延伸到它们的边缘。然而，一个偶然的旅行者可能会路过；我希望现在没有人看到我；陌生人会想知道我在做什么，在路标前徘徊，显然没有目标，迷失了方向。我可能会被盘问；我无法回答，只能说一些听起来不可思议、会引起怀疑的话。此时此刻，我与人类社会没有任何联系，没有任何魅力或希望召唤我去我的同类所在的地方，看到我的人都不会对我有善意的想法或美好的愿望。除了万物之母--大自然，我没有亲人；我要寻找她的胸怀，请求安息。

I struck straight into the heath ; I held on to a hollow I saw deeply furrowing the brown moorside ; I waded , knee - deep in its dark growth ; I turned with its turnings , and finding a moss - blackened granite crag in a hidden angle , I sat down under it . High banks of moor were about me ; the crag protected my head ; the sky was over that .

我径直走进荒野；我紧紧抓住一个凹陷，我看到它深深地犁开了褐色的荒野；我涉水而行，膝盖深陷在它的黑暗生长中；我随着它的转弯而转向，在一个隐蔽的角度找到了一块长满青苔的花岗岩峭壁，我在它下面坐了下来。高高的荒原围绕着我；峭壁保护着我的头部；天空就在那上面。

Some time passed before I felt tranquil even here ; I had a vague dread that wild cattle might be near , or that some sportsman or poacher might discover me . If a gust of wind swept the waste , I looked up , fearing it was the rush of a bull ; if a plover whistled , I imagined it a man . Finding my apprehensions unfounded , however , and calmed by the deep silence that reigned as evening declined to night - fall , I took confidence . As yet I had not thought ; I had only listened , watched , dreaded ; now I regained the faculty of reflection .

过了好一会儿，我才感觉到这里的宁静；我隐隐约约地担心野牛可能就在附近，或者一些运动员或偷猎者可能会发现我。如果有一阵风掠过荒原，我就会抬起头来，担心那是一头公牛在奔跑；如果有一只鸻鸣叫，我就会想象那是一个人。然而，我发现自己的担心是没有根据的，傍晚时分夜幕降临，四周一片寂静，这让我平静下来，于是我坚定了信心。到目前为止，我还没有思考过；我只是倾听、观察、害怕；现在我恢复了思考的能力。

What was I to do ? Where to go ? Oh , intolerable questions , when I could do nothing and go nowhere ! when a long way must yet be measured by my weary , trembling limbs , before I could reach human habitation — when cold charity must be entreated before I could get a lodging ; reluctant sympathy importuned ; almost certain repulse incurred , before my tale could be listened to , or one of my wants relieved !

我该怎么办？该去哪里？我什么也做不了，什么地方也去不了！我疲惫而颤抖的四肢还要丈量很长的路，才能到达人类的居住地--我必须恳求冷酷的施舍，才能找到住处；我必须恳求勉强的同情；我几乎肯定会遭到拒绝，我的故事才能被倾听，我的愿望才能得到满足！

I touched the heath ; it was dry , and yet warm with the heat of the summer day . I looked at the sky ; it was pure ; a kindly star twinkled just above the chasm ridge . The dew fell , but with propitious softness ; no breeze whispered . Nature seemed to me benign and good ; I thought she loved me , outcast as I was ; and I , who from man could anticipate only mistrust , rejection , insult , clung to her with filial fondness . To - night , at least , I would be her guest — as I was her child ; my mother would lodge me without money and without price . I had one morsel of bread yet, the remnant of a roll I had bought in a town we passed through at noon with a stray penny — my last coin . I saw ripe bilberries gleaming here and there , like jet beads in the heath ; I gathered a handful and eat them with the bread . My hunger , sharp before , was , if not satisfied , appeased by this hermit ’ s meal . I said my evening prayers at its conclusion , and then chose my couch .

我摸了摸荒草，它很干燥，却又带着夏日的热气。我看着天空，它是纯净的；一颗慈祥的星星就在山脊上方闪烁。露水淅淅沥沥地下着，但很柔和，没有微风拂过。在我看来，大自然是仁慈善良的；我想她是爱我的，尽管我是个弃儿；而我，这个从人类那里只能预料到不信任、拒绝和侮辱的人，却对她充满了孝心。至少今晚，我将是她的客人--就像我是她的孩子一样；我的母亲将免费为我提供住宿。我还有一丁点面包，那是中午我们路过一个小镇时，我用一个零钱--我最后一个硬币--买的面包卷的残片。我看到成熟的山桑子在这里和那里闪闪发光，就像石楠丛中的喷气珠子；我捡了一把，和面包一起吃了。这顿隐士的晚餐，即使不能满足我的饥饿感，也安抚了我。晚餐结束后，我做了晚祷，然后选择了我的沙发。

Beside the crag , the heath was very deep ; when I lay down my feet were buried in it ; rising high on each side , it left only a narrow space tor the night - air to invade . I folded my shawl double , and spread it over me for a coverlet ; a low , mossy swell was my pillow . Thus lodged , I was not , at least at the commencement of the night , cold .

峭壁旁的石楠林很深；当我躺下时，我的双脚被埋在里面；两边高高隆起，只留下狭窄的空间让夜风侵入。我把披肩叠成两层，铺在身上当被子；低矮的长满青苔的山丘就是我的枕头。就这样，至少在夜幕降临时，我没有感到寒冷。

My rest might have been blissful enough , only a sad heart broke it . It plained of its gaping wounds , its inward bleeding , its riven chords . It trembled for Mr . Rochester and his doom ; it bemoaned him with bitter pity ; it demanded him with ceaseless longing ; and , impotent as a bird with both wings broken , it still quivered its shattered pinions in vain attempts to seek him .

我的休息本来是很幸福的，只是一颗悲伤的心打破了这一切它诉说着它裂开的伤口，它内心的流血，它撕裂的心弦。它为罗切斯特先生和他的厄运而颤抖；它痛苦怜悯地哀叹他；它无休止地渴望他；它像折断双翼的鸟儿一样无能为力，却仍颤抖着破碎的羽翼，徒劳地试图寻找他。

Worn out with this torture of thought , I rose to my knees . Night was come , and her planets were risen ; a safe , still night ; too serene for the companionship of fear . We know that God is everywhere ; but certainly we feel His presence most when His works are on the grandest scale spread before us ; and it is in the unclouded night - sky , where His worlds wheel their silent course , that we read clearest His infinitude , His omnipotence , His omnipresence . I had risen to my knees to pray for Mr . Rochester . Looking up , I , with tear - dimmed eyes , saw the mighty milky - way . Remembering what it was — what countless systems there swept space like a soft trace of light — I felt the might and strength of God . Sure was I of His efficiency to save what He had made ; convinced I grew that neither earth should perish , nor one of the souls it treasured . I turned my prayer to thanksgiving ; the Source of Life was also the Saviour of spirits . Mr . Rochester was safe ; he was God ‘ s , and by God would he be guarded . I again nestled to the breast of the hill ; and ere long , in sleep , forgot sorrow .

我被这种思念折磨得筋疲力尽，跪了下来。夜幕降临了，她的行星也升起了；这是一个安全、寂静的夜晚；太安静了，没有恐惧的陪伴。我们知道，上帝无处不在；但当他的作为以最宏大的规模展现在我们面前时，我们当然最能感受到他的存在；正是在朦胧的夜空中，在他的世界静静转动的地方，我们才能最清晰地读出他的无限、他的全能、他的无所不在。我跪下来为罗切斯特先生祈祷。我抬起头，泪眼朦胧，看到了壮丽的银河。回想起那是什么--无数的系统像柔和的光痕一样掠过太空--我感受到了上帝的威力和力量。我确信，他有能力拯救他所创造的一切；我越来越相信，地球不会毁灭，它所珍视的灵魂也不会毁灭。我将祷告转为感恩；生命之源也是灵魂的救主。罗切斯特先生是安全的；他是上帝的，上帝会守护他。我再次依偎在小山的胸前，在睡夢中忘卻了憂傷。

But next day , want came to me , pale and bare . Long after the little birds had left their nests ; long after bees had come in the sweet prime of day to gather the heath honey before the dew was dried — when the long morning shadows were curtailed, and the sun filled earth and sky — I got up , and I looked round me .

但第二天，我又感到了匮乏，脸色苍白，赤身裸体。在小鸟离开它们的巢穴很久以后，在蜜蜂在甜美的白天来采集石楠蜂蜜很久以后，在露水干涸以前，在长长的晨影缩减，阳光洒满大地和天空以后，我站了起来，环顾四周。

What a still , hot , perfect day ! What a golden desert this spreading moor ! Everywhere sunshine . I wished I could live in it and on it . I saw a lizard run over the crag ; I saw a bee busy among the sweet bilberries . I would fain at the moment have become bee or lizard , that I might have found fitting nutriment , permanent shelter , here . But I was a human being , and had a human being ’ s wants ; I must not linger where there was nothing to supply them . I rose ; I looked back at the bed I had left . Hopeless of the future , I wished but this—that my Maker had that night thought good to require my soul of me while I slept ; 80 and that this weary frame , absolved by death from further conflict with fate , had now but to decay quietly , and mingle in peace with the soil of this wilderness . Life , however , was yet in my possession , with all its requirements , and pains , and responsibilities . The burden must be carried ; the want provided for ; the suffering endured ; the responsibility fulfilled . I set out .

多么静谧、炎热、完美的一天！一望无垠的荒原是多么金色的沙漠啊！到处都是阳光。我真希望能生活在这片荒原上。我看到一只蜥蜴在峭壁上奔跑；我看到一只蜜蜂在甜美的山桑子间忙碌。此时此刻，我真想变成蜜蜂或蜥蜴，在这里找到合适的营养和永久的栖息地。但我是一个人，有一个人的需求；我绝不能在没有任何东西可以满足我需求的地方逗留。我站了起来，回头看了看我离开的床。我对未来毫无希望，我只希望--我的造物主在我熟睡的那一夜认为需要我的灵魂是件好事；80 我这副疲惫不堪的身躯因死亡而不再与命运发生进一步的冲突，现在只需静静地腐烂，与这片荒野的土壤和平共处。然而，生命还在我的手中，还有它所有的要求、痛苦和责任。重担必须扛起，匮乏必须解决，苦难必须忍受，责任必须履行。我出发了。

Whitcross regained , I followed a road which led from the sun , now fervent and high . By no other circumstance had I will to decide my choice . I walked a long time , and when I thought I had nearly done enough , and might conscientiously yield to the fatigue that almost overpowered me — might relax this forced action , and , sitting down on a stone I saw near , submit resistlessly to the apathy that clogged heart and limb — I heard a bell chime — a church bell .

重新回到怀特克罗斯，我沿着一条路走，这条路通向太阳，现在太阳热情而高照。没有任何其他情况能让我决定我的选择。我走了很久，当我以为我已经走得差不多了，可以有意识地屈服于几乎压倒我的疲倦--可以放松这种被迫的行动，在我看到的附近的一块石头上坐下来，毫无抵抗地屈服于堵塞心脏和肢体的冷漠时，我听到了钟声--教堂的钟声。

I turned in the direction of the sound , and there , among the romantic hills , whose changes and aspect I had ceased to note an hour ago , I saw a hamlet and a spire . All the valley at my right hand was full of pasture - fields , and corn - fields , and wood ; and a glittering stream ran zig-zag through the varied shades of green , the mellowing grain , the sombre wood - land , the clear and sunny lea . Recalled by the rumbling of wheels to the road before me , I saw a heavily - laden wagon laboring up the hill ; and not far beyond were two cows and their drover . Human life and human labor were near . I must struggle on ; strive to live and bend to toil like the rest .

我顺着钟声的方向转过身去，在那浪漫的山丘之间，我看到了一个小村庄和一个尖塔。在我右手边的山谷里，到处都是牧场、玉米地和树林；一条闪闪发光的小溪蜿蜒流淌，穿过深浅不一的绿色、成熟的谷物、阴郁的林地和晴朗的草地。隆隆的车轮声让我想起了眼前的道路，我看到一辆满载货物的马车正在山上劳作；不远处是两头牛和它们的赶牛人。人类的生活和劳动近在咫尺。我必须继续奋斗，努力生活，像其他人一样弯腰劳作。

About two o ‘ clock P . M ., I entered the village . At the bottom of its one street , there was a little shop with some cakes of bread in the window . I coveted a cake of bread . With that refreshment I could perhaps regain a degree of energy ; without it , it would be difficult to proceed . The wish to have some strength and some vigor returned to me as soon as I was among my fellow - beings . I felt it would be degrading to faint with hunger on the causeway of a hamlet . Had I nothing about me I could offer in exchange for one of these rolls ? I considered . I had a small silk handkerchief tied round my throat ; I had my gloves . I could hardly tell how men and women in extremities of destitution proceeded . I did not know whether either of these articles would be accepted ; probably they would not , but I must try .

下午两点左右，我走进了村子。在一条街的尽头，有一家小店，橱窗里摆着一些面包蛋糕。我很想吃一块面包。有了它，我或许可以恢复一点体力；没有它，我将难以继续前行。一回到同伴中间，我就又萌生了想要一些力量和活力的愿望。我觉得，在一个小村庄的便道上饿晕过去是很没面子的。难道我身上没有任何东西可以用来交换这些面包卷吗？我想了想。我的喉咙上系着一条小丝帕，我带着手套。我几乎不知道处于极度贫困中的男人和女人是怎么做的。我不知道这两样东西是否会被接受；也许不会，但我必须试一试。

I entered the shop ; a woman was there . Seeing a respectably - dressed person , a lady as she supposed , she came forward with civility . How could she serve me ? I was seized with shame ; my tongue would not utter the request I had prepared . I dared not offer her the half - worn gloves , the creased handkerchief ; besides , I felt it would be absurd . I only begged permission to sit down a moment , as I was tired . Disappointed in the expectation of a customer , she coolly acceded to my request . She pointed to a seat ; I sunk into it . I felt sorely urged to weep ; but conscious how unseasonable such a manifestation would be , I restrained it . Soon I asked her “ if there were any dress - maker or plain - work womanha in the village ?”

我走进商店，一位妇女正在那里看到我衣着体面，应该是位女士，她便礼貌地走上前来。她怎么能为我服务呢？我羞愧难当，我的舌头说不出我准备好的请求。我不敢把半旧的手套和皱巴巴的手帕递给她，而且我觉得这样做太荒唐了。我只请求允许我坐一会儿，因为我累了。她对客人的期望落空了，于是冷淡地答应了我的请求。她指了指一个座位，我便坐了进去。我有一种想哭的冲动，但我意识到这种表现是多么不合时宜，所以我克制住了。不一会儿，我问她 "村里有没有做衣服的或做粗活的女人？"

I passed up the street , looking as I went at all the houses to the right hand and to the left ; but I could discover no pretext , nor see an inducement to enter any . I rambled round the hamlet , going sometimes to a little distance and returning again , for an hour or more . Much exhausted , and suffering greatly now for want of food , I turned aside into a lane , and sat down under the hedge . Ere many minutes had elapsed , I was again on my feet , however , and again searching something — a resource , or at least an informant . A pretty little house stood at the top of the lane , with a garden before it , exquisitely neat , and brilliantly blooming : I stopped at it . What business had I to approach the white door , or touch the glittering knocker ? In what way could it possibly be the interest of the inhabitants of that dwelling to serve me ? Yet I drew near and knocked . A mild - looking , cleanly attired young woman opened the door . In such a voice as might be expected from a hopeless heart and fainting frame — a voice wretchedly low and faltering — I asked if a servant was wanted here ?

我沿街而行，左顾右盼，却找不到任何借口，也看不出有什么诱人之处。我在村子里转来转去，有时走远一点，又返回来，转了一个多小时。我已经筋疲力尽，又因为缺乏食物而痛苦不堪，于是我拐进了一条小巷，在篱笆下坐了下来。然而，没过几分钟，我又站了起来，再次寻找着什么--一种资源，或者至少是一个线人。小巷的顶端矗立着一幢漂亮的小房子，房子前有一个花园，非常整洁，鲜花盛开：我在那里停了下来。我有什么资格靠近那扇白色的门，或者触摸那闪闪发光的门环？那座房子的居民怎么会愿意为我服务呢？但我还是走近去敲了敲门。一位面容温和、衣着整洁的年轻女士打开了门。我用绝望的心和虚弱的身躯所能发出的声音--低沉而颤抖的声音--问她这里是否需要仆人？

“ No ,” said she ; “ we do not keep a servant .”

"不，"她说，"我们不养仆人。

“ Can you tell me where I could get employment of any kind ,” I continued ; “ I am a stranger , without acquaintance , in this place . I want some work , no matter what .”

"你能告诉我在哪里可以找到工作吗？"我继续问道，"我是个陌生人，在这个地方没有熟人。无论如何，我都想找份工作。"

But it was not her business to think for me , or to seek a place for me : besides , in her eyes , how doubtful must have appeared my character , position , tale . She shook her head ; she “ was sorry she could give me no information ,” and the white door closed , quite gently and civilly ; but it shut me out . If she had held it open a little longer , I believe I should have begged a piece of bread ; for I was now brought low .

但这不是她该为我考虑的事，也不是她该为我找工作的事：况且，在她眼里，我的性格、地位和故事是多么可疑啊。她摇了摇头；她 "很抱歉不能给我提供任何信息"，白色的门轻轻地、礼貌地关上了，但却把我拒之门外。如果她再多开一会儿，我相信我就能乞讨到一块面包了；因为我现在的心情很低落。

I could not bear to return to the sordid village , where , besides , no prospect of aid was visible . I should have longed rather to deviate to a wood I saw not far off , which appeared , in its thick shade , to offer inviting shelter ; but I was so sick , so weak , so gnawed with nature ’ s cravings , instinct kept me roaming round abodes where there was a chance of food . Solitude would be no solitude — rest no rest — while the vulture hunger thus sunk beak and talons in my side .

我不忍心再回到那个肮脏的村庄，而且在那里也看不到援助的前景。我本想绕道去不远处的一片树林，那里浓密的树荫似乎能给我提供一个诱人的栖身之所；但我病得太重，太虚弱，被大自然的渴求折磨得体无完肤，本能让我在有可能得到食物的地方四处游荡。孤独不是孤独，休息也不是休息，而饥饿的秃鹫就这样把喙和爪子伸进了我的身体。

I drew near houses ; I left them , and came back again , and again I wandered away , always repelled by the consciousness of having no claim to ask — no right to expect interest in my isolated lot . Meantime , the afternoon advanced , while I thus wandered about like a lost and starving dog . In crossing a field , I saw the church - spire before me ; I hastened toward it . Near the church - yard , and in the middle of a garden , stood a well - built though small house , which I had no doubt was the parsonage . I remembered that strangers who arrive at a place where they have no friends , and who want employment , sometimes apply to the clergyman for introduction and aid . It is the clergyman ’ s function to help — at least with advice — those who wish to help themselves . I seemed to have something like a right to seek counsel here . Renewing , then , my courage , and gathering my feeble remains of strength , I pushed on . I reached the house , and knocked at the kitchen - door . An old woman opened ; I asked was this the parsonage ?

我走近房子，离开房子，又回来，又游荡开去，我总是意识到自己无权过问，无权期望别人对我孤立无援的命运感兴趣。就在我像一只迷路的饿狗一样四处游荡的时候，时间已经到了下午。在穿过一片田野时，我看到教堂的火光就在眼前；我急忙向教堂走去。在教堂院子附近的花园中央，矗立着一栋虽然不大，但建造精良的房子，我毫不怀疑这就是牧师住宅。我记得，陌生人到了一个没有朋友的地方，如果想找工作，有时会向牧师申请介绍和帮助。牧师的职责就是帮助那些希望自助的人，至少是提供建议。我似乎有权在这里寻求建议。于是，我重新鼓起勇气，鼓起仅存的微弱力量，继续向前走去。我到了那所房子，敲了敲厨房的门。一位老妇人打开了门；我问这是牧师住宅吗？

“ Yes .”

"是的"

“ Was the clergyman in ?”

"牧师在吗？"

“ No .”

不在

“ Would he be in soon ?”

"他很快会进来吗？"

“ No , he was gone from home .”

"不，他离家出走了"

"很远吗？"

“ Not so far — happen three mile . He had been called away by the sudden death of his father ; he was at Marsh End now , and would very likely stay there a fortnight longer .”

"不是很远，大概三英里吧他父亲突然去世了，他被叫走了，他现在在沼泽庄园，很可能会在那里多呆两个星期。"

Once more I took off my handkerchief — once more I thought of the cakes of bread in the little shop . Oh , but for a crust ! for but one mouthful to allay the pang of famine ! Instinctively I turned my face again to the village ; I found the shop again , and I went in ; and though others were there besides the woman , I ventured the request , “ Would she give me a roll for this handkerchief ?”

我再一次摘下手帕，再一次想起小店里的面包蛋糕。哦，但愿能有一块面包皮！但愿能有一口面包来减轻饥荒的痛苦！我本能地又把脸转向村子；我又找到了那家小店，我走了进去；虽然除了那个女人，还有其他人在那里，但我还是大胆地请求道："她能给我一卷手帕吗？

She looked at me with evident suspicion ; “ Nay , she never sold stuff i ’ that way .”

她明显带着怀疑的目光看着我："不，她从不那样卖东西。"

Almost desperate , I asked for half a cake ; she again refused . “ How could she tell where I had got the handkerchief ?” she said .

我几乎绝望了，要了半块蛋糕，她又拒绝了。"她说："她怎么知道我的手帕是从哪里来的？

“ Would she take my gloves ?”

Reader , it is not pleasant to dwell on these details . Some say there is enjoyment in looking back to painful experience past ; but at this day I can scarcely bear to review the times to which I allude ; the moral degradation , blent with the physical suffering , form too distressing a recollection ever to be willingly dwelt on . I blamed none of those who repulsed me . I felt it was what was to be expected , and what could not be helped ; an ordinary beggar is frequently an object of suspicion ; a well - dressed beggar inevitably so . To be sure , what I begged was employment ; but whose business was it to provide me with employment ? Not , certainly , that of persons who saw me then for the first time , and who knew nothing about my character . And as to the woman who would not take my handkerchief in exchange for her bread , why , she was right ; if the offer appeared to her sinister , or the exchange unprofitable . Let me condense now . I am sick of the subject .

读者，纠缠于这些细节并不令人愉快。有人说，回顾过去的痛苦经历是一种享受；但时至今日，我几乎不忍回顾我所提到的那段时光；道德的沦丧，加上肉体的痛苦，构成了令人痛心的回忆，让人永远不愿再提起。我没有责怪那些排斥我的人。我觉得这是意料之中的事，也是没有办法的事；一个普通的乞丐经常会被人怀疑，而一个衣冠楚楚的乞丐则不可避免。诚然，我乞讨的是一份工作；但为我提供工作是谁的事？当然不是那些第一次见到我、对我的性格一无所知的人。至于那个不愿意拿我的手绢换她的面包的女人，她是对的，如果她觉得我的提议是阴险的，或者交换无利可图的话。现在让我简短地说几句。我已经厌倦了这个话题。

A little before dark I passed a farmhouse , at the open door of which the farmer was sitting , eating his supper of bread and cheese ; I stopped and said :

天黑前，我路过一户农家，农夫正坐在敞开的门前，吃着面包和奶酪：

“ Will you give me a piece of bread ? for I am very hungry .” He cast on me a glance of surprise ; but without answering , he cut a thick slice from his loaf , and gave it to me . I imagine he did not think I was a beggar , but only an eccentric sort of lady who had taken a fancy to his brown loaf . As soon as I was out of sight of his house , I sat down and ate it .

"能给我一块面包吗，我很饿"他惊讶地看了我一眼，但没有回答，而是从他的面包上切下一块厚厚的面包给了我。我想，他并没有把我当成乞丐，而只是一个古怪的女士，看上了他的棕色面包。一离开他家的视线，我就坐下来吃了起来。

I could not hope to get a lodging under a roof , and sought it in the wood I have before alluded to . But my night was wretched , my rest broken ; the ground was damp , the air cold ; besides , intruders passed near me more than once , and I had again and again to change my quarters ; no sense of safety or tranquillity befriended me . Toward morning it rained ; the whole of the following day was wet . Do not ask me , reader , to give a minute account of that day ; as before , I sought work ; as before , I was repulsed ; as before , I starved ; but once did food pass my lips . At the door of a cottage I saw a little girl about to throw a mess of cold porridge into a pig - trough . “ Will you give me that ?” I asked .

我无法指望在屋檐下找到住处，只好到前面提到的树林里去找。但是，我的夜晚过得很糟糕，我的休息也中断了；地面潮湿，空气寒冷；此外，闯入者不止一次地从我附近经过，我不得不一次又一次地更换住处；我没有任何安全感或宁静感。天快亮的时候下起了雨，第二天一整天都是湿漉漉的。读者，不要要求我详细叙述那天的情况；和以前一样，我找工作；和以前一样，我被拒绝；和以前一样，我挨饿；但有一次，我的嘴边流过食物。在一间茅屋门口，我看到一个小女孩正要把一锅冷粥扔进猪槽。"你能把那个给我吗？我问道。

She stared at me . “ Mother !” she exclaimed ; “ there is a woman wants me to give her these porridge .”

她盯着我。"妈妈！"她喊道，"有个女人要我把这些粥给她。"

“ Well , lass ,” replied a voice within , “ give it her if she ’ s a beggar . T ’ pig doesn ’ t want it .”

"好吧，小姑娘，"里面一个声音回答道 "如果她是个乞丐，就给她吧。猪不会要的。"

The girl emptied the stiffened mould into my hand , and I devoured it ravenously .

女孩把僵硬的模具倒进我的手里，我狼吞虎咽地吃了起来。

As the wet twilight deepened , I stopped in a solitary bridle - path , which I had been pursuing an hour or more .

随着湿润的暮色加深，我在一条孤独的马道上停了下来，我已经走了一个多小时了。

“ My strength is quite failing me ,” I said in soliloquy . “ I feel I cannot go much further . Shall I be an outcast again this night ? While the rain descends so , must I lay my head on the cold , drenched ground ? I fear I cannot do otherwise ; for who will receive me ? But it will be very dreadful ; with this feeling of hunger , faintness , chill , and this sense of desolation — this total prostration of hope . In all likelihood , though , I should die before morning . And why cannot I reconcile myself to the prospect of death ? Why do I struggle to retain a valueless life ? Because I know , or believe , Mr . Rochester is still living ; and then , to die of want and cold , is a fate to which nature cannot submit passively . Oh , Providence ! sustain me a little longer ! Aid — direct me !”

"我体力不支了，"我独白道。"我觉得我走不动了。难道今晚我又要被遗弃吗？雨下得这么大，我还得躺在冰冷湿透的地上吗？恐怕我不能不这样做，因为谁会接纳我呢？但这将是非常可怕的；饥饿感、昏厥感、寒冷感、荒凉感--希望的彻底破灭。我很可能在天亮之前就会死去。为什么我不能接受死亡的前景？为什么我还要挣扎着维持毫无价值的生命？因為我知道或相信羅徹斯特先生還活著，而死於饑餓和寒冷，是天性無法被動接受的命運。哦，天意！再支持我一會兒！帮帮我，指引我！"

My glazed eye wandered over the dim and misty landscape . I saw I had strayed far from the village ; it was quite out of sight . The very cultivation surrounding it had disappeared . I had , by cross - ways and by - paths , once more drawn near the tract of moorland ; and now , only a few fields , almost as wild and unproductive as the heath from which they were scarcely reclaimed , lay between me and the dusky hill .

我炯炯有神的目光在昏暗迷蒙的景色中游移。我发现自己已经远离了村庄；村庄已经完全消失在我的视线中。村子周围的耕地也消失了。我穿过小路和小径，又一次靠近了那片荒原；现在，我和那座昏暗的小山之间只隔着几块田地，它们几乎和荒芜的荒原一样，没有任何生机。

“ Well ; I would rather die yonder than in a street or on a frequented road ,” I reflected . “ And far better that crows and ravens — if any ravens there be in these regions — should pick my flesh from my bones , than that they should be imprisoned in a workhouse coffin , and moulder in a pauper ’ s grave .”

"我想，"好吧，我宁愿死在那边，也不愿死在大街上或人来人往的路上"乌鸦和乌鸦--如果这些地方有乌鸦的话--从我的骨头里叼走我的肉，总比它们被囚禁在救济院的棺材里，在贫民的坟墓里发霉要好得多。"

To the hill , then , I turned . I reached it . It remained now only to find a hollow where I could lie down , and feel at least hidden , if not secure ; but all the surface of the waste looked level . It showed no variation but of tint ; green , where rush and moss overgrew the marshes ; black , where the dry soil bore only heath . Dark as it was getting , I could still see these changes , though but as mere alternations of light and shade , for color had faded with the daylight .

然后，我转身向山上走去。我到了。现在我只需要找到一个可以躺下的山洞，即使不安全，至少也能感觉到隐蔽。除了颜色之外，没有其他变化；绿色的地方是沼泽上长满了蒹葭和苔藓；黑色的地方是干燥的土壤上只有石楠。虽然天色渐暗，但我仍能看到这些变化，虽然只是明暗的交替，因为颜色已经随着日光褪去。

My eye still roved over the sullen swell , and along the moor - edge , vanishing amid the wildest scenery , when , at one dim point , far in among the marshes and the ridges , a light sprung up . “ That is an ignis fatuus ,” was my first thought ; and I expected it would soon vanish . It burned on , however , quite steadily , neither receding nor advancing . “ Is it , then , a bonfire just kindled ?” I questioned . I watched to see whether it would spread ; but no ; as it did not diminish , so it did not enlarge . “ It may be a candle in a house ,” I then conjectured ; “ but if so , I can never reach it . It is much too far away ; and were it within a yard of me , what would it avail ? I should but knock at the door to have it shut in my face .”

我的视线还在沉闷的波涛上游荡，沿着荒原的边缘，消失在最荒凉的景色中，这时，在远处沼泽和山脊之间的一个昏暗点上，出现了一束光。我的第一个念头是 "那是个火星"，我以为它很快就会消失。然而，它持续燃烧着，既没有后退，也没有前进。"那它是刚刚燃起的篝火吗？我问道。我观察着它是否会蔓延开来，但是没有；它没有减弱，也没有扩大。"可能是房子里的蜡烛，"我猜测道，"但如果是这样，我永远也够不着。它离我太远了；即使它就在离我一码之内，又有什么用呢？我只需敲敲门，它就会当着我的面关上。

And I sunk down where I stood , and hid my face against the ground . I lay still a while ; the night - wind swept over the hill and over me , and died moaning in the distance ; the rain fell fast , wetting me afresh to the skin . Could I but have stiffened to the still frost — the friendly numbness of death — it might have pelted on ; I should not have felt it ; but my yet living flesh shuddered to its chilling influence . I rose ere long .

我站在原地沉了下去，把脸贴在地上。我静静地躺了一会儿；夜风掠过山头，掠过我的头顶，在远处呻吟着消失了；雨下得很快，把我的皮肤都打湿了。如果我不是因为静止的冰霜--死亡的亲切麻木--而变得僵硬，雨可能会继续下；我本不应该感觉到它，但我还活着的肉体却因为它的寒冷影响而颤抖。我很快就站了起来。

The light was yet there , shining dim , but constant , through the rain . I tried to walk again ; I dragged my exhausted limbs slowly toward it . It led me aslant over the hill , through a wide bog , which would have been impassable in winter , and was splashy and shaking even now , in the height of summer . Here I fell twice ; but as often I rose and rallied my faculties . This light was my forlorn hope ; I must gain it .

光线还在，虽然昏暗，但一直在雨中闪烁。我试着重新走路，拖着疲惫不堪的四肢慢慢地向它走去。它带我斜斜地越过山丘，穿过一片宽阔的沼泽，这片沼泽在冬天是无法通行的，即使在盛夏的现在也是水花四溅，摇摇欲坠。在这里，我摔倒过两次；但我也经常站起来，重新振作起来。这束光是我渺茫的希望，我必须得到它。

Having crossed the marsh , I saw a trace of white over the moor . I approached it ; it was a road or a track ; it led straight up to the light , which now beamed from a sort of knoll , amid a clump of trees — firs , apparently , from what I could distinguish of the character of their forms and foliage through the gloom . My star vanished as I drew near ; some obstacle had intervened between me and it . I put out my hand to feel the dark mass before me ; I discriminated the rough stones of a low wall — above it something like palisades , and within , a high and prickly hedge . I groped on . Again a whitish object gleamed before me ; it was a gate — a wicket ; it moved on its hinges as I touched it . On each side stood a sable bush — holly or yew .

穿过沼泽地，我看到荒原上有一抹白色。我走近一看，那是一条路或一条小径，它直通那束光，现在光从一个山丘上射出，照在一丛树中间--显然是枞树，从我透过阴暗的光线可以分辨出它们的形状和叶子的特征。当我走近时，我的星星消失了；在我和它之间有一些障碍物。我伸出手摸了摸眼前的黑乎乎的一片；我分辨出了一堵矮墙的粗糙石头--上面是类似栅栏的东西，里面则是高高的、长满刺的树篱。我摸索着前进。我的眼前再次闪现出一个白色的东西；那是一扇门--一个门闩；当我触摸它时，它的铰链动了一下。两边各站着一棵黑貂灌木--冬青或紫杉。

Entering the gate and passing the shrubs , the silhouette of a house rose to view , black , low , and rather long ; but the guiding light shone nowhere . All was obscurity . Were the inmates retired to rest ? I feared it must be so . In seeking the door , I turned an angle ; there shot out the friendly gleam again , from the lozenged panes of a very small latticed window , within a foot of the ground , made still smaller by the growth of ivy or some other creeping plant , whose leaves clustered thick over the portion of the house wall in which it was set . The aperture was so screened and narrow , that curtain or shutter had been deemed unnecessary ; and when I stooped down and put aside the spray of foliage shooting over it , I could see all within . I could see clearly a room with a sanded floor , clean scoured ; a dresser of walnut , with pewter plates ranged in rows , reflecting the redness and radiance of a glowing peat - fire . "也许喝点水能让她恢复。汉娜，去打点水来。但她已经瘦得不成样子了。多么消瘦，多么没有血色！"

“ A mere spectre !”

"只是个幽灵！"

“ Is she ill , or only famished ?”

"她是病了，还是饿坏了？"

“ Famished , I think . Hannah , is that milk ? Give it me , and a piece of bread .”

"我想是饿坏了汉娜，那是牛奶吗？给我，再给我一块面包。"

Diana ( I knew her by the long curls which I saw drooping between me and the fire as she bent over me ) broke some bread , dipped it in milk , and put it to my lips . Her face was near mine ; I saw there was pity in it , and I felt sympathy in her hurried breathing . In her simple words , too , the same balm - like emotion spoke : “ Try to eat .”

黛安娜（我认识她，因为我看到她的长卷发垂在我和炉火之间，她弯下腰来）掰了一些面包，蘸上牛奶，放到我的嘴边。她的脸贴近我的脸，我看到她的脸上有怜悯，我从她急促的呼吸中感受到了同情。在她简单的话语中，也流露着同样如万金油般的情感："试着吃点东西"

“ Yes — try ,” repeated Mary , gently ; and Mary ’ s hand removed my sodden bonnet and lifted my head . I tasted what they offered me ; feebly , at first — eagerly , soon .

"是的，试一试，"玛丽温柔地重复道；玛丽的手拿开了我湿透的帽子，抬起了我的头。我尝了尝他们给我的东西，起初很微弱，但很快就热切起来。

“ Not too much , at first ; restrain her ,” said the brother ; “ she has had enough .” And he withdrew the cup of milk and the plate of bread .

"哥哥说："一开始不要喝太多，让她休息一下，她已经喝得够多了。他收回了牛奶杯和面包盘。

“ A little more , St . John ; look at the avidity in her eyes .”

"再多一点，圣约翰，看她那渴求的眼神"

“ No more at present , sister . Try if she can speak now — ask her her name .”

"现在不行了，妹妹。试试她现在能不能说话，问她叫什么名字。"

I felt I could speak , and I answered : “ My name is Jane Elliott .” Anxious as ever to avoid discovery , I had before resolved to assume an alias .

我觉得我可以说话了 我回答道"我叫简-艾略特"我一如既往地想避免被人发现 我之前已经决定用化名了

“ And where do you live ? Where are your friends ?”

"你住在哪里？你的朋友呢？"

I was silent .

我沉默不语。

“ Can we send for any one you know ?”

"我们可以找你认识的人吗？"

I shook my head .

我摇了摇头。

“ What account can you give of yourself?”

"你能为自己作何解释？"

Somehow, now that I had once crossed the threshold of this house , and once was brought face to face with its owners , I felt no longer outcast , vagrant , and disowned by the wide world . I dared to put off the mendicant — to resume my natural manner and character . I began once more to know myself ; and when Mr . St . John demanded an account — which , at present , I was far too weak to render — I said , after a brief pause :

不知怎的，既然我曾经跨进过这所房子的门槛，曾经与它的主人面对面，我就不再觉得自己是被遗弃的，流浪的，不被这个世界所承认的。我敢于卸下伪装，恢复自然的举止和性格。我又一次开始了解自己；当圣约翰先生要求我说明情况时，我在短暂的停顿之后说："先生，我不能给你任何解释：

“ Sir , I can give you no details to - night .”

"先生，今晚我无法向您提供任何细节"

“ But what , then ,” said he , “ do you expect me to do for you ?”

"那你希望我为你做什么呢？"

“ Nothing ,” I replied . My strength sufficed for but short answers . Diana took the word :

"没什么"，我回答道。我的力气只够简短地回答。戴安娜接过话头：

“ Do you mean ,” she asked , “ that we have now given you what aid you require ; and that we may dismiss you to the moor and the rainy night ?”

"你的意思是，"她问，"我们现在已经给了你所需要的帮助；我们可以把你遣送到荒野和雨夜了吗？"

I looked at her . She had , I thought , a remarkable countenance , instinct both with power and goodness . I took sudden courage . Answering her compassionate gaze with a smile , I said , “ I will trust you . If I were a masterless and stray dog , I know that you would not turn me from your hearth to - night ; as it is , I really have no fear . 81 Do with me and for me as you like , but excuse me from much discourse ; my breath is short — I feel a spasm when I speak .” All three surveyed me , and all three were silent .

我看着她。我想，她的面容非同一般，本能地散发着力量和善良。我突然鼓起勇气。我微笑着回应她怜悯的目光，说："我相信你。如果我是一条无主的流浪狗，我知道您今晚不会把我从您的炉台上赶走；现在，我真的无所畏惧。81 你们想怎么对我，怎么为我，就怎么对我，但请原谅我不能多说，我呼吸急促，一说话就感到痉挛。三个人都打量着我，都沉默不语。

“ Hannah ,” said Mr . St . John , at last , “ let her sit there at present , and ask her no questions ; in ten minutes more , give her the remainder of that milk and bread . Mary and Diana , let us go into the parlor and talk the matter over .”

"汉娜，"圣约翰先生最后说，"现在就让她坐在那里，不要问她任何问题；再过十分钟，把剩下的牛奶和面包给她吃。玛丽和黛安娜，让我们到客厅里好好谈谈这件事。"

They withdrew . Very soon one of the ladies returned — I could not tell which . A kind of pleasant stupor was stealing over me as I sat by the genial fire . In an under tone , she gave some directions to Hannah . Ere long , with the servant ’ s aid , I contrived to mount a stair - case — my dripping clothes were removed ; soon a warm , dry bed received me . I thanked God ; experienced amid unutterable exhaustion a glow of grateful joy — and slept .

她们退了出去。很快，其中一位女士回来了，我不知道是哪一位。当我坐在和煦的炉火旁时，一种惬意的昏昏欲睡的感觉笼罩着我。她用轻描淡写的语气对汉娜吩咐了几句。不久，在仆人的搀扶下，我爬上了楼梯--我的滴水的衣服被脱掉了；不久，一张温暖、干燥的床接纳了我。我感谢上帝，在难言的疲惫中体验到了感恩的喜悦，然后就睡了。
