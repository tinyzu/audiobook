﻿ Chapter IV

第四章

From my discourse with Mr . Lloyd , and from the above - reported conference between Bessie and Abbot , I gathered enough of hope to suffice as a motive for wishing to get well : a change seemed near — I desired and waited it in silence . It tarried , however ; days and weeks passed ; I had regained my normal state of health , but no new allusion was made to the subject over which I brooded . Mrs . Reed surveyed me at times with a severe eye , but seldom addressed me . Since my illness she had drawn a more marked line of separation than ever between me and her own children ; appointing me a small closetr to sleep in by myself , condemning me to take my meals alone , and pass all my time in the nursery , while my cousins were constantly in the drawing - room . Not a hint , however , did she drop about sending me to school ; still I felt an instinctive certainty that she would not long endure me under the same roof with her ; for her glance , now more than ever , when turned on me , expressed an insuperable and rooted aversion .

从我和劳埃德先生的谈话中，以及从上文提到的贝西和方丈的会谈中，我获得了足够的希望，足以成为我希望康复的动机：改变似乎近在眼前，我渴望并默默地等待着。然而，变化迟迟没有到来；几天又几周过去了；我已经恢复了正常的健康状况，但我一直耿耿于怀的话题却再也没有提起过。里德太太有时用严厉的目光打量着我，但很少对我说话。自从我生病以来，她在我和她自己的孩子之间划出了一条比以往任何时候都更明显的界线；她给我指定了一个小壁橱，让我一个人睡，让我一个人吃饭，让我把所有的时间都花在育儿室里，而我的堂兄弟姐妹们却经常在客厅里。不过，她并没有暗示要送我去上学；但我还是本能地确信，她不会长期忍受我和她同在一个屋檐下；因为她现在看我的眼神，比以往任何时候都更多地流露出一种难以克服的、根深蒂固的厌恶。

Eliza and Georgiana , evidently acting according to orders , spoke to me as little as possible . John thrust his tongue in his cheek whenever he saw me , and once attempted chastisement ; but as I instantly turned against him , roused by the same sentiment of deep ire and desperate revolt which had stirred my corruption s before , he thought it better to desist , and ran from me uttering execrations and vowing I had burst his nose . I had indeed levelled at that prominent feature as hard a blow as my knuckles could inflict ; and when I saw that either that or my look daunted him , I had the greatest inclination to follow up my advantageto purpose ; but he was already with his mamma . I heard him in a blubbering tone commence the tale of how “ that nasty Jane Eyre ” had flown at him like a mad cat —: he was stopped rather harshly —

伊丽莎和乔治亚娜显然是奉命行事，尽量少跟我说话。约翰一见到我就把舌头伸到腮帮子里，有一次还试图责备我；但由于我立刻转过身来反对他，被以前激起我的堕落的那种深深的愤怒和绝望的反抗情绪所激起，他觉得还是算了吧，于是从我身边跑开，口中骂骂咧咧，发誓说我打爆了他的鼻子。我确实用指关节狠狠地打了他的鼻子一下；当我看到他被我的指关节或我的表情吓倒时，我真想乘胜追击，但他已经和他妈妈在一起了。我听到他用泣不成声的语调开始讲述 "那个讨厌的简-爱 "是如何像只疯猫一样扑向他的--：他被严厉地制止了-

“ Don ’ t talk to me about her , John ; I told you not to go near her ; she is not worthy of notice ; I do not choose that either you or your sisters should associate with her .”

"别跟我谈她，约翰；我告诉过你不要靠近她；她不值得注意；我不希望你或你的姐妹们和她交往。"

Here , leaning over the banister , I cried out suddenly and without at all deliberating on my words ,

到这里，我靠在栏杆上，突然大叫起来，完全没有考虑我的措辞，

“ They are not fit to associate with me .”

"她们不适合和我交往"。

Mrs . Reed was rather a stout woman , but , on hearing this strange and audacious declaration , she ran nimbly up the stair , swept me like a whirlwind into the nursery , and crushing me down on the edge of my crib , t dared me in an emphatic voice to rise from that place or utter one syllable during the remainder of the day .

里德太太是个相当粗壮的女人，但听到这奇怪而大胆的声明后，她敏捷地跑上楼梯，像一阵旋风一样把我卷进了育儿室，把我按倒在婴儿床的边上，并用一种强调的声音让我在今天剩余的时间里不敢从那个地方站起来，也不敢发出一个音节。

“ What would Uncle Reed say to you if he were alive ?” was my scarcely voluntary demand . I say scarcely voluntary , for it seemed as if my tongue pronounced words without my will consenting to their utterance ; something spoke out of me over which I had no control .

"如果里德舅舅还活着，他会对你说什么？"我几乎是自愿提出这个要求的。我说 "几乎是自愿的"，是因为我的舌头似乎在不经我的意志同意的情况下说出了这些话；有什么东西从我口中说出，而我却无法控制。

“ What ?” said Mrs . Reed , under her breath . Her usually cold , composed gray eye became troubled with a look like fear ; she took her hand from my arm and gazed at me as if she really did not know whether I were child or fiend . I was now in for it .

"什么？"里德太太小声说。她那双一向冷漠、沉着的灰色眼睛变得不安起来，带着一种类似恐惧的神情；她把手从我的胳膊上拿开，凝视着我，好像她真的不知道我是孩子还是魔鬼。我现在是在劫难逃了。

“My Uncle Reed is in heaven , and can see all you do and think ; and so can papa and mamma ; they know how you shut me up all day long , and how you wish me dead .”

"我的里德舅舅在天堂，他能看到你的所做所想；爸爸和妈妈也能看到；他们知道你是怎么整天把我关在家里的，也知道你是怎么希望我死的。

Mrs . Reed soon rallied her spirits : she shook me most soundly ; she boxedu both my ears , and then left me without a word . Bessie supplied the hiatus by a homily of an hour ’ s length , in which she proved beyond a doubt that I was the most wicked and abandoned child ever reared under a roof . I half believed her ; for I felt indeed only bad feelings surging in my breast .

里德太太很快就恢复了精神：她用力地摇了摇我；她用拳头敲了敲我的两只耳朵，然后一声不吭地离开了我。贝茜用长达一个小时的说教弥补了这一间隙，她在说教中毫无疑问地证明，我是有史以来在屋檐下长大的最邪恶、最被遗弃的孩子。我对她的话半信半疑，因为我只觉得胸中涌动着坏情绪。

November , December , and half of January passed away . Christmas and the New Year had been celebrated at Gateshead with the usual festive cheer ; presents had been interchanged , dinners and evening parties given . From every enjoyment I was , of course , excluded : my share of the gayety consisted in witnessing the daily apparelling of Eliza and Georgiana , and seeing them descend to the drawing - room , dressed out in thin muslin frocks and scarlet sashes , with hair elaborately ringleted ; and afterward , in listening to the sound of the piano or the harp played below , to the passing to and fro of the butler and footmen , to the jingling of glass and china as refreshments were handed , to the broken hum of conversation as the drawing - room doors opened and closed . When tired of this occupation , I would retire from the stair - head to the solitary and silent nursery : there , though somewhat sad , I was not miserable . To speak truth , I had not the least wish to go into company , for in company I was very rarely noticed ; and if Bessie had but been kind and companionable , I should have deemed it a treat to spend the evenings quietly with her , instead of passing them under the formidable eye of Mrs . Reed , in a room full of ladies and gentlemen . But Bessie , as soon as she had dressed her young ladies , used to take herself off to the lively regions of the kitchen and housekeeper ’ s room , generally bearing the candle along with her . I then sat with my doll on my knee , till the fire got low , glancing round occasionally to make sure that nothing worse than myself haunted the shadowy room ; and when the embers sunk to a dull red , I undressed hastily , tugging at knots and strings as I best might , and sought shelter from cold and darkness in my crib . To this crib I always took my doll . Human beings must love something , and , in the dearth of worthier objects of affection , I contrived to find a pleasure in loving and cherishing a faded graven image , shabby as a miniature scare - crow . It puzzles me now to remember with what absurd sincerity I doted on this wooden toy , half fancying it alive and capable of sensation . I could not sleep unless it was folded in my night - gown ; and when it lay there safe and warm , I was comparatively happy , believing it happy likewise .

十一月、十二月和一月的一半时间过去了。圣诞节和新年在盖茨黑德过得像往常一样热闹；大家互赠礼物，共进晚餐，举行晚会。当然，我被排除在所有的欢乐活动之外：我的乐趣在于目睹伊丽莎和乔治亚娜每天穿戴整齐，看着她们穿着薄薄的薄纱连衣裙，系着猩红色的腰带，梳着精致的发圈，来到客厅；之后，听着楼下弹奏的钢琴声或竖琴声，听着管家和男仆们来来往往的脚步声，听着递送茶点时玻璃和瓷器发出的叮当声，听着客厅门开关时嘈杂的交谈声。当我厌倦了这种生活，我就会从楼梯口退到孤独寂静的育儿室：在那里，我虽然有些悲伤，但并不痛苦。说实话，我一点也不想与人作伴，因为在与人作伴时，我很少被人注意；如果贝茜是个和蔼可亲的伴侣，我就会认为能与她安静地度过夜晚是一种享受，而不是在里德太太可怕的目光下，在满是绅士淑女的房间里度过。但是贝茜一给年轻的女士们穿好衣服，就会带着蜡烛去厨房和女管家的房间里热闹去了。然后，我抱着洋娃娃坐在膝盖上，直到火势变小，不时瞥一眼四周，以确保没有比我更可怕的东西在阴暗的房间里出没；当火苗沉到暗红色时，我就匆忙脱掉衣服，尽力拽着绳结和绳子，到我的摇篮里躲避寒冷和黑暗。在这张小床上，我总是带着我的娃娃。人总得爱点什么，在缺乏更值得爱的对象的情况下，我想方设法在爱和珍惜一个褪色的雕像中找到了乐趣，它就像一个微型稻草人一样寒酸。现在回想起来，我对这个木制玩具的宠爱是多么荒唐而真诚，我半信半疑地以为它是活的，是有感觉的。除非把它叠在我的睡衣里，否则我无法入睡；当它安全而温暖地躺在那里时，我就会比较开心，相信它也同样开心。

Long did the hours seem while I waited the departure of the company , and listened for the sound of Bessie ’ s step on the stairs . Sometimes she would come up in the interval to seek her thimble or her scissors , or perhaps to bring me something by way of supper — a bun or a cheese - cake ; then she would sit on the bed while I ate it , and when I had finished she would tuck the clothes round me ; and twice she kissed me , and said , “ Good night , Miss Jane .” When thus gentle , Bessie seemed to me the best , prettiest , kindest being in the world ; and I wished most intensely that she would always be so pleasant and amiable , and never push me about , or scold , or task me unreasonably , as she was too often wont to do . Bessie Lee must , I think , have been a girl of good natural capacity ; for she was smart in all she did , and had a remarkable knack of narrative : so , at least , I judge from the impression made on me by her nursery tales . She was pretty , too , if my recollections of her face and person are correct . I remember her as a slim young woman , with black hair , dark eyes , very nice features , and good , clear complexion ; but she had a capricious and hasty temper , and indifferent ideas of principle or justice : still , such as she was , I preferred her to any one else at Gateshead Hall .

当我等待着同伴们离开，聆听着贝茜踏上楼梯的脚步声时，时间显得格外漫长。有时，她会在间隙上来找她的顶针或剪刀，或者给我带点晚饭--小面包或奶酪蛋糕；然后，我吃晚饭时，她会坐在床上，等我吃完后，她会给我掖好衣服；她还会亲我两次，说 "晚安，简小姐"。贝茜这样温柔的时候，在我看来是世界上最美好、最漂亮、最善良的人，我非常希望她永远这样和蔼可亲，永远不要像她经常做的那样，对我推来推去，或者责骂我，或者无理地责备我。我想，贝茜-李一定是个天资聪颖的女孩，因为她做什么事都很聪明，而且很会叙事：至少，我是从她的童话故事给我留下的印象中判断出来的。她也很漂亮，如果我对她容貌和性格的记忆是正确的话。在我的印象中，她是个苗条的年轻女子，黑头发，黑眼睛，五官端正，肤色清秀；但她的脾气任性而急躁，对原则和正义的看法漠不关心：尽管如此，比起盖茨黑德庄园的其他人，我还是更喜欢她。

It was the fifteenth of January , about nine o‘ clock in the morning : Bessie was gone down to breakfast ; my cousins had not yet been summoned to their mamma ; Eliza was putting on her bonnet and warm garden - coat to go and feed her poultry , an occupation of which she was fond ; and not less so of selling the eggs to the housekeeper and hoarding up the money she thus obtained . She had a turn for traffic , v and a marked propensity for saving , shown not only in the vending of eggs and chickens , but also in driving hard bargains with the gardener about flower - roots , seeds , and slips of plants ; that functionary having orders from Mrs . Reed to buy of his young lady all the products of her parterrew she wished to sell ; and Eliza would have sold the hair off her head if she could have made a handsome profit thereby . As to her money , she first secreted it in odd corners , wrapped in a rag or an old curl - paper ; but , some of these hoards having been discovered by the housemaid , Eliza , fearful of one day losing her valued treasure , consented to intrust it to her mother , at a usurious rate of interest — fifty or sixty per cent — which interest she exacted every quarter , keeping her accounts in a little book with anxious accuracy .

那是一月十五日，大约上午九点钟：贝茜去吃早饭了；我的表姐妹们还没有被叫去见她们的妈妈；伊丽扎正戴上她的帽子，穿上暖和的花园大衣，去喂她的家禽，这是她最喜欢做的事情；她还喜欢把鸡蛋卖给女管家，然后囤积起来。她不仅喜欢贩卖鸡蛋和鸡，还喜欢跟园丁讨价还价，讨价还价的对象是花根、种子和植物碎片；里德太太命令园丁向她买下她想卖的所有园艺产品；如果能从中获得丰厚的利润，伊丽莎连自己的头发都愿意卖掉。至于她的钱，她先是把钱藏在零星的角落里，用破布或旧卷纸包着；但是，这些囤积的钱有一些被女佣发现了，伊丽莎害怕有一天会失去她宝贵的财富，就同意把钱托付给她的母亲，以高利贷的方式收取利息--百分之五十或六十--她每个季度都收取利息，并在一个小本子上精确地记账。

Georgiana sat on a high stool , dressing her hair at the glass , and interweaving her curls with artificial flowers and faded feathers , of which she had found a store in a drawer in the attic . I was making my bed , having received strict orders from Bessie to get it arranged before she returned , ( for Bessie now frequently employed me as a sort of under nursery maid , to tidy the room , dust the chairs , & c .). Having spread the quilt and folded my night - dress , I went to the window - seat to put in order some picture - books and doll ’ s house furniture scattered there ; an abrupt command from Georgiana to let her playthings alone (for the tiny chairs and mirrors, the fairy plates and cups , were her property ) stopped my proceedings ; and then , for lack of other occupation , I fell to breathing on the frost - flowers with which the window was fretted , and thus clearing a space in the glass through which I might look out on the grounds , where all was still and petrified under the influence of a hard frost .

乔治亚娜坐在一张高凳上，对着玻璃梳妆打扮，用人造花和褪色的羽毛编织她的卷发，她在阁楼的抽屉里发现了不少这些东西。我正在整理床铺，贝茜严令我在她回来之前把床铺整理好（因为贝茜现在经常雇我当保姆，整理房间、擦椅子等）。铺好被子，叠好睡衣，我走到靠窗的位置，想把散落在那里的一些画册和娃娃家的家具摆放整齐；乔治亚娜突然命令我不要碰她的玩物（因为小椅子和镜子、仙女盘子和杯子都是她的财产），这阻止了我的行动；后来，因为没有别的事可做，我就趴在窗前的霜花上喘气，这样就在玻璃上清理出了一块空地，我就可以透过这块空地眺望院子里的一切，在严霜的影响下，院子里的一切都静止了，石化了。

From this window were visible the porter ’ s lodge and the carriage road , and just as I had dissolved so much of the silver - white foliage veiling the panes as left room to look out , I saw the gates thrown open and a carriage roll through . I watched it ascending the drive with indifference : carriages often came to Gateshead , but none ever brought visitors in whom I was interested ; it stopped in front of the house , the door - bell rung loudly , the new - comer was admitted . All this being nothing to me , my vacant attention soon found livelier attraction in the spectacle of a little hungry robin , which came and chirped on the twigs of the leafless cherry - tree nailed against the wall near the casement . The remains of my breakfast of bread and milk stood on the table , and having crumbled a morsel of roll , I was tugging at the sash to put out the crumbs on the window - sill , when Bessie came running up stairs into the nursery .

从这扇窗户可以看到门房和马车道，正当我把遮住窗玻璃的银白色树叶拨开，留出空隙向外眺望时，我看到大门被推开，一辆马车驶了进来。我漠不关心地看着马车驶上车道：盖茨黑德经常有马车来，但从来没有一辆马车带来过我感兴趣的访客；马车在房前停下，门铃响起，新来的人被带了进去。这一切对我来说都不算什么，我空洞的注意力很快就被一只饥饿的小知更鸟吸引住了，它来到窗前，在钉在墙边的无叶樱桃树的树枝上叽叽喳喳地叫着。面包和牛奶的早餐残渣还在桌上，我把面包卷嚼得粉碎，正拽着窗扇想把窗台上的面包屑倒掉，贝茜跑上楼来，走进了婴儿房。

“ Miss Jane , take off your pinafore : what are you doing there ? Have you washed your hands and face this morning ?” I gave another tug before I answered , for I wanted the bird to be secure of its bread : the sash yielded ; I scattered the crumbs , some on the stone sill , some on the cherry - tree bough ; then , closing the window , I replied :

"简小姐，脱掉你的夹克衫：你在那儿干什么？今天早上你洗手洗脸了吗？"在回答之前，我又拽了拽窗帘，因为我想让小鸟吃到面包：窗帘让开了；我把面包屑撒了一地，有的撒在石台上，有的撒在樱桃树的枝桠上；然后，关上窗户，我回答说："没有，贝西；我把面包屑撒在窗台上了：

“ No , Bessie ; I have only just finished dusting .”

"不，贝茜，我刚打扫完"

“ Troublesome , careless child ! and what are you doing now ? You look quite red , as if you had been about some mischief : what were you opening the window for ?”

"麻烦、粗心的孩子！你现在在做什么？你看起来满脸通红，好像在做什么坏事：你为什么要打开窗户？

I was spared the trouble of answering , for Bessie seemed in too great a hurry to listen to explanations ; she hauled me to the washstand , inflicted a merciless , but , happily , brief scrub on my face and hands with soap , water , and a coarse towel ; disciplined my head with a bristly brush , denuded me of my pinafore , and then hurrying me to the top of the stairs , bid me go down directly , as I was wanted in the breakfast - room .

她把我拖到洗脸台前，用肥皂、水和一条粗毛巾毫不留情地、但令人高兴的是很短时间地擦洗了我的脸和手，用一把带毛的刷子训斥了我的头，脱掉了我的夹衣，然后急匆匆地把我拉到楼梯口，要我直接下去，因为早餐室里有人找我。

I would have asked who wanted me ; I would have demanded if Mrs . Reed was there ; but Bessie was already gone , and had closed the nursery door upon me ; I slowly descended . For nearly three months I had never been called to Mrs . Reed ’ s presence : restricted so long to the nursery , the breakfast , dining , and drawing - rooms were become for me awful regions , on which it dismayed me to intrude .

我本想问是谁找我；我本想问里德太太在不在；但是贝茜已经走了，她把育婴室的门给我关上了；我慢慢地下了楼。近三个月来，我从未被叫去见里德太太：由于长期被限制在育婴室里，早餐室、餐厅和客厅对我来说都成了可怕的地方，让我不敢轻易闯入。

I now stood in the empty hall ; before me was the breakfast - room door , and I stopped , intimidated and trembling . What a miserable little poltroonx had fear , engendered of unjust punishment , made of me in those days ! I feared to return to the nursery ; I feared to go forward to the parlor ; ten minutes I stood in agitated hesitation ; the vehement ringing of the breakfast - room bell decided me ; I must enter .

"我不撒谎；如果我撒谎，我就会说我爱你；但我宣布我不爱你；除了约翰-里德，我最不喜欢你；这本关于骗子的书，你可以把它送给你的女儿乔治亚娜，因为是她在撒谎，而不是我。

Mrs . Reed ’ s hands still lay on her work inactive ; her eye of ice continued to dwell freezing on mine :

里德太太的手仍然不自觉地放在她的工作上；她那双冰冷的眼睛继续凝视着我：

“ What more have you to say ?” she asked , rather in the tone in which a person might address an opponent of adult age than such as is ordinarily used to a child .

她问道："你还有什么要说的？"她的语气就像一个人对一个成年的对手说话时的语气，而不是通常对一个孩子说话时的语气。

That eye of hers , that voice , stirred every antipathy I had . Shaking from head to foot , thrilled with ungovernable excitement , I continued :

她的眼神，她的声音，激起了我所有的反感。我从头抖到脚，激动得难以抑制，继续说道：

“ I am glad you are no relation of mine ; I will never call you aunt again as long as I live ; I will never come to see you when I am grown up ; and if any one asks me how I liked you , and how you treated me , I will say the very thought of you makes me sick , and that you treated me with miserable cruelty .”

"我很高兴你不是我的亲戚；只要我活着，我再也不会叫你姨妈；我长大后再也不会来看你；如果有人问我喜欢你吗，你是怎么对待我的，我就会说一想到你我就恶心，你对待我非常残忍。"

“ How dare you affirm that , Jane Eyre ?”

"你怎么敢这么说，简爱？"

“ How dare I , Mrs . Reed ? How dare I ? Because it is the truth . You think I have no feelings , and that I can live without one bit of love or kindness ; but I cannot live so ; and you have no pity . I shall remember how you thrust me back — roughly and violently thrust me back into the red - room , and locked me up there — to my dying day ; though I was in agony ; though I cried out , while suffocating with distress , ‘ Have mercy ! Have mercy , Aunt Reed !’ And that punishment you made me suffer because your wicked boy struck me — knocked me down for nothing . I will tell anybody who asks me questions , this exact tale . People think you a good woman ; but you are bad - hard - hearted . You are deceitful !”

"我怎么敢，里德太太？我怎么敢？因为这是事实。你以为我没有感情，没有一点爱和仁慈我也能活下去；但我不能这样活下去，你没有怜悯之心。我会记得你是如何把我推回去的--粗暴地把我推回红房间，把我锁在那里--直到我死去的那一天；尽管我痛苦不堪；尽管我在窒息的痛苦中喊道：'发发慈悲吧！可怜可怜我吧，里德姨妈！而你让我遭受的惩罚，就是因为你那邪恶的儿子打了我--白白把我打倒在地。我会把这个故事原原本本地告诉任何问我问题的人。人们认为你是个好女人，可你心肠太坏了。你是个骗子！"

Ere I had finished this reply my soul began to expand , to exult , with the strangest sense of freedom , of triumph , I ever felt . It seemed as if an invisible bond had burst , and that I had struggled out into unhoped - for liberty . Not without cause was this sentiment ; Mrs . Reed looked frightened ; her work had slipped from her knee ; she was lifting up her hands , rocking herself to and fro , and even twisting her face as if she would cry .

还没等我回答完，我的灵魂就开始膨胀、沸腾，产生了一种从未有过的奇异的自由感和胜利感。仿佛有一条无形的纽带绷断了，我奋力挣扎，终于获得了无望的自由。里德太太看起来很害怕，她的工作从膝盖上滑落，她举起双手，摇来摇去，甚至扭曲着脸，好像要哭出来。

“ Jane , you are under a mistake ; what is the matter with you ? Why do you tremble so violently ? Would you like to drink some water ?”

"简，你搞错了，你怎么了？你为什么颤抖得这么厉害？你想喝点水吗？"

“ No , Mrs . Reed .”

"不，里德太太。"

“ Is there anything else you wish for , Jane ? I assure I desire to be your friend .”

"你还想要什么吗，简？我保证我想成为你的朋友"

“ Not you . You told Mr . Brocklehurst I had a bad character , a deceitful disposition ; and I ’ ll let everybody at Lowood know what you are , and what you have done .”

"不是说你你告诉布洛克赫斯特先生我品行不端，有欺骗性；我会让洛伍德的每个人都知道你的为人和所作所为。"

“ Jane , you don’ t understand these things ; children must be corrected for their faults .”

"简，你不明白这些事情；孩子们的缺点必须得到纠正。

“ Deceit is not my fault !” I cried out in a savage , high voice .

"欺骗不是我的错！"我用野蛮而高昂的声音喊道。

“ But you are passionate , Jane ; that you must allow ; and now return to the nursery , there ’ s a dear , and lie down a little .”

"但你是有激情的，简，这一点你必须允许；现在回育儿室去吧，那里有一个亲爱的，你可以躺一会儿。"

“ I am not your dear ; I cannot lie down ; send me to school soon , Mrs . Reed , for I hate to live here .”

"我不是你的宝贝，我不能躺下；快点送我去上学吧，里德太太，我讨厌住在这里。

“ I will indeed send her to school soon ,” murmured Mrs . Reed , sotto voce ; and gathering up her work , she abruptly quitted the apartment .

"我会尽快送她去学校的，"里德太太喃喃地说，然后收拾起她的工作，突然离开了房间。

I was left there alone , winner of the field . It was the hardest battle I had fought , and the first victory I had gained . I stood awhile on the rug , where Mr . Brocklehurst had stood , and I enjoyed my conqueror ’ s solitude . First , I smiled to myself and felt elate ; but this fierce pleasure subsided in me as fast as did the accelerated throb of my pulses . A child cannot quarrel with its elders , as I had done ; cannot give its furious feelings uncontrolled play , as I had given mine , without experiencing afterward the pang of remorse and the chill of reaction . A ridge of lighted heath , alive , glancing , devouring , would have been a meet emblem of my mind when I accused and menaced Mrs . Reed ; the same ridge , black and blasted after the flames are dead , would have represented as metely my subsequent condition , when half an hour’ s silence and reflection had shown me the madness of my conduct , and the dreariness of my hated and hating position .

只剩下我一个人，成了战场上的胜利者。这是我打的最艰苦的一仗，也是我取得的第一个胜利。我在布罗克赫斯特先生站过的地毯上站了一会儿，享受着征服者的孤独。起初，我自嘲地笑了笑，感到欣喜若狂；但这种强烈的快感很快就消退了，就像我的脉搏加速跳动一样。一个孩子不可能像我一样与长辈争吵，不可能像我一样肆无忌惮地玩弄自己狂暴的感情，而事后不会感到懊悔和寒冷。当我指责和威胁里德太太的时候，我的脑海中会浮现出一道亮光闪烁、吞噬一切的沼泽山脊；当半小时的沉默和反思让我明白了自己行为的疯狂，以及我被憎恨和憎恨的处境的悲惨时，这道在火焰熄灭后变得漆黑一片的山脊也会如实地反映出我后来的处境。

Something of vengeance I had tasted for the first time ; as aromatic wine it seemed on swallowing , warm and racy ; z its after - flavor , metallic and corroding , gave me a sensation as if I had been poisoned . Willingly would I now have gone and asked Mrs . Reed ’ s pardon ; but I knew , partly from experience and partly from instinct , that was the way to make her repulse me with double scorn , thereby re ë xciting every turbulent impulse of my nature .

我第一次尝到了复仇的滋味；它就像芳香的葡萄酒，咽下时温暖而爽口；z 它的余味是金属味和腐蚀味，给我的感觉就像中了毒。我现在很想去请求里德太太的原谅；但我知道，部分是经验，部分是本能，这样做会让她加倍鄙视我，从而重新激起我天性中的每一个动荡的冲动。

I would fain exercise some better faculty than that of fierce speaking ; fain find some nourishment for some less fiendish feeling than that of sombre indignation . I took a book , some Arabian tales ; I sat down and endeavored to read . I could make no sense of the subject ; my own thoughts swam always between me and the page I had always found fascinating . I opened a glass door in the breakfast - room ; the shrubbery was quite still ; the black frost reigned , unbroken by sun or breeze , through the grounds . I covered my head and arms with the skirt of my frock , and went out to walk in a part of the plantation which was quite sequestered ; but I found no pleasure in the silent trees , the fallen fir - cones , the congealed relics of autumn , russet leaves , swept by past winds in heaps , and now stiffened together . I leaned against a gate , and looked into an empty field , where no sheep were feeding , where the short grass was nipped and blanched . It was a very gray day ; a most opaque sky , “ onding on snaw ,” aa canopied all ; thence flakes fell at intervals , which settled on the hard path and on the hoary lea without melting . I stood , a wretched child enough , whispering to myself over and over again , “ What shall I do ? what shall I do ?”

我很想行使一些比激烈言辞更好的能力；很想为一些比阴沉的愤慨更温和的感情找到一些养料。我拿了一本书，是一些阿拉伯故事；我坐下来，努力阅读。我无法理解书中的主题；我自己的思绪总是在我和我一直认为引人入胜的书页之间游荡。我打开早餐室的玻璃门，灌木丛静悄悄的，黑霜笼罩着大地，没有阳光，也没有微风。我用连衣裙的裙摆遮住头和胳膊，到种植园中一处相当隐蔽的地方散步；但我在寂静的树木、倒下的杉果、凝固的秋天遗物、赤褐色的树叶中找不到任何乐趣，这些树叶被过去的风卷起成堆，现在又僵硬在一起。我倚着一扇门，望着空旷的田野，那里没有羊群在觅食，短短的青草被咬得枯黄。今天天气灰蒙蒙的，不透光的天空 "嘎吱嘎吱 "地笼罩着一切，片片雪花时不时地飘落下来，落在坚硬的小路上，落在苍老的树叶上，却没有融化。我站在原地，像个可怜的孩子，一遍又一遍地自言自语："我该怎么办，我该怎么办？"

All at once I heard a clear voice call , “ Miss Jane ! where are you ? Come to lunch !”

突然，我听到一个清脆的声音叫道："简小姐！你在哪里？快来吃午饭！"

It was Bessie , I knew well enough ; but I did not stir : her light step came tripping down the path .

是贝茜，我很清楚；但我没有动：她轻盈的脚步从小路绊了过来。

“ You naughty little thing !” she said . “Why don’t you come when you are called ?”

"你这个淘气的小东西！"她说。"叫你为什么不来？"

Bessie ’ s presence now, compared with the thoughts over which I had been brooding , seemed cheerful ; even though , as usual , she was somewhat cross . The fact is , after my conflict with , and victory over , Mrs . Reed , I was not disposed to care much for the nurse - maid ’ s transitory anger ; and I was disposed to bask in her youthful lightness of heart . I just put my two arms round her , and said , “ Come , Bessie ! don ’ t scold .”

贝茜现在的样子，比起我一直耿耿于怀的思绪，显得很开朗；尽管她像往常一样，有些不高兴。事实上，在我和里德太太发生冲突并战胜她之后，我就不太在乎这位女护士短暂的愤怒了；我愿意沉浸在她年轻时的轻松愉快之中。我用两只胳膊搂住她，说："来，贝茜！别骂人。"

The action was more frank and fearless than any I was habituated to indulge in ; somehow it pleased her .

这个举动比我习惯的任何举动都要坦率、无畏；不知怎么的，这让她很高兴。

“ You are a strange child , Miss Jane ,” she said , as she looked down at me ; “ a little , roving , solitary thing ; and you are going to school , I suppose ?”

"简小姐，你真是个奇怪的孩子，"她低头看着我说，"一个四处游荡、独来独往的小东西，我想你是要去上学吧？"

I nodded .

我点了点头。

“ And won ’ t you be sorry to leave poor Bessie ?”

"离开可怜的贝茜，你不难过吗？"

“ What does Bessie care for me ? She is always scolding me.”

"贝茜在乎我什么？她总是骂我

“Because you’ re such a queer , frightened , shy little thing . You should be bolder .”

"因为你是个古怪、害怕、害羞的小东西你应该更大胆些

“ What ! to get more knocks ?”

"怎么，想挨更多的打吗？

“Nonsense! But you are rather put upon , that ’ s certain . My mother said , when she came to see me last week , that she would not like a little one of her own to be in your place . Now come in, and I’ ve some good news for you .”

"胡说八道"胡说八道！""但你确实被人欺负了。我妈妈上星期来看我的时候说，她不希望她自己的孩子代替你的位置。进来吧，我有好消息要告诉你。"

“ I don’ t think you have , Bessie .”

"我想你没有，贝茜"

“ Child ! What do you mean ? What sorrowful eyes you fix on me ! Well ! but missis and the young ladies and Master John are going out to tea this afternoon , and you shall have tea with me . I ’ ll ask the cook to bake you a little cake , and then you shall help me to look over your drawers ; for I am soon to pack your trunk . Missis intends you to leave Gateshead in a day or two , and you shall choose what toys you like to take with you .”

"孩子你什么意思？你看我的眼神多悲伤啊好吧，但是夫人和年轻的女士们还有约翰少爷今天下午要出去喝茶，你要和我一起喝茶。我会让厨娘给你烤个小蛋糕，然后你帮我翻翻你的抽屉，因为我马上就要给你收拾行李了。小姐打算让你在一两天内离开盖茨黑德，你可以选择你喜欢的玩具。

“ Bessie , you must promise not to scold me any more till I go .”

"贝茜，你要保证在我走之前不再骂我。"

“ Well , I will ; but mind you are a very good girl , and don ’ t be afraid of me . Don ’ t start when I chance to speak rather sharply ; it ’ s so provoking .”

"好的，我会的；不过你要记住，你是个非常乖的女孩，不要怕我。当我说话比较尖刻的时候，你可别发火，那太让人生气了。

“ I don ’ t think I shall ever be afraid of you again , Bessie , because I’ve got used to you ; and I shall soon have another set of people to dread .”

"我想我再也不会怕你了，贝茜，因为我已经习惯了你；而且我很快就会有另一群人让我害怕了。"

“ If you dread them they’ ll dislike you .”

"如果你害怕他们 他们就会不喜欢你"

“ As you do , Bessie ?”

"就像你一样吗，贝茜？

“ I don ’ t dislike you , miss ; I believe I am fonder of you than all the others .”

"我不讨厌你，小姐 我相信我比其他人都更喜欢你"

“ You don ’ t show it .”

"你没有表现出来"

“ You little sharp thing ! you ’ ve got quite a new way of talking . What makes you so venturesome and hardy ?”

"你这个小尖嘴猴腮！你说话的方式可真新鲜。是什么让你如此冒险和坚韧？"

“ Why , I shall soon be away from you , and besides —.” I was going to say something about what had passed between me and Mrs . Reed ; but on second thoughts I considered it better to remain silent on that head .

"为什么，我很快就要离开你了，而且......"我本想说说我和里德太太之间的事，但转念一想，还是不说为好。

“ And so you are glad to leave me ?”

"所以你很高兴离开我？"

“ Not at all , Bessie ; indeed , just now I am rather sorry .”

"一点也不，贝茜；事实上，刚才我还挺遗憾的。"

“ Just now ! and rather ! How coolly my little lady says it ! I dare say now , if I were to ask you for a kiss you wouldn’ t give it me ; you’d say you would rather not .”

"刚才！而且是相当遗憾！我的小姑娘说得多么冷静啊！我敢说，现在如果我向你要一个吻，你不会给我的；你会说你宁愿不要。

“ I ’ ll kiss you and welcome ; bend your head down .” Bessie stooped ; we mutually embraced , and I followed her into the house quite comforted . That afternoon lapsed in peace and harmony ; and in the evening Bessie told me some of her most en - chaining stories , and sung me some of her sweetest songs . Even for me life had its gleams of sunshine .

"我会吻你，欢迎你，把头弯下来"贝茜弯下腰，我们相互拥抱，我很欣慰地跟着她进了屋。那个下午在平静与和谐中度过；晚上，贝茜给我讲了一些她最动人的故事，唱了一些她最动听的歌曲。即使对我来说，生活也充满了阳光。
