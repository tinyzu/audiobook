Ere the half - hour ended , five o ‘ clock struck ; school was dismissed , and all were gone into the refectory to tea . I now ventured to descend ; it was deep dusk ; I retired into a corner and sat down on the floor . The spell by which I had been so far supported began to dissolve ; reaction took place , and soon , so overwhelming was the grief that seized me , I sunk prostrate with my face to the ground . Now I wept ; Helen Burns was not here ; nothing sustained me ; left to myself , I abandoned myself , and my tears watered the boards . I had meant to be so good and to do so much at Lowood ; to make so many friends , to earn respect , and win affection . Already I had made visible progress ; that very morning I had reached the head of my class ; Miss Miller had praised me warmly ; Miss Temple had smiled approbation ; she had promised to teach me drawing , and to let me learn French , if I continued to make similar improvement two months longer : and then I was well received by my fellow - pupils ; treated as an equal by those of my own age , and not molested by any ; now , here I lay again crushed and trodden on ; and could I ever rise more ?

半小时还没结束，五点钟就到了；学校放学了，所有人都到餐厅去喝茶了。我现在才敢下楼，天色已经很暗了，我退到一个角落里，席地而坐。一直以来支撑着我的魔力开始消散；我的身体发生了反应，很快，我就被悲伤淹没了，脸朝下瘫坐在地上。现在我哭了，海伦-伯恩斯不在这里，没有任何东西能支撑我，我只能靠自己，我放弃了自己，我的眼泪浇湿了木板。我本想在罗伍德做得这么好，做得这么多；交这么多朋友，赢得尊敬，赢得爱戴。我已经取得了明显的进步；就在那天早上，我已经在班上名列前茅；米勒小姐热情地表扬了我；坦普尔小姐也露出了赞许的微笑；她答应教我画画，如果我再有两个月的类似进步，她还会让我学法语：然后，我受到了同学们的欢迎；同龄人平等地对待我，没有人欺负我；现在，我又躺在这里，被人踩在脚下，我还能再站起来吗？

“ Never ,” I thought ; and ardently I wished to die . While sobbing out this wish in broken accents , some one approached ; I started up — again Helen Burns was near me ; the fading fires just showed her coming up the long vacant room ; she brought my coffee and bread .

我想，"永远不可能了"，我热切地希望死去。就在我用断断续续的声音啜泣着说出这个愿望的时候，有人走了过来；我站了起来--海伦-伯恩斯又出现在了我的身边；微弱的火光正好显示她走到了空了很久的房间里；她给我端来了咖啡和面包。

“ Come , eat something ,” she said ; but I put both away from me , feeling as if a drop or a crumb would have choked me in my present condition . Helen regarded me , probably with surprise ; I could not now abate my agitation , though I tried hard ; I continued to weep aloud . She sat down on the ground near me , embraced her knees with her arms , and rested her head upon them ; in that attitude she remained silent as an Indian . I was the first who spoke .

她说："来，吃点东西，"但我把这两样东西放在一边，感觉好像在我现在的状况下，一滴水或一粒面包屑都会让我窒息。海伦看了看我，可能是感到惊讶；我现在无法平复激动的心情，尽管我很努力；我继续大声哭泣。她坐在我身边的地上，用双臂抱住膝盖，把头靠在上面；她就像印第安人一样保持着这种姿势，一言不发。我第一个开口说话。

“ Helen , why do you stay with a girl whom everybody believes to be a liar ?”

"海伦，你为什么要和一个大家都认为是骗子的女孩在一起？"

“ Everybody , Jane ? Why , there are only eighty people who have heard you called so , and the world contains hundreds of millions ?”

"所有人，简？为什么，只有八十个人听过你这么说 而世界上有上亿人呢？"

“ But what have I to do with millions ? The eighty I know despise me .”

"可我和几百万人有什么关系？我认识的八十个人都瞧不起我。

“ Jane , you are mistaken ; probably not one in the school either despises or dislikes you ; many , I am sure , pity you much .”

"简，你错了，学校里可能没有一个人鄙视或不喜欢你；我敢肯定，很多人都非常同情你。"

"布罗克赫斯特先生都这么说了，他们怎么会可怜我呢？

Had he treated you as an especial favorite , you would have found enemies , declared or covert , all around you : as it is , the greater number would offer you sympathy if they dared . Teachers and pupils may look coldly on you for a day or two , but friendly feelings are concealed in their hearts ; and if you persevere in doing well , these feelings will ere long appear so much the more evidently for their temporary suppression . "布罗克赫斯特先生不是神，甚至也不是受人敬仰的伟人，他在这里很少受人喜欢，他从来没有采取过措施让自己受人喜欢。如果他把你当做一个特别受宠的人，你就会发现周围到处都是或明或暗的敌人：现在的情况是，如果他们敢的话，更多的人会对你表示同情。老师和学生们可能会对你冷眼相待一两天，但他们心中都隐藏着友好的情感；如果你坚持不懈地做好自己，这些情感很快就会因为暂时的压抑而显露出来。此外，简--"她停顿了一下。

"好了，海伦！"我说，把手伸进她的手心里；她轻轻地皴了皴我的手指，让它们暖和起来，然后继续

"不，我知道我应该为自己着想，但这还不够，如果别人不爱我，我宁死也不愿意活下去，我不能忍受孤独和被人憎恨，海伦。看，为了得到你，或者坦普尔小姐，或者其他我真心爱的人的一些真正的爱，我甘愿让人打断我的手臂，或者让一头公牛把我扑倒，或者站在一匹踢马的后面，让它用蹄子踢我的胸膛--"

“ Hush , Jane ! you think too much of the love of human beings ; you are too impulsive , too vehement ; the Sovereign hand that created your frame , and put life into it , has provided you with other resources than your feeble self , or than creatures feeble as you . Brocklehurst has weakly and pompously repeated at second - hand from Mrs . Reed ; for I read a sincere nature in your ardent eyes and on your clear front ), and God waits only the separation of spirit from flesh to crown us with a full reward . "嘘，简，你对人类的爱想得太多了，你太冲动、太激烈了；创造你的身体、赋予你生命的主宰之手，除了你这个弱小的自己，或者像你一样弱小的生物之外，还为你提供了其他资源。除了这个地球，除了人类，还有一个无形的世界和一个灵魂的王国；那个世界就在我们周围，因为它无处不在；那些灵魂注视着我们，因为他们受命守护我们；如果我们在痛苦和耻辱中死去，如果四面八方的蔑视击打着我们，仇恨压垮了我们，天使们会看到我们的折磨，承认我们的无辜（如果我们是无辜的，就像我知道你对布罗克尔赫斯特先生软弱无力的指控一样）。布罗克赫斯特先生软弱无力、华而不实地重复了里德夫人的指控；因为我从你热切的眼神和清澈的脸庞上读出了真诚的本性），上帝只等着灵肉分离，就会给我们以充分的奖赏。既然生命这么快就会结束，而死亡又是通往幸福和荣耀的必经之路，我们为什么还要沉浸在痛苦之中呢？

I was silent ; Helen had calmed me ; but in the tranquillity she imparted there was an alloy of inexpressible sadness . I felt the impression of woe as she spoke , but I could not tell whence it came ; and when , having done speaking , she breathed a little fast and coughed a short cough , I momentarily forgot my own sorrows to yield to a vague concern for her .

我沉默不语；海伦使我平静下来；但在她传递给我的宁静中，却夹杂着难以言喻的悲伤。在她说话的时候，我感到了一种悲哀的感觉，但我不知道这种感觉从何而来；当她说完话后，呼吸有点急促，咳嗽了一阵，我顿时忘记了自己的悲伤，转而对她产生了一种模糊的担忧。

Resting my head on Helen ’ s shoulder , I put my arms round her waist ; she drew me to her , and we reposed in silence . We had not sat long thus , when another person came in . Some heavy clouds , swept from the sky by a rising wind , had left the moon bare ; and her light , streaming in through a window near , shone full both on us and on the approaching figure , which we at once recognized as Miss Temple .

我把头靠在海伦的肩膀上，双手环抱着她的腰，她把我拉向她，我们就这样静静地躺着。我们这样坐了没多久，另一个人走了进来。几片厚厚的乌云被一阵风卷走了，天空中只剩下一轮明月；她的光芒从附近的一扇窗户射进来，照得我们和那个走近的身影都很清楚，我们一眼就认出那是坦普尔小姐。

“ I came on purpose to find you , Jane Eyre ,” said she ; “ I want you in my room ; and as Helen Burns is with you , she may come too .”

"我是特意来找你的，简-爱，"她说，"我想让你到我的房间里来，既然海伦-伯恩斯和你在一起，她也可以来。"

We went ; following the superintendent ’ s guidance , we had to tread some intricate passages , and mount a stair - case before we reached her apartment ; it contained a good fire , and looked cheerful . Miss Temple told Helen Burns to be seated in a low arm - chair on one side of the hearth , and herself taking another , she called me to her side .

我们去了，在管理员的指引下，我们走了一些错综复杂的通道，上了一个楼梯，才到了她的居室；里面生着旺火，看起来很热闹。坦普尔小姐让海伦-伯恩斯坐在壁炉一侧的矮扶手椅上，她自己则坐在另一张椅子上，然后把我叫到她身边。

“ Is it all over ?” she asked , looking down at my face . “ Have you cried your grief away ?”

"一切都结束了吗？"她看着我的脸问道。"你的悲伤都哭出来了吗？"

“ I am afraid I never shall do that .”

"恐怕我永远也哭不出来了"

“ Why ?”

"为什么？

“ Because I have been wrongly accused ; and you , ma ‘ am , and everybody else , will now think me wicked .”

"因为我被冤枉了" "而你，夫人，还有其他人" "现在都会认为我是邪恶的"

“ We shall think you what you prove yourself to be , my child . Continue to act as a good girl , and you will satisfy me .”

"我们会认为你证明了自己是什么，我的孩子。继续做个好女孩 你会让我满意的"

“ Shall I , Miss Temple ?”

"我会吗，坦普尔小姐？

“ You will ,” said she , passing her arm round me . “ And now tell me who is the lady whom Mr . Brocklehurst called your benefactress ?”

"你会的，"她说，用胳膊搂着我"现在告诉我 布洛克赫斯特先生称你为恩人的女士是谁？"

“ Mrs . Reed , my uncle ’ s wife . My uncle is dead , and he left me to her care .”

"里德太太，我叔叔的妻子。我叔叔去世了，他把我托付给她照顾。"

“ Did she not , then , adopt you of her own accord ?”

"那她不是自愿收养你的吗？"

“ No , maam ; she was sorry to have to do it ; but my uncle , as I have often heard the servants say , got her to promise , before he died , that she would always keep me .”

"不是的，太太，她很抱歉要这么做，但是我叔叔，就像我经常听仆人们说的那样，在他死之前让她答应永远抚养我。

“ Well , now , Jane , you know , or at least I will tell you , that when a criminal is accused , he is always allowed to speak in his own defence . You have been charged with falsehood ; defend yourself to me as well as you can . Say whatever your memory suggests as true ; but add nothing and exaggerate nothing .”

"好了，简，你知道，至少我要告诉你，当一个罪犯被指控时，他总是可以为自己辩护的。你被指控犯有虚假罪，请尽可能地为自己辩护。只要你的记忆认为是真实的，就说出来；但不要添加任何东西，也不要夸大任何东西。

I resolved in the depth of my heart that I would be most moderate — most correct ; and , having reflected a few minutes in order to arrange coherently what I had to say , I told her all the story of my sad childhood . Exhausted by emotion , my language was more subdued than it generally was when it developed that sad theme ; and mindful of Helen ’ s warnings against the indulgence of resentment , I infused into the narrative far less of gall and wormwood than ordinary . Thus restrained and simplified , it sounded more credible ; I felt as I went on that Miss Temple fully believed me .

我从心底里下定决心，我一定要做到最温和、最正确；我思考了几分钟，以便连贯地安排我要说的话，然后我向她讲述了我悲惨童年的所有故事。由于情绪激动，我的语言比一般人在讲述这个悲惨的主题时更加低沉；考虑到海伦警告我不要放纵怨恨，我在叙述中注入的胆汁和苦艾比一般人少得多。这样一来，我的叙述就更有节制、更简洁，听起来也更可信了；我觉得坦普尔小姐完全相信了我的话。

In the course of the tale I had mentioned Mr . Lloyd as having come to see me after the fit ; for I never forgot the , to me , frightful episode of the red - room ; in detailing which , my excitement was sure , in some degree , to break bounds ; for nothing could soften in my recollection the spasm of agony which clutched my heart when Mrs . Reed spurned my wild supplication for pardon , and locked me a second time in the dark and haunted chamber .

在讲故事的过程中，我提到劳埃德先生在我发病后来看过我；因为我从来没有忘记过红屋里那段对我来说可怕的插曲；在详细叙述这段插曲时，我的激动肯定会在某种程度上突破界限；因为在我的记忆中，当里德太太拒绝了我疯狂的求饶，第二次把我锁在黑暗而闹鬼的房间里时，那种紧紧抓住我的心的痛苦痉挛是任何东西都无法抚平的。

I had finished . Miss Temple regarded me a few minutes in silence ; she then said —

我说完了。坦普尔小姐沉默地看了我几分钟，然后说

“ I know something of Mr . Lloyd ; I shall write to him ; if his reply agrees with your statement , you shall be publicly cleared from every imputation ; to me , Jane , you are clear now .”

"我知道劳埃德先生的一些情况，我会写信给他；如果他的回信与你的陈述一致，你就可以公开洗脱一切罪名；对我来说，简，你现在已经清白了。

She kissed me , and still keeping me at her side ( where I was contented to stand , for I derived a child ’ s pleasure from the contemplation of her face , her dress , her one or two ornaments , her white forehead , her clustered and shining curls , and beaming dark eyes ), she proceeded to address Helen Burns .

"海伦，今晚过得好吗？今天咳嗽得厉害吗？"

"我想不太咳了，夫人"

"胸口的疼痛呢？"

"好多了"

坦普尔小姐站起身来，握着她的手，检查了一下她的脉搏，然后她回到了自己的座位上；当她重新坐下来时，我听到她低低的叹了口气。她沉思了几分钟，然后振作起来，愉快地说

"但你们两位今晚是我的访客，我必须这样对待你们"。她按响了门铃。

"芭芭拉，"她对应铃的仆人说，"我还没喝茶，把托盘端来，给这两位年轻女士放上杯子。"

And the tray was soon brought . How pretty to my eyes did the china cups and bright tea - pot look , placed on the little round table near the fire ! How fragrant was the steam of the beverage , and the scent of the toast ! of which , however , I , to my dismay ( for I was beginning to be hungry ), discerned only a very small portion ; Miss Temple discerned it too .

托盘很快就端了上来瓷杯和明亮的茶壶放在靠近炉火的小圆桌上，在我眼里是多么漂亮！饮料的蒸汽和吐司的香味是多么芬芳啊！然而，令我沮丧的是（因为我开始饿了），我只看到了一小部分；坦普尔小姐也看到了。

“ Barbara ,” said she , “ can you not bring a little more bread and butter ? There is not enough for three .”

"芭芭拉，"她说，"你能不能多拿点面包和黄油？不够三个人吃的。"

Barbara went out ; she returned soon :

芭芭拉出去了，很快又回来了

“ Madam , Mrs . Harden says she has sent up the usual quantity .”

"夫人，哈登太太说她已经送来了平常的份量"

Mrs . Harden , be it observed , was the housekeeper , a woman after Mr . Brocklehurst ’ s own heart , made up of equal parts whale - bone and iron .

哈登太太是女管家，布罗克尔赫斯特先生心目中的好女人，是由鲸骨和铁组成的。

“ Oh , very well !” returned Miss Temple ; “ we must make it do , Barbara , I suppose .” And as the girl withdrew , she added , smilingly , “ Fortunately , I have it in my power to supply deficiencies for this once .”

"哦，很好！"坦普尔小姐回道，"我想，我们必须这样做，芭芭拉。"女孩走后，她笑着补充道："幸运的是，我有能力弥补这一次的不足。"

Having invited Helen and me to approach the table , and placed before each of us a cup of tea , with one delicious but thin morsel of toast , she got up , and unlocked a drawer , and taking from it a parcel wrapped in paper , disclosed presently to our eyes a good - sized seed - cake .

她请海伦和我走近餐桌，在我们面前每人放了一杯茶，还有一块美味但很薄的吐司，然后她站起身来，打开抽屉的锁，从里面拿出一个用纸包着的包裹，在我们眼前露出了一个大小适中的种子蛋糕。

“ I meant to give each of you some of this to take with you ,” said she ; “ but as there is so little toast you must have it now ,” and she proceeded to cut slices with a generous hand .

她说："我本想给你们每人一些，让你们带着吃，""但现在吐司太少了，你们必须得吃。"说完，她慷慨地切了几片。

We feasted that evening as on nectar and ambrosia ; and not the least delight of the entertainment was the smile of gratification with which our hostess regarded us , as we satisfied our famished appetites on the delicate fare she liberally supplied . Tea over and the tray removed , she again summoned us to the fire ; we sat one on each side of her , and now a conversation followed between her and Helen , which it was indeed a privilege to be admitted to hear .

当晚，我们大快朵颐，如饮甘露；当我们用女主人慷慨提供的精美食物满足了饥肠辘辘的胃口时，女主人露出了欣慰的笑容，这并不是娱乐中最令人高兴的事。喝完茶，端起托盘，她又把我们叫到火炉边；我们一左一右地坐在她身边，接着是她和海伦之间的谈话，能听到这样的谈话真是一种荣幸。

Miss Temple had always something of serenity in her air , of state in her mien , of refined propriety in her language , which precluded deviation into the ardent , the excited , the eager ; something which chastened the pleasure of those who looked on her and listened to her , by a controlling sense of awe ; such was my feeling now ; but as to Helen Burns , I was struck with wonder .

坦普尔小姐的气质总是那么宁静，神态总是那么庄重，语言总是那么文雅得体，这使她不至于偏离热情、激动和急切的轨道；这使那些看着她、听着她说话的人感到一种控制不住的敬畏感，从而抑制了他们的快乐；我现在就是这种感觉；但是，对于海伦-伯恩斯，我却感到十分惊奇。

The refreshing meal , the brilliant fire , the presence and kindness of her beloved instructress , or perhaps more than all these , something in her own unique mind , had roused her powers within her . They woke , they kindled ; first , they glowed in the bright tint of her cheek , which till this hour I had never seen but pale and bloodless ; then they shone in the liquid lustre of her eyes , which had suddenly acquired a beauty more singular than that of Miss Temple ‘ s — a beauty neither of fine color , nor long eyelash , nor pencilled brow , but of meaning , of movement , of radiance . Then her soul sat on her lips , and language flowed , from what source I cannot tell . Has a girl of fourteen a heart large enough , vigorous enough , to hold the swelling spring of pure , full , fervid eloquence ? Such was the characteristic of Helen ’ s discourse on that , to me , memorable evening ; her spirit seemed hastening to live within a very brief span as much as many live during a protracted existence .

清爽的饭菜、灿烂的炉火、她敬爱的女教师的在场和亲切，或者比这一切更重要的是，她自己独特的心灵中的某种东西唤醒了她内心的力量。它们苏醒了，它们点燃了；首先，它们在她脸颊的亮色中闪耀，在这之前，我从未见过她的脸颊如此苍白，毫无血色；然后，它们在她眼睛的液体光泽中闪耀，她的眼睛突然变得比坦普尔小姐的眼睛更奇特--既不是肤色的美，也不是长睫毛的美，更不是笔挺的眉毛的美，而是内涵的美，灵动的美，光芒四射的美。然后，她的灵魂坐在嘴唇上，语言流淌出来，至于源头是什么，我就不得而知了。一个十四岁的女孩，是否有一颗足够大、足够有力的心，来容纳纯洁、饱满、狂热的口才的涌泉？在那个令我难忘的夜晚，海伦的言谈就具有这样的特点；她的精神似乎就像许多人在漫长的生命中一样，在短暂的时间里匆匆地活了下来。

They conversed of things I had never heard of ; of nations and times past ; of countries far away ; of secrets of nature discovered or guessed at ; they spoke of books ; how many they had read ! What stores of knowledge they possessed ! Then they seemed so familiar with French names and French authors ; but my amazement reached its climax when Miss Temple asked Helen if she sometimes snatched a moment to recall the Latin her father had taught her ; and , taking a book from a shelf , bade her read and construe a page of Virgil ; and Helen obeyed , my organ of veneration expanding at every sounding line . She had scarcely finished ere the bell announced bed - time ; no delay could be admitted ; Miss Temple embraced us both , saying as she drew us to her heart :

她们谈论着我从未听说过的事情；谈论着过去的国家和时代；谈论着遥远的国家；谈论着发现或猜测到的自然秘密；她们谈论着书籍；她们读过多少书！他们拥有多么丰富的知识！我的惊讶达到了顶点，因为坦普尔小姐问海伦是否有时会抽空回想一下她父亲教给她的拉丁文，并从书架上拿起一本书，让她读一读维吉尔的一页。她还没读完，钟声就宣布就寝时间到了；不能再耽搁了；坦普尔小姐拥抱了我们俩，一边把我们拉到她的怀里，一边说："上帝保佑你们，我的孩子们！""上帝保佑你们，我的孩子们！"她说：

“ God bless you , my children !”

"上帝保佑你们，我的孩子们！"

Helen she held a little longer than me ; she let her go more reluctantly ; it was Helen her eye followed to the door ; it was for her she a second time breathed a sad sigh ; for her she wiped a tear from her cheek .

她抱着海伦的时间比抱着我的时间要长一些；她让海伦走得更不情愿；她的目光一直追随着海伦走到门口；她为海伦第二次发出了悲伤的叹息；她为海伦擦去了脸颊上的一滴眼泪。

On reaching the bed - room , we heard the voice of Miss Scatcherd ; she was examining drawers ; she had just pulled out Helen Burns ’, and when we entered Helen was greeted with a sharp reprimand , and told that to - morrow she should have half a dozen of untidy folded articles pinned to her shoulder .

到了卧室，我们听到斯卡彻德小姐的声音；她正在检查抽屉；她刚拉出海伦-伯恩斯的抽屉，当我们进去时，海伦受到了严厉的训斥，并被告知明天她的肩上要别上半打叠得乱七八糟的东西。

“ My things were , indeed , in shameful disorder ,” murmured Helen to me , in a low voice ; “ I intended to have arranged them , but I forgot .”

海伦低声对我说，"我本来想把它们整理好的，但我忘了。""我的东西确实乱得不成样子，"海伦喃喃地说。

Next morning , Miss Scatcherd wrote in conspicuous characters on a piece of pasteboard the word “ Slattern ,” and bound it like a phylactery round Helen ’ s large , mild , intelligent , and benign - looking forehead . She wore it till evening , patient , unresentful , regarding it as a deserved punishment . The moment Miss Scatcherd withdrew after afternoon school , I ran to Helen , tore it off , and thrust it into the fire ; the fury of which she was incapable had been burning in my soul all day , and tears , hot and large , had continually been scalding my cheek ; for the spectacle of her sad resignation gave me an intolerable pain at the heart .

第二天一早，斯卡彻德小姐在一块糊纸上用醒目的大字写了 "荡妇 "两个字，然后像法器一样把它绑在了海伦宽大、温和、聪慧、慈祥的额头上。她一直戴到傍晚，忍耐着，毫无怨言，把它看作是应得的惩罚。下午放学后，斯卡彻德小姐一走，我就跑到海伦身边，把它撕下来，扔进火里；她无法发泄的怒火在我的心灵中燃烧了一整天，又热又大的泪水不断地烫着我的脸颊；因为看到她伤心地辞职的样子，我的心痛得无法忍受。

About a week subsequently to the incidents above narrated , Miss Temple , who had written to Mr . Lloyd , received his answer : it appeared that what he said went to corroborate my account . Miss Temple , having assembled the whole school , announced that inquiry had been made into the charges alleged against Jane Eyre , and that she was most happy to be able to pronounce her completely cleared from every imputation . The teachers then shook hands with me and kissed me , and a murmur of pleasure ran through the ranks of my companions .

在上述事件发生一周后，给劳埃德先生写信的坦普尔小姐收到了他的回信：看来他的话证实了我的说法。坦普尔小姐召集了全校师生，宣布已经对简-爱受到的指控进行了调查，她非常高兴地宣布简-爱完全洗清了一切罪名。随后，老师们与我握手、亲吻，我的同伴们发出了一阵阵欢呼声。

Thus relieved of a grievous load , I from that hour set to work afresh , resolved to pioneer my way through every difficulty ; I toiled hard , and my success was proportionate to my efforts ; my memory , not naturally tenacious , improved with practice ; exercise sharpened my wits ; in a few weeks I was promoted to a higher class ; in less than two months I was allowed to commence French and drawing . I learned the first two tenses of the verb Etre , and sketched my first cottage ( whose walls , by - the - by , outrivalled in slope those of the leaning tower of Pisa ), on the same day . That night , on going to bed , I forgot to prepare in imagination , the Barmecide supperau of hot roast potatoes , or white bread and new milk , with which I was wont to amuse my inward cravings : I feasted , instead , on the spectacle of ideal drawings which I saw in the dark ; all the work of my own hands ; freely pencilled houses and trees , picturesque rocks and ruins , Cuyp - likeavgroups of cattle , sweet paintings of butterflies hovering over unblown roses , of birds picking at ripe cherries , of wrens ’ nests inclosing pearl - like eggs , wreathed about with young ivy sprays . I examined , too , in thought , the possibility of my ever being able to translate currently aw a certain little French story - book , which Madame Pierrot had that day shown me ; nor was that problem solved to my satisfaction ere I fell sweetly asleep .

就这样，我从沉重的负担中解脱出来，从那时起，我重新开始工作，决心开拓进取，克服每一个困难；我努力工作，我的成功与我的努力成正比；我的记忆力天生并不顽强，但通过练习，我的记忆力得到了提高；锻炼磨练了我的智慧；几个星期后，我升入了更高的班级；不到两个月，我就被允许开始学习法语和绘画。我学会了动词 Etre 的前两个时态，并在同一天画出了我的第一座小屋（顺便说一句，它的墙壁坡度比比萨斜塔的墙壁还要高）。当晚，上床睡觉时，我忘记了在想象中准备巴麦凯德晚餐，即热烤土豆或白面包和新牛奶，我习惯用它们来满足内心的渴望：取而代之的，是我在黑暗中看到的理想图画的景象；所有这些都是我亲手绘制的；用铅笔随意涂抹的房屋和树木、风景如画的岩石和废墟、凯普式的牛群、蝴蝶盘旋在尚未绽放的玫瑰上的甜美画作、鸟儿采摘成熟樱桃的画作、鹪鹩巢中珍珠般的鸟蛋、周围环绕着嫩常春藤的画作。皮埃罗夫人那天给我看了一本法国小故事书，我还在想，我现在能不能把这本书翻译出来；在我进入甜蜜的梦乡之前，这个问题还没有得到满意的解决。
