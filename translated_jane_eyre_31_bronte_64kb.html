What did St . John Rivers think of this earthly angel ? I naturally asked myself that question as I saw him turn to her and look at her ; and , as naturally , I sought the answer to the inquiry in his countenance . He had already withdrawn his eye from the Peri , hl and was looking at a humble tuft of daisies which grew by the wicket .

圣约翰-里弗斯是怎么看待这位人间天使的呢？当我看到他转过身来看着她时，我自然而然地问了自己这个问题；同样，我也自然而然地从他的表情中寻找答案。他的视线已经从佩里希尔身上移开，正看着门边一丛不起眼的雏菊。

“ A lovely evening ; but late for you to be out alone ,” he said , as he crushed the snowy heads of the closed flowers with his foot .

"他一边说，一边用脚踩碎了闭合的花朵雪白的头。

“Oh, I only came home from S ——( she mentioned the name of a large town some twenty miles distant ) this afternoon . Papa told me you had opened your school , and that the new mistress was come ; and so I put on my bonnet after tea , and ran up the valley to see her ; this is she ?” pointing to me .

"哦，我今天下午才从斯------（她提到了一个大城镇的名字，离这里大约有 20 英里远）回家。爸爸告诉我你的学校开学了，新女主人来了，所以我喝完茶就戴上帽子，跑到山谷里来看她；这是她吗？"她指着我说。

“ It is ,” said St . John .

"是的，"圣约翰说。

“ Do you think you shall like Morton ?” she asked of me , with a direct and naive simplicity of tone and manner , pleasing , if childlike .

"你觉得你会喜欢莫顿吗？"她问我，语气和举止直接而天真单纯，虽然像个孩子，却很讨人喜欢。

“ I hope I shall . I have many inducements to do so .”

"我希望我会。我有很多这样做的理由。"

“ Did you find your scholars as attentive as you expected ?”

"你觉得你的学生们和你想象的一样专心吗？"

It seemed to me that Mr . St . John ’ s under lip protruded , and his upper lip curled a moment . His mouth certainly looked a good deal compressed , and the lower part of his face unusually stern and square , as the laughing girl gave him this information . He lifted his gaze , too , from the daisies , and turned it on her . An unsmiling , a searching , a meaning gaze it was . She answered it with a second laugh ; and laughter well became her youth , her roses , her dimples , her bright eyes .

在我看来，圣约翰先生的下嘴唇突出来了，上嘴唇也卷曲了一下。当然，当这位爱笑的姑娘告诉他这个消息时，他的嘴看起来被压得很紧，脸的下半部分也显得异常严厉和方正。他也把目光从雏菊上移开，转向她。那是一种不苟言笑的、探究的、意味深长的目光。她用第二次笑声回应了他，笑声也成了她的青春、她的玫瑰、她的酒窝、她的明眸。

As he stood , mute and grave , she again fell to caressing Carlo . “ Poor Carlo loves me ,” said she . “ He is not stern and distant to his friends ; and if he could speak , he would not be silent .”

他呆呆地站着，表情严肃，她又开始抚摸卡罗。"可怜的卡罗爱我，"她说。"他对朋友并不严厉和疏远；如果他能说话，他就不会沉默。"

As she patted the dog ’ s head , bending with native grace before his young and austere master , I saw a glow rise to that master ’ s face . I saw his solemn eye melt with sudden fire , and flicker with resistless motion . Flushed and kindled thus , he looked nearly as beautiful for a man as she for a woman . His chest heaved once , as if his large heart , weary of despotic constriction , had expanded , despite the will , and made a vigorous bound for the attainment of liberty . But he curbed it , I think , as a resolute rider would curb a rearing steed . He responded neither by word nor movement to the gentle advances made him .

当她拍着狗头，在它年轻而肃穆的主人面前优雅地弯腰时，我看到主人的脸上浮现出一丝光彩。我看到他庄严的眼睛突然融化了火光，闪烁着坚定的神情。他的面色红润，神采飞扬，就像她的美貌一样。他的胸膛剧烈地起伏了一下，仿佛他那颗厌倦了专制束缚的硕大的心，不顾一切地膨胀起来，为获得自由而奋力一搏。但我想，他抑制住了，就像一个果断的骑手抑制住一匹狂奔的骏马一样。他既不说话，也不回应别人对他的温柔示好。

"不是时候！但我宣布现在正是时候。现在正是爸爸最需要人陪的时候，工厂已经关门了，他也没有什么事情可做。现在，里弗斯先生，来吧。你怎么这么害羞，这么愁眉苦脸的？她用自己的回答填补了他沉默的空隙。

"你还好吗？"她问道。她问得好，他的脸和她的长袍一样苍白。

“ Quite well ,” he enunciated ; and , with a bow , he left the gate . She went one way ; he another . She turned twice to gaze after him , as she tripped fairylike down the field ; he , as he strode firmly across , never turned at all .

"很好，"他说，然后鞠了一躬，离开了大门。她走了一条路，他走了另一条路。她两次转过身去注视着他，就像在田野里绊倒了仙女一样；而他，坚定地走过去，根本就没有转过身来。

This spectacle of another’ s suffering and sacrifices wrapped my thoughts from exclusive meditation on my own . Diana Rivers had designated her brother “ inexorable as death .” She had not exaggerated .

这种他人受难和牺牲的场面，把我的思绪从对自己的沉思中裹挟了出来。戴安娜-里弗斯曾说她的哥哥 "像死神一样无情"。她并没有夸大其词。
