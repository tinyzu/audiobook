The next day commenced as before , getting up and dressing by rushlight : but this morning we were obliged to dispense with the ceremony of washing : the water in the pitchers was frozen . A change had taken place in the weather the preceding evening , and a keen north - east wind , whistling through the crevices of our bed - room windows all night long , had made us shiver in our beds , and turned the contents of the ewers to ice .

第二天一如既往，我们打着手电筒起床穿衣，但今天早上我们不得不省去了洗漱的仪式：因为水壶里的水结冰了。前一天晚上天气发生了变化，东北风整夜从卧室窗户的缝隙中呼啸而过，吹得我们在床上瑟瑟发抖，水壶里的水也结成了冰。

Before the long hour and a half of prayers and Bible reading was over , I felt ready to perish with cold . Breakfast - time came at last , and this morning the porridge was not burned ; the quality was eatable , the quantity small . How small my portion seemed ! I wished it had been double .

一个半小时的祷告和读经还没结束，我就觉得自己快要冻死了。早餐时间终于到了，今天早上的粥没有烧焦；质量还可以吃，但量很少。我的份量似乎太少了！我真希望是双份。

In the course of the day I was enrolled a member of the fourth class , and regular tasks and occupations were assigned me . Hitherto I had only been a spectator of the proceedings at Lowood — I was now to become an actor therein . At first , being little accustomed to learn by heart , the lessons appeared to me both long and difficult ; the frequent change from task to task , too , bewildered me ; and I was glad , when , about three o‘ clock in the afternoon Miss Smith put into my hands a border of muslin , two yards long , together with needle , thimble , & c ., and sent me to sit in a quiet corner of the school - room , with directions to hem the same . At that hour most of the others were sewing likewise ; but one class still stood round Miss Scatcherd’ s chair reading , and as all was quiet , the subject of their lessons could be heard , together with the manner in which each girl acquitted herself , and the animadversions or commendations of Miss Scatcherd on the performance . It was English history . Among the readers , I observed my acquaintance of the verandah ; at the commencement of the lesson , her place had been at the top of the class , but for some error of pronunciation or some inattention to stops , ak she was suddenly sent to the very bottom . Even in that obscure position , Miss Scatcherd continued to make her an object of constant notice ; she was continually addressing to her such phrases as the following :—

这一天，我被编入第四班，并被分配了固定的任务和工作。在此之前，我只是罗伍德的一名旁观者，而现在我将成为其中的一名演员。起初，我不太习惯于死记硬背，课程对我来说既冗长又艰涩；任务与任务之间的频繁转换也让我感到困惑；当下午三点钟左右，史密斯小姐把一块两码长的薄纱边框，连同针、顶针等交到我手里时，我很高兴，她让我坐在教室一个安静的角落里，并嘱咐我把边框缝好。这时候，其他大多数同学也在缝纫；但有一个班的同学还围着斯卡彻德小姐的椅子在看书，由于大家都很安静，所以可以听到她们上课的内容，还有每个女孩的表现，以及斯卡彻德小姐对她们表现的批评或表扬。那是英国历史。在朗读者中，我看到了我在阳台上的熟人；刚开始上课时，她的成绩在班上名列前茅，但由于发音错误或不注意停顿，她一下子被打入了倒数第一名。即使在这样一个不起眼的位置上，斯卡切德老师仍然让她成为大家不断注意的对象；她不断地对她说以下这样的话：

“ Burns ” ( such it seems was her name ; the girls here were all called by their surnames , as boys are elsewhere ), “ Burns , you are standing on the side of your shoe ; turn your toes out immediately .” “ Burns , you poke your chin most unpleasantly ; draw it in .” “ Burns , I insist on your holding your head up ; I will not have you before me in that attitude ,” & c ., & c .

"伯恩斯"（看来这是她的名字；这里的女孩都用姓来称呼，就像其他地方的男孩一样），"伯恩斯，你站在鞋的侧面，马上把脚趾转出来"。"伯恩斯，你的下巴戳得很不舒服，把它收起来""伯恩斯，我坚持要你把头抬起来；我不会让你在我面前以这种姿态出现，"等等，等等。

A chapter having been read through twice , the books were closed and the girls examined . The lesson had comprised part of the reign of Charles I, and there were sundry questions about tonnage and poundage , and ship - money , which most of them appeared unable to answer ; still , every little difficulty was solved instantly when it reached Burns ; her memory seemed to have retained the substance of the whole lesson , and she was ready with answers on every point . I kept expecting that Miss Scatcherd would praise her attention ; but , instead of that , she suddenly cried out :

一章读完两遍后，合上书本，对女孩们进行检查。这节课包括查理一世统治时期的部分内容，有许多关于吨位、英镑和船币的问题，她们中的大多数人似乎都答不上来；不过，每一个小难题到了伯恩斯那里，都会立即迎刃而解；她的记忆似乎保留了整节课的内容，每一点她都能对答如流。我一直以为斯卡切德小姐会表扬她的专心，但她却突然大叫起来：

“ You dirty , disagreeable girl ! you have never cleaned your nails this morning !”

"你这个又脏又讨人厌的女孩！你今天早上从来没有洗过指甲！"

Burns made no answer ; I wondered at her silence .

伯恩斯没有回答；我对她的沉默感到奇怪。

“ Why ,” thought I , “ does she not explain that she could neither clean her nails nor wash her face , as the water was frozen ?”

"为什么，"我想，"她不解释一下，因为水结冰了，她既不能洗指甲，也不能洗脸？"

My attention was now called off by Miss Smith desiring me to hold a skein of thread . While she was winding it , she talked to me from time to time , asking whether I had ever been at school before , whether I could mark , al stitch , knit , & c .; till she dismissed me , I could not pursue my observations on Miss Scatcherd ’ s movements . When I returned to my seat , that lady was just delivering an order , of which I did not catch the import ; but Burns immediately left the class , and , going into the small inner room where the books were kept , returned in half a minute , carrying in her hand a bundle of twigs tied together at one end . This ominous tool she presented to Miss Scatcherd with a respectful courtesy ; then she quietly , and without being told , unloosed her pinafore , and the teacher instantly and sharply inflicted on her neck a dozen strokes with the bunch of twigs . Not a tear rose to Burns ’ eye ; and , while I paused from my sewing , because my fingers quivered at this spectacle with a sentiment of unavailing and impotent anger , not a feature of her pensive face altered its ordinary expression .

这时，史密斯小姐要我拿着一根线，我的注意力一下子被吸引了在她绕线的时候，她不时地跟我说话，问我以前是否上过学，是否会做记号、缝针、编织等等；直到她把我打发走，我才得以继续观察斯卡彻德小姐的一举一动。当我回到座位上时，那位女士正在下命令，我没听清是什么意思；但伯恩斯马上离开了教室，走进放书的小内室，半分钟后又回来了，手里拿着一捆一端绑在一起的树枝。她恭恭敬敬地把这个不祥的工具交给斯卡彻德老师，然后悄悄地，不由分说地解开了自己的夹克衫，老师立刻用那捆树枝在她的脖子上狠狠地抽了十几下。伯恩斯的眼睛里没有一滴眼泪；当我因为看到这一幕而手指颤抖，带着无用而无力的愤怒而暂停缝纫的时候，她那沉思的脸上却没有一丝一毫改变其平常的表情。

“ Hardened girl !” exclaimed Miss Scatcherd , “ nothing can correct you of your slatternly habits ; carry the rod away .”

"死丫头！"斯卡切德小姐喊道，"你这邋遢的习性是怎么也改不了的，把棍子拿开。

Burns obeyed . I looked at her narrowly as she emerged from the book - closet ; she was just putting back her handkerchief into her pocket , and the trace of a tear glistened on her thin cheek .

伯恩斯听话地走了。她从书橱里出来时，我仔细地看了她一眼；她刚把手帕放回口袋，瘦削的脸颊上闪烁着一丝泪光。

The play - hour in the evening I thought the pleasantest fraction of the day at Lowood ; the bit of bread , the draught of coffee swallowed at five o ‘ clock , had revived vitality , if it had not satisfied hunger ; the long restraint of the day was slackened ; the school room felt warmer than in the morning ; its fires being allowed to burn a little more brightly , to supply , in some measure , the place of candles , not yet introduced ; the ruddy gloaming , am the licensed uproar , the confusion of many voices , gave one a welcome sense of liberty .

我认为傍晚的游戏时间是罗伍德一天中最惬意的时光；五点钟吞下的一点面包、一壶咖啡，即使不能满足饥饿，也让人恢复了活力；一天的长期束缚松弛了下来；教室里感觉比早上暖和多了；炉火烧得更旺了，在某种程度上取代了还未使用的蜡烛的位置；朦胧的灯光、许可的喧闹声、混乱的人声，给人一种自由自在的感觉。

On the evening of the day on which I had seen Miss Scatcherd flog her pupil Burns , I wandered as usual among the forms and tables and laughing groups , without a companion , yet not feeling lonely ; when I passed the windows I now and then lifted a blind and looked out ; it snowed fast ; a drift was already forming against the lower panes ; putting my ear close to the window , I could distinguish , from the gleeful tumult within , the disconsolate moan of the wind outside .

就在我看到斯卡彻德小姐鞭打她的学生伯恩斯的那天傍晚，我像往常一样在人群、桌子和欢笑声中徘徊，没有同伴，但并不感到孤独；经过窗户时，我不时掀开百叶窗向外张望；雪下得很快，下层窗玻璃上已经形成了一片雪花；把耳朵贴近窗户，我可以从窗内欢快的喧闹声中分辨出窗外风的凄厉呻吟。

Probably , if I had lately left a good home and kind parents , this would have been the hour when I should most keenly have regretted the separation ; that wind would then have saddened my heart ; this obscure chaos would have disturbed my peace ; as it was , I derived from both a strange excitement , and , reckless and feverish , I wished the wind to howl more wildly , the gloom to deepen to darkness , and the confusion to rise to clamor .

也许，如果我不久前离开了美好的家庭和慈祥的父母，此时此刻，我一定会对分离感到最深切的悔恨；那时，那风一定会让我心酸；这晦暗的混乱一定会扰乱我的平静；而现在，我却从这两者中获得了一种莫名的兴奋，我不顾一切地、狂热地希望那风发出更狂野的嚎叫，希望那阴郁加深为黑暗，希望那混乱上升为喧嚣。

Jumping over forms and creeping under tables , I made my way to one of the fireplaces ; there , kneeling by the high wire fender , I found Burns , absorbed , silent , abstracted from all round her by the companionship of a book , which she read by the dim glare of the embers .

"还是拉塞拉斯吗？"我走到她身后问道。

"是的，"她说，"我刚刚读完"
