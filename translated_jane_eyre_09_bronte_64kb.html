第九章

But the privations , or , rather , the hardships , of Lowood , lessened . Spring drew on ; she was , indeed , already come ; the frosts of winter had ceased ; its snows were melted ; its cutting winds ameliorated . My wretched feet , flayed and swelled to lameness by the sharp air of January , began to heal and subside under the gender breathings of April . The nights and mornings no longer , by their Canadian temperature , froze the very blood in our veins ; we could now endure the play - hour passed in the garden . Sometimes , on a sunny day , it began even to be pleasant and genial ; and a greenness grew over those brown beds , which , freshening daily , suggested the thought that Hope traversed them at night , and left each morning brighter traces of her steps . Flowers peeped out among the leaves — snowdrops , crocuses , purple auriculas , and golden - eyed pansies . On Thursday afternoons ( half - holidays ) we now took walks , and found still sweeter flowers opening by the way - side , under the hedges .

但罗伍德的匮乏，或者说是艰辛，有所减轻。春天来临了，她确实已经来了，冬天的霜冻已经停止，雪融化了，刺骨的寒风也减弱了。我那被一月份尖锐的空气折磨得肿胀跛行的双脚，在四月的春风吹拂下开始愈合和消退。夜晚和清晨的加拿大气温不再冻结我们血管里的血液；我们现在可以忍受在花园里玩耍的时光了。有时，在阳光明媚的日子里，花园甚至开始变得怡人和煦；棕色的花坛上长满了绿色，每天都焕然一新，让人联想到霍普曾在夜里走过这些花坛，并在每天清晨留下了更鲜艳的脚印。花朵从树叶间探出头来--雪绒花、番红花、紫色金鸡菊和金眼三色堇。星期四下午（半休日），我们去散步，发现路边、树篱下开放着更甜美的花朵。

I discovered , too , that a great pleasure — an enjoyment which the horizon only bounded — lay all outside the high and spike - guarded walls of our garden . This pleasure consisted in a prospect of noble summits girding a great hill - hollow , rich in verdure and shadow ; in a bright beck , full of dark stones and sparkling eddies . How different had this scene looked when I viewed it laid out beneath the iron sky of winter , stiffened in frost , shrouded with snow — when mists as chill as death wandered to the impulse of east winds along those purple peaks , and rolled down “ ing ” and holm till they blended with the frozen fog of the beck ! ax That beck itself was then a torrent , turbid and curbless ; it tore asunder the wood , and sent a raving sound through the air , often thickened with wild rain or whirling sleet ; and for the forest on its banks , that showed only ranks of skeletons .

我还发现，在我们花园高高的围墙外，还有一种极大的乐趣--一种地平线所无法企及的乐趣。这种乐趣在于：高耸的山峰环绕着一个巨大的山谷，山谷中绿树成荫；一条明亮的小溪，溪水中布满了暗色的石头和闪闪发光的漩涡。当我在寒冬的铁色天空下欣赏这幅景象时，它是多么的与众不同，寒霜凛冽，白雪皑皑--那时，寒冷如死神的薄雾随着东风的吹拂，沿着紫色的山峰游荡，从 "ing "和冬青树上滚落下来，直到与小河的冻雾融为一体！溪流本身就是一股激流，浑浊而无路可走；它撕裂树林，在空中发出咆哮的声音，常常夹杂着狂暴的雨水或呼啸的雨夹雪；而溪流两岸的森林，只露出一排排骷髅。

April advanced to May . A bright , serene May it was ; days of blue sky , placid sunshine , and soft western or southern gales , filled up its duration . And now vegetation matured with vigor ; Lowood shook loose its tresses ; it became all green , all flowery ; its great elm , ash , and oak skeletons were restored to majestic life ; woodland plants sprung up profusely in its recesses ; unnumbered varieties of moss filled its hollows ; and it made a strange ground - sunshine out of the wealth of its wild primrose plants ; I have seen their pale , gold gleam , in overshadowed spots , like scatterings of the sweetest lustre . All this I enjoyed often and fully , free , unwatched , and almost alone ; for this unwonted liberty and pleasure there was a cause , to which it now becomes my task to advert .

四月进入五月。这是一个明媚宁静的五月；蓝天白云、灿烂阳光、柔和的西风或南风充斥着整个五月。现在，植被茁壮成长；罗伍德摇曳着它的发丝；它变得绿意盎然，繁花似锦；它那巨大的榆树、白蜡树和橡树骸骨又恢复了雄伟的生机；林地植物在它的凹处大量萌发；数不清的苔藓品种布满了它的空地；它用丰富的野生报春花植物制造出奇异的地面阳光；我曾看到它们在阴影中闪烁着淡淡的金光，就像散落的最甜美的光泽。我经常充分享受着这一切，自由自在，没有人监视，几乎是独自一人；之所以有这种从未有过的自由和快乐，是有原因的，现在我的任务就是要说明这一点。

Have I not described a pleasant site for a dwelling , when I speak of it as bosomed in hill and wood , and rising from the verge of a stream ? Assuredly , pleasant enough ; but whether healthy or not is another question .

当我说到它被山丘和树林环抱，从溪流边拔地而起时，我是不是描述了一个宜人的居住地？当然，是很宜人，但是否健康则是另一个问题。

That forest dell , where Lowood lay , was the cradle of fog and fog - bred pestilence ; 30 which , quickening with the quickening spring , crept into the Orphan Asylum , breathed typhus through its crowded school - room and dormitory , and , ere May arrived , transformed the seminary into a hospital .

罗伍德所在的森林小丘，是雾和雾气滋生的瘟疫的摇篮；30 随着春天的来临，瘟疫悄悄地钻进了孤儿院，在拥挤的教室和宿舍里传播伤寒，五月还没到，就把神学院变成了医院。

Semi - starvation and neglected colds had predisposed most of the pupils to receive infection . Forty-five out of the eighty girls lay ill at one time . Classes were broken up , rules relaxed . The few who continued well were allowed almost unlimited license , because the medical attendant insisted on the necessity of frequent exercise to keep them in health ; and had it been otherwise , no one had leisure to watch or restrain them . Miss Temple ’ s whole attention was absorbed by the patients ; she lived in the sick room , never quitting it except to snatch a few hours ’ rest at night . The teachers were fully occupied with packing up and making other necessary preparations for the departure of those girls who were fortunate enough to have friends and relations able and willing to remove them from the seat of contagion . Many , already smitten , went home only to die ; some died at the school , and were buried quietly and quickly , the nature of the malady forbidding delay .

半饥饿状态和被忽视的感冒使大多数学生容易受到感染。八十名女生中有四十五人曾一度患病。班级被拆散，规则被放宽。少数身体尚好的学生几乎可以无拘无束地玩耍，因为医务人员坚持认为必须经常锻炼才能保持健康。坦普尔小姐的全部精力都放在了病人身上；她住在病房里，除了晚上休息几个小时外，从不离开病房。老师们则忙着收拾行李和做其他必要的准备工作，以便让那些幸运地有朋友和亲戚能够并愿意带她们离开传染地的女孩离开。许多人已经被传染，回家后却死去了；有些人死在了学校里，由于疾病的性质不允许耽搁，她们被安静而迅速地埋葬了。

While disease had thus become an inhabitant of Lowood , and death its frequent visitor ; while there was gloom and fear within its walls ; while its rooms and passages steamed with hospital smells — the drug and the pastileay striving vainly to overcome the effluvia of mortality — that bright May shone unclouded over the bold hills and beautiful woodland out - of - doors . Its garden , too , glowed with flowers ; hollyhocks had sprung up tall as trees , lilies had opened , dahlias and roses were in bloom ; the borders of the little beds were gay with pink thrift and crimson double - daisies ; the sweet - briers gave out , morning and evening , their scent of spice and apples ; and these fragrant treasures were all useless for most of the inmates of Lowood , except to furnish now and then a handful of herbs and blossoms to put in a coffin .

疾病成了罗伍德的居民，死亡成了罗伍德的常客；围墙内弥漫着阴郁和恐惧；房间和走廊里弥漫着医院的气味--药物和牧草的气味在徒劳地克服着死亡的气息--而那个明媚的五月，却丝毫没有遮挡地照耀着室外粗犷的山丘和美丽的林地。罗伍德的花园里也是花团锦簇；冬青树已经长得像树一样高，百合花已经开放，大丽花和玫瑰也盛开了；小花坛的边上开满了粉红色的鹅掌楸和深红色的重瓣雏菊；香蒲从早到晚都散发着香料和苹果的芬芳；这些芬芳的宝物对罗伍德的大多数居民来说都是无用的，只能偶尔在棺材里放上一把香草和花朵。

But I , and the rest who continued well , enjoyed fully the beauties of the scene and season . They let us ramble in the wood , like gypsies , from morning till night ; we did what we liked — went where we liked ; we lived better , too . Mr . Brocklehurst and his family never came near Lowood now ; household matters were not scrutinized into ; the cross housekeeper was gone , driven away by the fear of infection ; her successor , who had been matron at the Lowton Dispensary , unused to the ways of her new abode , provided with comparative liberality . Besides , there were fewer to feed ; the sick could eat little ; our breakfast - basins were better filled ; when there was no time to prepare a regular dinner , which often happened , she would give us a large piece of cold pie , or a thick slice of bread and cheese , and this we carried away with us to the wood , where we each chose the spot we liked best , and dined sumptuously .

但我和其他身体尚好的人却充分享受了这一景致和季节的美好。他们让我们像吉普赛人一样在树林里漫步，从早到晚；我们想做什么就做什么，想去哪里就去哪里；我们的生活也变得更好了。布罗克赫斯特先生和他的家人现在从不靠近洛伍德了；家务事也不用细究了；十字架女管家走了，因为害怕传染病而被赶走了；她的继任者曾是洛顿药房的女院长，不习惯新住处的生活方式，她的供给相对宽松。此外，要养活的人少了；病人吃不了多少东西；我们的早餐桶里的食物更充足了；当没有时间准备常规晚餐时（这种情况经常发生），她会给我们一大块冷馅饼，或者厚厚的一片面包和奶酪，我们带着这些食物去树林里，在那里我们各自选择自己最喜欢的位置，丰盛地用餐。

My favorite seat was a smooth and broad stone , rising white and dry from the very middle of the beck , and only to be got at by wading through the water — a feat I accomplished barefoot . The stone was just broad enough to accommodate comfortably me and another girl — at that time my chosen comrade — one Mary Ann Wilson , a shrewd , observant personage , whose society I took pleasure in , partly because she was witty and original , and partly because she had a manner which set me at my ease . Some years older than I , she knew more of the world , and could tell me many things I liked to hear . With her my curiosity found gratification . To my faults , also , she gave ample indulgence , never imposing curb or rein on anything I said . She had a turn for narrative — I for analysis ; she liked to inform — I to question ; so we got on swimmingly together , deriving much entertainment , if not much improvement , from our mutual intercourse .

我最喜欢的座位是一块光滑而宽阔的石头，它从溪水中央露出来，又白又干，只有蹚过溪水才能坐上去--我是赤着脚坐上去的。这块石头足够宽敞，可以让我和另一个女孩--当时我选中的伙伴--玛丽-安-威尔逊（Mary Ann Wilson）舒适地坐着，她是一个精明、善于观察的人，我很喜欢和她交往，一方面是因为她机智、新颖，另一方面是因为她的举止让我很自在。她比我年长几岁，对这个世界了解更多，能告诉我许多我喜欢听的事情。有了她，我的好奇心得到了满足。对于我的缺点，她也给予了充分的宽容，从不对我说的话加以限制或约束。她喜欢叙述，我则喜欢分析；她喜欢告知，我则喜欢提问；因此，我们相处得很愉快，从我们的相互交往中获得了很多娱乐，即使不是很大的进步。

And where , meantime , was Helen Burns ? Why did I not spend these sweet days of liberty with her ? Had I forgotten her ? or was I so worthless as to have grown tired of her pure society ? Surely the Mary Ann Wilson I have mentioned was inferior to my first acquaintance ; she could only tell me amusing stories , and reciprocate any racy and pungent gossip I chose to indulge in ; while , if I have spoken truth of Helen , she was qualified to give those who enjoyed the privilege of her converse a taste of far higher things .

与此同时，海伦-伯恩斯在哪里呢？为什么我没有和她一起度过这美好的自由时光呢？是我忘了她，还是我太没出息，已经厌倦了她纯洁的社交生活？我提到的玛丽-安-威尔逊肯定不如我的初识；她只能给我讲一些有趣的故事，对我沉溺于其中的任何饶舌和辛辣的流言蜚语都能给予回应；而如果我对海伦说的是实话，她有资格让那些有幸与她交谈的人品尝到更高尚的东西。

True , reader , and I knew and felt this ; and though I am a defective being , with many faults and few redeeming points , yet I never tired of Helen Burns , nor ever ceased to cherish for her a sentiment of attachment as strong , tender , and respectful as any that ever animated my heart . How could it be otherwise , when Helen , at all times , and under all circumstances , evinced for me a quiet and faithful friendship , which ill - humor never soured nor irritation ever troubled ? But Helen was ill at present ; for some weeks she had been removed from my sight to I knew not what room , up stairs . She was not , I was told , in the hospital portion of the house , with the fever patients ; for her complaint was consumption , not typhus ; and by consumption I , in my ignorance , understood something mild , which time and care would be sure to alleviate .

的确，读者，我知道并感受到了这一点；虽然我是一个有缺陷的人，缺点很多，可取之处很少，但我从未厌倦过海伦-伯恩斯，也从未停止过对她的依恋之情，这种感情强烈、温柔、令人尊敬，不亚于任何曾使我心动的感情。在任何时候、任何情况下，海伦都对我表现出一种宁静而忠实的友谊，这种友谊既不会因为我的恶意而变质，也不会因为我的恼怒而烦恼，怎么可能不是这样呢？但是，海伦现在病了；几个星期以来，她从我的视线中消失了，不知去了楼上哪个房间。有人告诉我，她并不是和发烧病人一起住在医院里；因为她得的是肺痨，而不是斑疹伤寒；我无知地认为，肺痨是一种轻微的病，经过一段时间的治疗和护理，一定会好起来的。

I was confirmed in this idea by the fact of her once or twice coming down stairs , on very warm sunny afternoons , and being taken by Miss Temple into the garden ; but , on these occasions , I was not allowed to go and speak to her ; I only saw her from the school - room window , and then not distinctly , for she was much wrapped up , and sat at a distance under the verandah .

有一两次，在阳光非常暖和的下午，她下楼来，坦普尔小姐把她带到花园里，这证实了我的想法；但在这些场合，我都不能去和她说话；我只能从教室的窗户里看到她，而且看不太清楚，因为她裹得很严实，远远地坐在阳台下。

One evening , in the beginning of June , I had stayed out very late with Mary Ann in the wood ; we had , as usual separated ourselves from the others , and had wandered far — so far that we lost our way , and had to ask it at a lonely cottage , where a man and woman lived , who looked after a herd of half - wild swine that fed on the mast in the wood . When we got back , it was after moonrise ; a pony , which we knew to be the surgeon ‘ s , was standing at the garden - door . Mary Ann remarked that she supposed some one must be very ill , as Mr . Bates had been sent for at that time of the evening . She went into the house . I stayed behind a few minutes to plant in my garden a handful of roots I had dug up in the forest , and which I feared would wither if I left them till morning . This done , I lingered yet a little longer ; the flowers smelled so sweet as the dew fell ; it was such a pleasant evening , so serene , so warm ; the still glowing west promised so fairly another fine day on the morrow ; the moon rose with such majesty in the grave east . I was noting these things , and enjoying them as a child might , when it entered my mind , as it had never done before :

六月初的一个傍晚，我和玛丽-安在树林里待到很晚，我们像往常一样和其他人分开，走了很远，以至于迷了路，不得不到一间孤零零的小屋去问路，那里住着一男一女，他们照看着一群以树林里的桅杆为食的半野生猪。我们回来的时候，天已经蒙蒙亮了；一匹小马站在花园门口，我们知道那是外科医生的马。玛丽-安说，她想一定是有人病得很重，因为贝茨先生晚上这个时候才被派来。她进屋去了。我留了几分钟，在花园里种了一把我在森林里挖的树根，我担心如果把它们留到天亮就会枯萎。做完这些，我又逗留了一会儿；露水落下时，花儿散发着甜美的香气；这是一个多么惬意的夜晚，多么宁静，多么温暖；西边依然闪耀着光芒，预示着明天又是美好的一天；月亮在严肃的东方升起，显得格外庄严。我像个孩子一样注视着这一切，享受着这一切，这时，我的脑海中浮现出一个从未有过的念头：

“ How sad to be lying now on a sick bed , and to be in danger of dying ! This world is pleasant ; it would be dreary to be called from it , and to have to go — who knows where ?”

"现在躺在病床上，面临死亡的危险，这是多么可悲啊！这个世界是令人愉悦的；如果被召唤离开这个世界，而且不得不去--谁知道会去哪里--那将是多么沉闷啊！"

And then my mind made its first earnest effort to comprehend what had been infused into it concerning heaven and hell , and for the first time it recoiled , baffled ; and , for the first time , glancing behind , on each side , and before it , it saw all round an unfathomed gulf ; it felt the one point where it stood — the present ; all the rest was formless cloud and vacant depth ; and it shuddered at the thought of tottering and plunging amid that chaos . While pondering this new idea , I heard the front door open . Mr . Bates came out , and with him was a nurse . After she had seen him mount his horse and depart , she was about to close the door , but I ran up to her .

The nurse closed the front door ; I went in by the side entrance , which led to the school - room . I was just in time ; it was nine o ‘ clock , and Miss Miller was calling the pupils to go to bed .

It might be two hours later , probably near eleven , when I — not having been able to fall asleep , and deeming , from the perfect silence of the dormitory , that my companions were all wrapped in profound repose — rose softly , put on my frock over my night - dress , and , without shoes , crept from the apartment , and set off in quest of Miss Temple ’ s room . It was quite at the other end of the house ; but I knew my way ; and the light of the unclouded summer moon , entering here and there at passage windows , enabled me to find it without difficulty . An odor of camphor and burned vinegar warned me when I came near the fever - room ; and I passed its door quickly, fearful lest the nurse , who sat up all night , should hear me . I dreaded being discovered and sent back ; for I must see Helen — I must embrace her before she died — I must give her one last kiss , exchange with her one last word .

可能过了两个小时，大概快十一点的时候，我还没来得及入睡，又觉得宿舍里静悄悄的，我的同伴们都沉浸在浓浓的睡意中，于是我轻轻地爬起来，在睡衣外面套上连衣裙，鞋也没穿，蹑手蹑脚地走出房间，去找坦普尔小姐的房间。她的房间在房子的另一头，但我认得路，而且夏日的月光不时从通道的窗户射进来，让我不费吹灰之力就找到了她的房间。当我走近发烧室时，一股樟脑和烧醋的气味提醒着我；我快速地穿过房门，生怕整夜未眠的护士听到我的声音。我害怕被发现，害怕被送回去；因为我一定要见到海伦，一定要在她死之前拥抱她，一定要给她最后一个吻，和她说最后一句话。

Having descended a stair - case , traversed a portion of the house below , and succeeded in opening and shutting , without noise , two doors , I reached another flight of steps ; these I mounted , and then just opposite to me was Miss Temple ’ s room . A light shone through the key - hole , and from under the door . A profound stillness pervaded the vicinity . Coming near , I found the door slightly ajar ; probably to admit some fresh air into the close abode of sickness . Indisposed to hesitate , and full of impatient impulses — soul and senses quivering with keen throes — I put it back and looked in . My eyes sought Helen , and feared to find death .

我走下楼梯，穿过房子下面的一部分，成功地无声无息地开关了两扇门，然后又到了另一段台阶；我登上台阶，对面就是坦普尔小姐的房间。一束光透过钥匙孔和门缝照了进来。周围一片寂静。我走近一看，发现房门微微虚掩着，可能是为了让新鲜空气进入这间密闭的病榻。我不禁犹豫起来，内心充满了迫不及待的冲动--灵魂和感官都在剧烈地颤抖--我把门放了回去，向里面望去。我的目光在寻找海伦，却害怕找到死亡。

Close by Miss Temple ’ s bed , and half covered with its white curtains , there stood a little crib . I saw the outline of a form under the clothes , but the face was hid by the hangings . The nurse I had spoken to in the garden sat in an easy - chair , asleep ; an unsnuffed candle burned dimly on the table . Miss Temple was not to be seen ; I knew afterward that she had been called to a delirious patient in the fever - room . I advanced ; then paused by the crib - side . My hand was on the curtain , but I preferred speaking before I withdrew it . I still recoiled at the dread of seeing a corpse .

坦普尔小姐的床边，半掩着白色的窗帘，放着一张小婴儿床。我看到衣服下面有一个人的轮廓，但脸被窗帘遮住了。在花园里和我说过话的护士坐在安乐椅上睡着了；桌子上一支未熄灭的蜡烛昏暗地燃烧着。坦普尔小姐不见了；事后我才知道，她被叫去看发热室里一个神志不清的病人了。我向前走去，然后在婴儿床边停了下来。我的手搭在窗帘上，但我宁愿先说几句话再收回来。我仍然害怕看到一具尸体。

“ Helen !” I whispered softly ; “ are you awake ?”

"海伦！"我轻声说："你醒了吗？"

She stirred , herself put back the curtain , and I saw her face , pale , wasted , but quite composed . She looked so little changed that my fear was instantly dissipated .

她动了动，自己把帘子放了回去，我看到了她的脸，苍白、消瘦，但很镇定。她看起来变化不大，我的恐惧立刻烟消云散。

“ Can it be you , Jane ?” she asked , in her own gentle voice .

"是你吗，简？"她用自己温柔的声音问道。

“ Oh !” I thought , “ she is not going to die ; they are mistaken ; she could not speak and look so calmly if she were .”

"哦！"我想，"她不会死的，他们弄错了，如果她真的死了，她不可能这么平静地说话和看人。"

I got on to her crib and kissed her . Her forehead was cold , and her cheek both cold and thin ; and so were her hand and wrist ; but she smiled as of old .

我坐到她的婴儿床上，亲吻她。她的额头冰凉，脸颊又冷又瘦；她的手和手腕也是如此；但她还是像以前一样微笑着。

“ Why are you come here , Jane ? It is past eleven o ‘ clock ; I heard it strike some minutes since .”

"你为什么来这里，简？已经十一点多了，我听到钟声敲了几分钟。

“ I came to see you , Helen; I heard you were very ill , and I could not sleep till I had spoken to you .”

"我是来看你的，海伦；我听说你病得很重，不和你谈谈我睡不着。"

“ You came to bid me good - by , then ; you are just in time , probably .”

"那你是来向我告别的；你来得正是时候，也许吧。"

“ Are you going somewhere , Helen ? Are you going home ?”

"你要去哪儿吗，海伦？你要回家吗？"

“ Yes ; to my long homeaz — my last home .”

"是的，回我久别的家，我最后的家"

“ No , no , Helen .” I stopped , distressed . While I tried to devour my tears , a fit of coughing seized Helen ; it did not , however , wake the nurse ; when it was over , she lay some minutes exhausted ; then she whispered —

"不，不，海伦"我停下了脚步，心痛极了。在我努力咽下眼泪的时候，海伦突然咳嗽起来，不过并没有吵醒护士；咳嗽结束后，她筋疲力尽地躺了几分钟；然后她轻声说-

“ Jane , your little feet are bare ; lie down and cover yourself with my quilt .”

"简，你的小脚光着呢；躺下，盖上我的被子。"

I did so ; she put her arm over me , and I nestled close to her . After a long silence , she resumed ; still whispering —

我照做了；她把胳膊搭在我身上，我紧紧地依偎着她。沉默了许久，她又继续说道；仍然是轻声细语-

“ I am very happy , Jane ; and when you hear that I am dead you must be sure and not grieve ; there is nothing to grieve about . We all must die one day , and the illness which is removing me is not painful ; it is gentle and gradual ; my mind is at rest . I leave no one to regret me much ; I have only a father ; and he is lately married , and will not miss me . By dying young I shall escape great sufferings . I had not qualities or talents to make my way very well in the world ; I should have been continually at fault .”

"我很幸福，简；当你听说我死了，你一定要相信，不要悲伤；没有什么好悲伤的。我们都有死去的一天，而我的病并不痛苦，它是温和而渐进的，我的心已经安息。我没有给任何人留下遗憾；我只有一个父亲；他刚刚结婚，不会想念我。英年早逝，我将摆脱巨大的痛苦。我没有在世界上闯荡的品质和才能，我应该一直都在犯错。

“But where are you going to , Helen ? Can you see ? Do you know ?”

"可你要去哪儿呢，海伦？你看得见吗？你知道吗？"

“ I believe ; I have faith ; I am going to God .”

"我相信，我有信仰，我要去见上帝。

“ Where is God ? What is God ?”

"上帝在哪里？上帝是什么？"

“ My Maker and yours ; who will never destroy what he created . I rely implicitly on his power , and confide wholly in his goodness ; I count the hours till that eventful one arrives which shall restore me to him , reveal him to me .”

"我的造物主，也是你的造物主；他永远不会毁灭他所创造的一切。我完全信赖他的力量，完全相信他的仁慈；我数着时间，直到那个重要的时刻到来，让我回到他身边，让他向我显现。"

“ You are sure , then , Helen , that there is such a place as heaven ; and that our souls can get to it when we die ?”

"那么，海伦，你确信有天堂这样一个地方；我们死后，灵魂可以到达那里？"

“ I am sure there is a future state ; I believe God is good ; I can resign my immortal part to him without any misgiving . God is my father ; God is my friend ; I love him ; I believe he loves me .”

"我确信有一个未来的国度；我相信上帝是善良的；我可以把我不朽的部分交给他，而不会有任何疑虑。上帝是我的父亲，上帝是我的朋友，我爱他，我相信他也爱我。

“And shall I see you again , Helen , when I die ?”

"我死后还能再见到你吗 海伦"

“ You will come to the same region of happiness ; be received by the same mighty , universal Parent , no doubt , dear Jane .”

"你会来到同一个幸福的地方；毫无疑问，亲爱的简，你会被同一个伟大的、普世的父母所接纳。"

Again I questioned ; but this time only in thought . “ Where is that region ? Does it exist ?” And I clasped my arms closer round Helen ; she seemed dearer to me than ever ; I felt as if I could not let her go ; I lay with my face hidden on her neck . Presently she said , in the sweetest tone —

我再次提出疑问，但这次只是在思考。"那个地方在哪里？它存在吗？我紧紧地抱住了海伦；她似乎比以往任何时候都更亲近我；我仿佛舍不得让她离开；我把脸伏在她的脖子上。这时，她用最甜美的语调说-

“ How comfortable I am ! That last fit of coughing has tired me a little ; I feel as if I could sleep ; but don ’ t leave me , Jane ; I like to have you near me .”

"我舒服极了！上次的咳嗽让我有点累了，我觉得我好像可以睡着了；但是不要离开我，简；我喜欢有你在我身边。

“ I ’ ll stay with you , dear Helen ; no one shall take me away .”

"我会陪着你的，亲爱的海伦，谁也不能把我带走。"

“ Are you warm , darling ?”

"你暖和吗，亲爱的？

“ Yes .”

暖和

“ Good - night , Jane .”

"晚安 简

“ Good - night , Helen .”

"晚安 海伦

She kissed me , and I her ; and we both soon slumbered .

她吻了我，我也吻了她 我们很快都睡着了

When I awoke it was day ; an unusual movement roused me ; I looked up ; I was in somebody ’ s arms ; the nurse held me ; she was carrying me through the passage back to the dormitory . I was not reprimanded for leaving my bed ; people had something else to think about . No explanation was afforded then to my many questions ; but a day or two afterward I learned that Miss Temple , on returning to her own room at dawn , had found me laid in the little crib ; my face against Helen Burns ’ shoulder , my arms round her neck . I was asleep , and Helen was — dead .

当我醒来的时候已经是白天了，一个不寻常的动静把我惊醒了，我抬头一看，我在别人的怀里，护士抱着我，她正把我从通道抱回宿舍。我没有因为离开病床而受到训斥，因为人们还有别的事情要做。当时，我的许多问题都没有得到解释；但一两天后，我得知坦普尔小姐在黎明时分回到她自己的房间时，发现我躺在小床上；我的脸靠在海伦-伯恩斯的肩膀上，我的胳膊搂着她的脖子。我睡着了，而海伦却死了。

Her grave is in Brocklebridge church - yard ; for fifteen years after her death it was only covered by a grassy mound ; but now a gray marble tablet marks the spot , inscribed with her name , and the word “ Resurgam .”

她的墓地在布洛克布里奇教堂的院子里；在她死后的十五年里，墓地一直被一个草堆覆盖着；但现在，一块灰色的大理石碑刻在那里，上面刻着她的名字和 "复活 "两个字。
