“ No — Ad è le is not answerable for either her mother ’ s faults or yours ; I have a regard for her , and now that I know she is , in a sense , parentless — forsaken by her mother and disowned by you , sir — I shall cling closer to her than before . How could I possibly prefer the spoiled pet of a wealthy family , who would hate her governess as a nuisance , to a lonely little orphan , who leans towards her as a friend ?”

"不，阿黛尔不必为她母亲和您的过错负责；我对她是有感情的，现在我知道她在某种意义上是无父无母的--被她母亲抛弃，又被您嫌弃，先生--我会比以前更亲近她。我怎么可能宁愿要一个有钱人家宠坏了的宠物呢？""她讨厌家庭教师，把她当成讨厌鬼。""我怎么可能宁愿要一个孤独的小孤儿呢？""她把家庭教师当成朋友。""

“ Oh , that is the light in which you view it ! Well , I must go in now , and you too ; it darkens .”

"哦，你就是这么想的！好了，我得进去了，你也进去吧，天黑了"。

But I stayed out a few minutes longer with Adè le and Pilot — ran a race with her , and played a game of battledore and shuttlecock . When we went in and I had removed her bonnet and coat , I took her on my knee , kept her there an hour , allowing her to prattle as she liked , not rebuking even some little freedoms and trivialities into which she was apt to stray when much noticed , and which betrayed in her a superficiality of character , inherited probably from her mother , hardly congenial to an English mind . Still she had her merits ; and I was disposed to appreciate all that was good in her to the utmost . I sought in her countenance and features a likeness to Mr . Rochester , but found none ; no trait , no turn of expression , announced relationship . It was a pity ; if she could but have been proved to resemble him , he would have thought more of her .

但我还是和阿黛尔、皮洛特在外面多呆了几分钟--和她赛跑，踢毽子。我们进屋后，我脱下她的帽子和外套，把她抱在膝上，让她在那里呆了一个小时，任她絮絮叨叨，甚至连一些小自由和琐事也不斥责，因为她一受到关注就容易陷入这些小自由和琐事中，这暴露了她性格中的肤浅，可能是从她母亲那里遗传来的，很难适应英国人的思维。尽管如此，她还是有她的优点；我愿意尽情欣赏她身上的一切优点。我在她的面容和特征中寻找与罗切斯特先生的相似之处，但一无所获；她的任何特征、任何表情都不能说明与罗切斯特先生的关系。真可惜，如果能證明她與羅徹斯特先生相似，羅徹斯特先生一定會更喜歡她。

It was not till after I had withdrawn to my own chamber for the night , that I steadily reviewed the tale Mr . Rochester had told me . As he had said , there was probably nothing at all extraordinary in the substance of the narrative itself ; a wealthy English -man’ s passion for a French dancer , and her treachery to him , were every - day matters enough , no doubt , in society ; but there was something decidedly strange in the paroxysm of emotion which had suddenly seized him , when he was in the act of expressing the present contentment of his mood , and his newly - revived pleasure in the old hall and its environs . I meditated wonderingly on this incident ; but gradually quitting it , as I found it for the present inexplicable , I turned to the consideration of my master ’ s manner to myself . The confidence he had thought fit to repose in me seemed a tribute to my discretion ; I regarded and accepted it as such . His deportment had now for some weeks been more uniform toward me than at first . I never seemed in his way ; he did not take fits of chilling hauteur ; when he met me unexpectedly the encounter seemed welcome ; he had always a word and sometimes a smile for me ; when summoned by formal invitation to his presence , I was honored by a cordiality of reception that made me feel I really possessed the power to amuse him , and that these evening conferences were sought as much for his pleasure as for my benefit .

直到晚上我回到自己的房間後，我才仔細回想羅徹斯特先生告訴我的故事。正如他所言，故事本身并没有什么特别之处；一个富有的英国人对一个法国舞女动了情，而她又背叛了他，这在社会上无疑是家常便饭；但是，当他正在表达他目前的满足心情，以及他对旧厅堂及其周围环境刚刚恢复的愉悦时，突然发作的情绪却有些奇怪。我纳闷地思索着这件事，但渐渐地，我发现这件事暂时无法解释，便放弃了，转而思考主人对我的态度。他对我的信任似乎是对我的谨慎的赞美，我认为并接受了这一点。几个星期以来，他对我的态度比最初更加一致了。我似乎从来没有妨碍过他；他也不那么冷酷无情了；当他与我不期而遇时，他似乎很欢迎我；他对我总是有一句话，有时还有一个微笑；当他正式邀请我去见他时，我受到了他的热情接待，这使我感到我真的有能力逗他开心，而这些晚上的会面既是为了我的利益，也是为了他的快乐。

I , indeed , talked comparatively little ; but I heard him talk with relish . It was his nature to be communicative ; he liked to open to a mind unacquainted with the world , glimpses of its scenes and ways ( I do not mean its corrupt scenes and wicked ways , but such as derived their interest from the great scale on which they were acted , the strange novelty by which they were characterized ); and I had a keen delight in receiving the new ideas he offered , in imagining the new pictures he portrayed , and following him in thought through the new regions he disclosed , never startled or troubled by one noxious allusion .

事实上，我的话相对较少，但我听到他说得津津有味。他的天性就是喜欢交流；他喜欢向一个不了解世界的人展示世界的景象和方式（我指的不是腐朽的景象和邪恶的方式，而是那些因规模宏大、新奇奇特而引人入胜的景象和方式）；我非常乐于接受他提出的新观点，想象他描绘的新画面，跟随他的思路去探索他所揭示的新领域，从未被任何恶毒的暗示所惊吓或困扰。

The ease of his manner freed me from painful restraint ; the friendly frankness , as correct as cordial , with which he treated me , drew me to him . I felt , at times , as if he were my relation , rather than my master ; yet he was imperious sometimes still , but I did not mind that — I saw it was his way . So happy , so gratified , did I become with this new interest added to life , that I ceased to pine after kindred . My thin - crescent - destiny seemed to enlarge ; the blanks of existence were filled up ; my bodily health improved ; I gathered flesh and strength .

他从容不迫的态度使我摆脱了痛苦的束缚；他待我友好坦率，既正确又亲切，吸引着我。有时，我觉得他好像是我的亲戚，而不是我的主人；但他有时还是很专横，但我并不介意，因为我知道这是他的方式。生活增添了新的情趣，我变得如此快乐，如此欣慰，以至于我不再渴求亲情。我單薄的新月形命運似乎擴大了，生活的空白被填滿了，我的身體健康改善了，我有了肉體和力量。

And was Mr . Rochester now ugly in my eyes ? No , reader . Gratitude , and many associations , all pleasurable and genial , made his face the object I best liked to see . His presence in a room was more cheering than the brightest fire . Yet I had not forgotten his faults ; indeed , I could not , for he brought them frequently before me . He was proud , sardonic , harsh to inferiority of every description . In my secret soul I knew that his great kindness to me was balanced by unjust severity to others . He was moody , too , unaccountably so . I more than once , when sent for to read to him , found him sitting in his library alone , with his head bent on his folded arms ; and when he looked up , a morose , almost a malignant , scowl blackened his features . But I believe that his moodiness , his harshness , and his former faults of morality ( I say former , for now he seemed corrected of them ) had their source in some cruel cross of fate . I believed he was naturally a man of better tendencies , higher principles , and purer tastes than such as circumstances had developed , education instilled , or destiny encouraged . I thought there were excellent materials in him , though , for the present , they hung together somewhat spoiled and tangled . I cannot deny that I grieved for his grief , whatever that was , and would have given much to assuage it .

在我眼中，羅徹斯特先生是醜陋的嗎？不，讀者。感激之情，以及許多令人愉悅、和煦的聯想，使他的臉成為我最喜歡看的對象。他的出現比最明亮的火光更令人心曠神怡。然而，我并没有忘记他的缺点；事实上，我无法忘记，因为他经常把这些缺点带到我面前。他骄傲、尖酸刻薄，对各种劣根性都很苛刻。在我隐秘的灵魂深处，我知道他对我的仁慈是通过对他人的不公正的严厉来平衡的。他也喜怒无常，令人费解。有一次，我被派去给他读书，发现他一个人坐在书房里，头歪在交叠的双臂上；当他抬起头来时，一种忧郁的、几乎是恶毒的蔑视使他的面容变得阴沉。但我相信，他的喜怒无常，他的苛刻，以及他以前的道德过失（我说以前，是因为现在他似乎改正了这些过失），都是源于命运的某种残酷折磨。我相信他天生就具有比环境培养、教育灌输或命运怂恿的人更好的倾向、更高的原则和更纯粹的品味。我认为他身上有很多优秀的品质，尽管就目前而言，这些品质有些被破坏了，有些纠结在一起。我不能否认，我为他的悲痛而悲伤，不管那是什么，我愿意付出很多去抚平他的悲痛。

Though I had now extinguished my candle and was laid down in bed , I could not sleep , for thinking of his look when he paused in the avenue , and told how his destiny had risen up before him and dared him to be happy at Thornfield .

虽然我已经熄灭了蜡烛，躺在床上，但我无法入睡，因为我想到了他在林荫道上停顿时的神情，他讲述了他的命运是如何在他面前升起，让他敢于在桑菲尔德幸福地生活下去。

“ Why not ?” I asked myself . “ What alienates him from the house ? Will he leave it again soon ? Mrs . Fairfax said he seldom stayed here longer than a fortnight at a time , and he has now been resident eight weeks . If he does go the change will be doleful . Suppose he should be absent , spring , summer , and autumn , how joyless sunshine and fine days will seem !”

"为什么不呢？"我问自己"是什么让他疏远了这所房子？他会很快再离开吗？费尔法克斯太太说过，他每次在这里住的时间很少超过两星期，而他现在已经住了八个星期了。如果他真的走了，那变化可就大了。如果他不在，春天、夏天和秋天，阳光和晴朗的日子会显得多么无趣啊！"

I hardly know whether I had slept or not after this musing ; at any rate I started wide awake on hearing a vague murmur , peculiar and lugubrious , which sounded , I thought , just above me . I wished I had kept my candle burning . The night was drearily dark ; my spirits were depressed . I rose and sat up in bed , listening . The sound was hushed .

我几乎不知道自己在胡思乱想之后是否睡着了；总之，当我听到一阵隐约的杂音时，我就惊醒了，我想，这声音就在我的头顶上，既奇特又令人讨厌。我真希望我的蜡烛还在燃烧。夜色沉沉，我的精神萎靡不振。我站起身，坐在床上倾听。声音消失了。

I tried again to sleep ; but my heart beat anxiously ; my inward tranquility was broken . The clock , far down in the hall , struck two . Just then it seemed my chamber - door was touched , as if fingers had swept the panels in groping a way along the dark gallery outside . I said , “ Who is there ?” Nothing answered . I was chilled with fear .

我再次试着入睡，但我的心焦急地跳动着，内心的宁静被打破了。大厅里的大钟敲响了两下。就在这时，我的房门似乎被人碰了一下，好像有人用手指扫过门板，沿着外面漆黑的长廊摸索着前进。我问："谁在那儿？"没有人回答。我吓得浑身发抖。

All at once I remembered that it might be Pilot ; who , when the kitchen - door chanced to be left open , not unfrequently found his way up to the threshold of Mr . Rochester ’ s chamber . I had seen him lying there myself in the mornings . The idea calmed me somewhat ; I laid down . Silence composes the nerves , and as an unbroken hush now reigned again through the whole house , I began to feel the return of slumber . But it was not fated that I should sleep that night . A dream had scarcely approached my ear , when it fled affrighted , scared by a marrow - freezing incident enough .

我突然想起可能是皮洛特，他經常在廚房門打開的時候，跑到羅徹斯特先生房間的門口。我曾在早晨看到他躺在那里。這個想法讓我稍稍平靜下來，我躺了下來。寧靜能使人神清氣爽，當整個屋子再次籠罩在一片寧靜中時，我開始感到睡意漸濃。但那天晚上我注定睡不着。一个梦还没靠近我的耳朵，它就被一个足以冻彻骨髓的事件吓得惊慌失措地逃走了。

This was a demoniac laugh ; low , suppressed , and deep , muttered , as it seemed , at the very key - hole of my chamber - door . The head of my bed was near the door , and I thought , at first , the goblin - laughter stood at my bed - side , or , rather , crouched by my pillow . But I rose , looked round , and could see nothing ; while , as I still gazed , the unnatural sound was reiterated , and I knew it came from behind the panels . My first impulse was to rise and fasten the bolt ; my next , again to cry out , “ Who is there ?”

那是一阵恶魔般的笑声，低沉、压抑、深沉，似乎就在我房门的钥匙孔上喃喃自语。我的床头离门很近，起初我以为妖精的笑声就站在我的床边，或者说，就蹲在我的枕头边。但我站起身，环顾四周，却什么也看不见；当我还在凝视时，那不自然的声音又响了起来，我知道那声音来自门板后面。我的第一反应是起身把门闩拴上 接着又喊道："谁在那儿？"

Something gurgled and moaned . Ere long , steps retreated up the gallery toward the third story stair - case . A door had lately been made to shut in that stair - case ; I heard it open and close , and all was still .

有什么东西发出潺潺的呻吟声。没过多久，脚步声沿着长廊向三楼楼梯口退去。最近，楼梯间的一扇门被关上了；我听到了开门和关门的声音，一切都静止了。

“ Was that Grace Poole ? and is she possessed with a devil ?” thought I . Impossible now to remain longer by myself , I must go to Mrs . Fairfax . I hurried on my frock and a shawl . I withdrew the bolt and opened the door with a trembling hand . There was a candle burning just outside , left on the matting in the gallery . I was surprised at this circumstance , but still more was I amazed to perceive the air quite dim , as if filled with smoke ; and , while looking to the right hand and left , to find whence these blue wreaths issued , I became further aware of a strong smell of burning .

我心想："那是格蕾丝-普尔吗？""她是不是被魔鬼附身了？"我想。我赶紧穿上连衣裙，披上披肩。我收回门闩，用颤抖的手打开了门。门外有一支蜡烛在燃烧，就放在长廊的垫子上。我对这一情况感到惊奇，但更让我惊奇的是，我发现空气非常昏暗，好像充满了烟雾；当我左顾右盼，想找到这些蓝色花环是从哪里发出来的时候，我又闻到了一股浓烈的烧焦味。

Something creaked ; it was a door ajar ; and that door was Mr . Rochester ‘ s , and the smoke rushed in a cloud from thence . I thought no more of Mrs . Fairfax ; I thought no more of Grace Poole or the laugh . In an instant I was within the chamber . Tongues of flame darted round the bed ; the curtains were on fire . In the midst of blaze and vapor , Mr . Rochester lay stretched motionless , in deep sleep .

有什么东西吱吱作响，原来是一扇门虚掩着，那扇门是罗切斯特先生的，烟雾从门缝里一冲而出。我不再想費爾法克斯太太，也不再想格蕾絲-普爾和那笑聲。瞬間，我已經進了房間。火舌在床上飞舞，窗帘也着了火。在火光和水汽中，羅徹斯特先生一動不動地躺在床上，沉沉睡去。

“ Wake ! wake !” I cried . I shook him , but he only murmured and turned ; the smoke had stupefied him . Not a moment could be lost ; the very sheets were kindling . I rushed to his basin and ewer ; fortunately , one was wide and the other deep , and both were filled with water . I heaved them up , deluged the bed and its occupant , flew back to my own room , brought my own water - jug , baptized the couch afresh , and by God ’ s aid , succeeded in extinguishing the flames which were devouring it .

"醒醒，醒醒！"我喊道。我搖晃著他，但他只是喃喃地轉過身；濃煙使他昏昏欲睡。一刻也不能耽搁，床单都快烧着了。我冲向他的脸盆和水壶；幸运的是，一个很宽，另一个很深，都装满了水。我把它们举起来，浇灌了床和床上的人，飞快地跑回自己的房间，拿来自己的水壶，重新给沙发洗了个澡，在上帝的帮助下，成功地扑灭了正在吞噬它的火焰。

The hiss of the quenched element , the breakage of a pitcher which I flung from my hand when I had emptied it , and , above all , the splash of the shower - bath I had liberally bestowed , roused Mr . Rochester at last . Though it was now dark , I knew he was awake ; because I heard him fulminating strange anathemas at finding himself lying in a pool of water .

熄灭的火苗发出嘶嘶声，我把水壶倒空后从手中甩出，水壶破了，最重要的是，我喷洒的水花终于唤醒了罗切斯特先生。虽然天已经黑了，但我知道他醒了，因为我听到他发现自己躺在一滩水里时，发出了奇怪的咒骂声。

“ Is there a flood ?” he cried .

"他喊道："發洪水了嗎？

“ No , sir ,” I answered ; “ but there has been a fire ; get up , do , you are quenched now ; I will fetch you a candle .”

"没有，先生，"我回答道，"但是着火了；起来吧，你现在已经熄灭了；我去给你拿根蜡烛。"

“ In the name of all the elves in Christendom , is that Jane Eyre ?” he demanded . “ What have you done with me , witch , sorceress ? Who is in the room besides you ? Have you plotted to drown me ?”

"以基督世界所有精灵的名义，那是简-爱吗？"他问道。"你对我做了什么，巫婆，女巫？房间里除了你还有谁？你是不是想淹死我？"

“ I will fetch you a candle , sir ; and in Heaven ’ s name , get up . Somebody has plotted something ; you cannot too soon find out who and what it is .”

"我去给你拿蜡烛，先生；看在上帝的份上，起来吧。有人在密谋什么事，你不能太快知道是谁，是什么。"

“ There — I am up now ; but at your peril you fetch a candle yet ; wait two minutes till I get into some dry garments , if any dry there be — yes , here is my dressing - gown , now run !”

"好了，我已经起来了；不过，你还是去拿蜡烛吧；等两分钟，等我穿上干衣服，如果有干衣服的话--是的，这是我的睡衣，快跑！"

I did run ; I brought the candle which still remained in the gallery . He took it from my hand , held it up , and surveyed the bed , all blackened and scorched , the sheets drenched , the carpet round swimming in water .

我真的跑了，我拿来了还留在长廊里的蜡烛。他从我手里接过蜡烛，举起来，打量着这张全被烧焦变黑的床，床单湿透了，地毯上到处都是水。

“ What is it ? and who did it ?” he asked .

"他问道："这是什么，是谁干的？

I briefly related to him what had transpired ; the strange laugh I had heard in the gallery ; the step ascending to the third story ; the smoke — the smell of fire which had conducted me to his room ; in what state I had found matters there , and how I had deluged him with all the water I could lay hands on .

我简要地向他讲述了事情的经过；我在长廊里听到的奇怪的笑声；登上三楼的台阶；浓烟--火的气味把我引到他的房间；我在那里发现的情况，以及我是如何用我能弄到的所有水浇他的。

He listened very gravely ; his face , as I went on , expressed more concern than astonishment ; he did not immediately speak when I had concluded .

他非常严肃地听着，当我继续说下去时，他脸上的关切多于惊讶；当我说完后，他没有立即说话。

“ Shall I call Mrs . Fairfax ?” I asked .

"要我给费尔法克斯太太打电话吗？我问道。

“ Mrs . Fairfax ? No — what the deuce would you call her for ? What can she do ? Let her sleep unmolested .”

"费尔法克斯太太？不，你叫她干什么？她能做什么？让她好好睡吧

“ Then I will fetch Leah , and wake John and his wife .”

"那我去叫莉亚，叫醒约翰和他妻子"

“ Not at all ; just be still . You have a shawl on ; if you are not warm enough , you may take my cloak yonder ; wrap it about you , and sit down in the arm - chair ; there — I will put it on . Now place your feet on the stool , to keep them out of the wet . I am going to leave you a few minutes . I shall take the candle . Remain where you are till I return ; be as still as a mouse . I must pay a visit to the third story . Don ’ t move , remember , or call any one .”

"不用了，别乱动。你披着披肩，如果不够暖和，可以把我的斗篷拿过来，裹在身上，然后坐在扶手椅上，我来给你披上。现在把你的脚放在凳子上，这样就不会淋湿了。我要离开你几分钟。我去拿蜡烛。在我回来之前不要动，要像老鼠一样安静。我得去三楼看看。不要动，记住，也不要叫任何人。

He went ; I watched the light withdraw . He passed up the gallery very softly , unclosed the stair - case door with as little noise as possible , shut it after him , and the last ray vanished . I was left in total darkness . I listened for some noise , but heard nothing . A very long time elapsed . I grew weary ; it was cold , in spite of the cloak ; and then I did not see the use of staying , as I was not to rouse the house . I was on the point of risking Mr . Rochester’ s displeasure by disobeying his orders , when the light once more gleamed dimly on the gallery - wall , and I heard his unshod feet tread the matting . “ I hope it is he ,” thought I , “ and not something worse .”

他走了，我目送着烛光消失。他轻手轻脚地走过长廊，尽可能小声地关上楼梯间的门，然后关上，最后一束光也消失了。我陷入了一片黑暗之中。我倾听着一些声音，但什么也没听到。时间过去了很久。我开始感到疲倦；尽管披着斗篷，天气还是很冷；而且我不认为再呆下去有什么用处，因为我不能惊动房子里的人。我正想冒着罗切斯特先生不悦的危险，违抗他的命令，这时长廊墙壁上再次闪出微弱的光，我听到他未穿鞋的脚踩在垫子上的声音。"我希望是他，"我想，"而不是更糟的东西。"

He reentered , pale and very gloomy . “ I have found it all out ,” said he , setting his candle down on the wash - stand ; “ it is as I thought .”

他又进来了，脸色苍白，神情阴郁。"我都查清楚了，"他把蜡烛放在洗脸台上说，"和我想的一样。"

“ How , sir ?”

"怎么发现的，先生？"

He made no reply , but stood with his arms folded , looking on the ground . At the end of a few minutes he inquired , in rather a peculiar tone —

他没有回答，只是抱着胳膊站在那里，看着地面。几分钟后，他用一种奇怪的语气问道

“ I forget whether you said you saw anything when you opened your chamber - door .”

"我忘了你是否说过，当你打开房门时看到了什么

“ No , sir , only the candlestick on the ground .”

"没有，先生，只有地上的烛台。"

“ But you heard an odd laugh ? You have heard that laugh before I should think , or something like it ?”

"但是你听到了奇怪的笑声？我想你以前听过这种笑声 或者类似的声音吧？"

“ Yes , sir ; there is a woman who sews here , called Grace Poole — she laughs in that way . She is a singular person .”

"是的，先生，有一个在这里缝衣服的女人，叫格蕾丝-普尔，她就是这样笑的。她是个奇特的人。"

‘ Just so . Grace Poole ; you have guessed it . She is , as you say , singular — very . Well , I shall reflect on the subject . Meantime , I am glad that you are the only person , besides myself , acquainted with the precise details of tonight ’ s incident . You are no talking fool ; say nothing about it . I will account for this state of affairs ” ( pointing to the bed ); ” and now return to your own room . I shall do very well on the sofa in the library for the rest of the night . It is near four ; in two hours the servants will be up .”

"正是如此。格蕾丝-普尔，你已经猜到了。正如你所说，她是个奇特的人，非常奇特。好吧，我会考虑这个问题的。同时，我很高兴，除了我之外，你是唯一了解今晚事件细节的人。你又不是傻瓜，什么都不要说。我会解释这种状况的"（指了指床），"现在回你自己的房间去吧。我想在书房的沙发上好好睡一觉。现在快四点了，再过两个小时仆人们就要起床了。

“ Good - night , then , sir ,” said I , departing .

"那晚安了，先生。"我说着离开了。

He seemed surprised — very inconsistently so , as he had just told me to go .

他似乎很惊讶--很不协调地惊讶，因为他刚叫我走。

“ What !” he exclaimed , “ are you quitting me already ; and in that way ?”

"什么！"他惊呼道，"你就这样离开我？"

“ You said I might go , sir .”

"你说我可以走了，先生"

“ But not without taking leave ; not without a word or two of acknowledgment and good will ; not , in short , in that brief , dry fashion . Why , you have saved my life ! snatched me from a horrible and excruciating death ! and you walk past me as if we were mutual strangers ! At least shake hands .”

"但不是不辞而别，不是不说一两句感谢和善意的话，总之，不是以那种简短、干巴巴的方式。为什么，你救了我的命，把我从可怕而痛苦的死亡中解救出来！而你从我身边走过，好像我们互不相识！至少握个手吧。

He held out his hand ; I gave him mine ; he took it first in one , then in both his own .

他伸出手，我也伸出手，他先用一只手握住我的手，然后用双手握住我的手。

“ You have saved my life ; I have a pleasure in owing you so immense a debt . I cannot say more . Nothing else that has being would have been tolerable to me in the character of creditor for such an obligation ; but you , it is different — I feel your benefits no burden , Jane .”

"你救了我的命，我很高兴欠你这么多。我不能再说什么了。作为债权人，我不能容忍其他任何东西承担这样的义务，但你不同，我觉得你的恩惠不是负担，简。

He paused ; gazed at me ; words almost visible trembled on his lips — but his voice was checked .

他停顿了一下，凝视着我，嘴唇上几乎可以看见颤抖的话语，但他的声音被抑制住了

“ Good - night again , sir . There is no debt , benefit , burden , obligation , in the case .”

"晚安，先生。在这件事上，没有债务、利益、负担、义务"。

“ I knew ,” he continued , “ you would do me good in some way , at some time ; I saw it in your eyes when I first beheld you ; their expression and smile did not ”—( again he stopped )—“ did not ” ( he proceeded hastily ) “ strike delight to my very inmost heart so for nothing . People talk of natural sympathies ; I have heard of good genii ; there are grains of truth in the wildest fable . My cherished preserver , good - night !”

"我知道，"他继续说，"你会在某个时候以某种方式对我有所帮助；我第一次见到你时就从你的眼睛里看到了这一点；你的表情和微笑并没有"--（他又停住了）--"并没有"（他急忙接着说）"让我的内心如此欣喜。人们都说有天生的同情心；我听说过善良的精灵；在最荒诞的寓言中也有真理的颗粒。我珍爱的守护者，晚安！"

Strange energy was in his voice ; strange fire in his look .

他的声音里充满了奇异的活力；他的神情里充满了奇异的火焰。

“ I am glad I happened to be awake ,” I said ; and then I was going .

"我很高兴我碰巧醒着，"我说，然后我就走了。

“ What , you will go ?”

"怎么，你要走了？

“ I am cold , sir .”

"我很冷，先生

“ Cold ? Yes — and standing in a pool . Go , then , Jane ; go !” But he still retained my hand , and I could not free it . I bethought myself of an expedient .

"冷？是的，而且还站在水池里。走吧，简，走吧！"但他仍然握着我的手，我挣脱不开。我想到了一个权宜之计。

“ I think I hear Mrs . Fairfax move , sir ,” said I .

"我好像听到费尔法克斯太太的动静了，先生，"我说，

“ Well , leave me .” He relaxed his fingers , and I was gone .

"好吧，放开我。"他放松了手指，我就走了。

I regained my couch , but never thought of sleep . Till morning dawned I was tossed on a buoyant but unquiet sea , where billows of trouble rolled under surges of joy . I thought sometimes I saw beyond its wild waters a shore , sweet as the hills of Beulah ; 46 and now and then a freshening gale , wakened by hope , bore my spirit triumphantly toward the bourne ; but I could not reach it , even in fancy — a counteracting breeze blew off land , and continually drove me back . Sense would resist delirium ; judgment would warn passion . Too feverish to rest , I rose as soon as day dawned .

我重新躺在沙发上，但从未想过要睡觉直到天亮，我还在浮动但不平静的海面上辗转反侧，烦恼的波涛在欢乐的浪潮下翻滚。有时，我仿佛看到茫茫大海的彼岸，就像贝拉的山丘一样甜美；46 不时有一阵被希望唤醒的狂风，带着我的精神胜利地驶向彼岸；但是，即使在幻想中，我也无法到达那里--一阵阵微风从陆地吹来，不断地把我驱赶回去。理智会抵制谵妄，判断会警告激情。我发烧得无法休息，天一亮就爬了起来。
