Mr . Rochester had given me but one week ’ s leave of absence ; yet a month elapsed before I quitted Gateshead . I wished to leave immediately after the funeral , but Georgiana entreated me to stay till she could get off to London — whither she was now at last invited by her uncle , Mr . Gibson , who had come down to direct his sister ’ s interment , and settle the family affairs . Georgiana said she dreaded being left alone with Eliza ; from her she got neither sympathy in her dejection , support in her fears , nor aid in her preparations ; so I bore with her feeble - minded quailings and selfish lamentations as well as I could , and did my best in sewing for her and packing her dresses . It is true , that while I worked , she would idle ; and I thought to myself , “ If you and I were destined to live always together , cousin , we would commence matters on a different footing . I should not settle tamely down into being the forbearing party ; I should assign you your share of labor , and compel you to accomplish it , or else it should be left undone ; I should insist , also , on your keeping some of those drawling , half - insincere complaints hushed in your own breast . It is only because our connection happens to be very transitory , and comes at a peculiarly mournful season , that I consent thus to render it so patient and compliant on my part .”

羅徹斯特先生只給了我一周的假期，但一個月後我才離開蓋茨黑德。我本想在葬礼结束后立即离开，但乔治亚娜恳求我留下来，直到她能动身去伦敦，她的舅舅吉布森先生终于邀请她去伦敦，他是来指导妹妹的安葬事宜，并处理家族事务的。乔治亚娜说她害怕一个人和伊丽莎呆在一起；从她那里，她的沮丧得不到同情，她的恐惧得不到支持，她的准备工作也得不到帮助；所以我尽可能地忍受着她虚弱的哀嚎和自私的悲叹，尽我所能地为她缝补衣服，为她收拾行装。的确，当我工作的时候，她总是无所事事；我心想："表姐，如果你和我注定要永远生活在一起，我们就应该在不同的基础上开始新的生活。我不应该安于现状，做忍让的一方；我应该分派你的工作，强迫你完成，否则就不做了；我还应该坚持要求你把那些絮絮叨叨、半真半假的抱怨压在心底。只是因为我们之间的关系很短暂，而且又是在一个特别悲痛的季节，所以我才同意让你这么忍耐，让你这么顺从。"

At last I saw Georgiana off ; but now it was Eliza ’ s turn to request me to stay another week . Her plans required all her time and attention , she said ; she was about to depart for some unknown bourn ; and all day long she stayed in her own room , her door bolted within , filling trunks , emptying drawers , burning papers , and holding no communication with any one . She wished me to look after the house , to see callers , and answer notes of condolence .

最后，我送走了乔治亚娜；但现在轮到伊丽莎请求我再多留一个星期了。她说，她的计划需要她投入所有的时间和精力；她即将启程去某个未知的地方；她整天呆在自己的房间里，房门紧锁，装满箱子，清空抽屉，烧毁文件，不与任何人联系。她希望我照看房子，接见来访者，回复慰问信。

One morning she told me I was at liberty . “ And ,” she added , “ I am obliged to you for your valuable services and discreet conduct . There is some difference between living with such a one as you , and with Georgiana ; you perform your own part in life , and burden no one . To - morrow ,” she continued , “ I set out for the continent . I shall take up my abode in a religious house , near Lisle — a nunnery , you would call it ; there I shall be quiet and unmolested . I shall devote myself for a time to the examination of the Roman Catholic dogmas , and to a careful study of the workings of their system ; if I find it to be , as I half suspect it is , the one best calculated to insure the doing of all things decently and in order , I shall embrace the tenets of Rome and probably take the veil .”

一天早上，她告诉我可以自由活动了。"她还说："我还要感谢你的宝贵服务和谨慎行为。与你这样的人生活在一起和与乔治亚娜生活在一起是有区别的；你在生活中履行自己的职责，不给任何人添负担。明天，"她继续说，"我启程去欧洲大陆。我将住在里斯勒附近的一所修道院里，你可以叫它尼姑庵；在那里我将安安静静，不受打扰。我将在一段时间内潜心研究罗马天主教的教义，并仔细研究其体系的运作；如果我发现，正如我半信半疑的那样，它是最能保证一切事情体面有序地进行的体系，我将接受罗马的信条，并很可能会披上面纱"。

I neither expressed surprise at this resolution nor attempted to dissuade her from it . “ The vocation will fit you to a hair ,” I thought ; “ much good may it do you !”

我既没有对她的这一决定表示惊讶，也没有试图劝阻她。我想，"这个使命会让你如虎添翼"，"对你大有裨益！"

When we parted , she said : “ Good - by , cousin Jane Eyre , I wish you well ; you have some sense .”

我们分别时，她说："再见，简-爱表妹，祝你好运，你还有点理智"

I then returned : “ You are not without sense , cousin Eliza ; but what you have , I suppose , in another year will be walled up alive in a French convent . However , it is not my business , and , so it suits you , I don ’ t much care .”

我回道"你不是没有理智，伊丽莎表妹；但我想，你所拥有的，再过一年 就会被活生生地囚禁在法国修道院里了。不过，这不关我的事，你高兴就好，我也不在乎。"

“ You are in the right ,” said she ; and with these words we each went our separate way . As I shall not have occasion to refer either to her or her sister again , I may as well mention here that Georgiana made an advantageous match with a wealthy worn - out man of fashion ; and that Eliza actually took the veil , and is at this day superior of the convent where she passed the period of her novitiate , and which she endowed with her fortune .

"你是对的，"她说，然后我们就各奔东西了。由于我没有机会再提到她或她的妹妹，我不妨在这里提一下，乔治亚娜与一个富有的、过时的时尚人士做了一桩有利的婚事；伊丽莎真的戴上了面纱，如今是修道院的院长，她在那里度过了她的见习期，并将她的财产捐给了修道院。

How people feel when they are returning home after an absence , long or short , I did not know . I had never experienced the sensation . I had known what it was to come back to Gateshead , when a child , after a long walk — to be scolded for looking cold or gloomy ; and later , what it was to come back from church to Lowood — to long for a plenteous meal and a good fire , and to be unable to get either . Neither of these returnings were very pleasant or desirable ; no magnet drew me to a given point , increasing in its strength of attraction the nearer I came . The return to Thornfield was yet to be tried .

我不知道人们在离开家很久或很短时间后回到家会有什么感觉。我从未体验过这种感觉。我知道小时候走了很远的路回到盖茨黑德是什么感觉--因为看起来很冷或很阴沉而挨骂；后来，从教堂回到洛伍德又是什么感觉--渴望一顿丰盛的饭菜和一个好火炉，但两者都得不到。这两次回来都不太愉快，也不太令人向往；没有磁铁把我吸引到某一点，越靠近就越有吸引力。返回桑菲尔德还有待考验。

My journey seemed tedious — very tedious ; fifty miles one day , a night spent at an inn ; fifty miles the next day . During the first twelve hours I thought of Mrs . Reed in her last moments ; I saw her disfigured and discolored face , and heard her strangely altered voice ; I mused on the funeral day , the coffin , the hearse , the black train of tenants and servants — few was the number of relatives — the gaping vault , the silent church , the solemn service . Then I thought of Eliza and Georgiana . I beheld one the cynosure of a ball - room , the other the inmate of a convent cell ; and I dwelt on and analyzed their separate peculiarities of person and character . The evening arrival at the great town of — scattered these thoughts ; night gave them quite another turn . Laid down on my traveller ’ s bed , I left reminiscence for anticipation .

我的旅途似乎很乏味--非常乏味；一天走了 50 英里，在旅馆住了一晚；第二天又走了 50 英里。在最初的十二个小时里，我想到了里德夫人最后的时刻；我看到了她毁容、褪色的脸，听到了她怪异变化的声音；我想到了葬礼那天，棺材、灵车、黑压压的佃户和仆人--只有少数几个亲戚--空旷的穹顶、寂静的教堂、庄严的仪式。然后我想起了伊丽莎和乔治亚娜。一个是舞厅的焦点，一个是修道院的囚徒，我仔细观察并分析了她们各自的性格和特点。傍晚抵达大城时，我的思绪被打散了；夜晚又让我的思绪有了新的变化。躺在旅人的床上，我把回忆留给了期待。

I was going back to Thornfield ; but how long was I to stay there ? Not long — of that I was sure . I had heard from Mrs . Fairfax in the interim of my absence . The party at the hall was dispersed ; Mr . Rochester had left for London three weeks ago , but he was then expected to return in a fortnight . Mrs . Fairfax surmised that he was gone to make arrangements for his wedding , as he had talked of purchasing a new carriage . She said , the idea of his marrying Miss Ingram still seemed strange to her ; but from what everybody said , and from what she had herself seen , she could no longer doubt that the event would shortly take place . “ You would be strangely incredulous if you did doubt it ,” was my mental comment ; “ I don ’ t doubt it .”

我要回桑菲尔德了，但我要在那里呆多久呢？我确信不会太久。在我离开的这段时间里，我收到了费尔法克斯太太的消息。羅徹斯特先生三周前去了倫敦，但預計兩星期後回來。費爾法克斯太太猜測他是去為婚禮做安排，因為他說要買一輛新馬車。她说，他要娶英格拉姆小姐的想法对她来说仍然很奇怪；但从大家的说法和她自己的所见所闻来看，她已经不再怀疑这件事很快就会发生了。"如果你真的怀疑，那就太不可思议了，"我在心里评论道，"我不怀疑。

The question followed , “ Where was I to go ?” I dreamed of Miss Ingram all the night . In a vivid morning dream , I saw her closing the gates of Thornfield against me and pointing me out another road ; and Mr . Rochester looked on with his arms folded , smiling sardonically , as it seemed , at both her and me .

I had not notified to Mrs . Fairfax the exact day of my return , for I did not wish either carfr or carriage to meet me at Millcote . I proposed to walk the distance quietly by myself ; and very quietly , after leaving my box in the hostler ’ s care , did I slip away from the George Inn , about six o ‘ clock of a June evening , and take the old road to Thornfield ; a road which lay chiefly through fields , and was now little frequented .

It was not a bright or splendid summer evening , though fair and soft . The hay - makers were at work all along the road , and the sky , though far from cloudless , was such as promised well for the future — its blue , where blue was visible , was mild and settled , and its cloud strata high and thin . The west , too , was warm ; no watery gleam chilled it ; it seemed as if there was a fire lighted — an altar burning behind its screen of marbled vapor — and out of apertures shone a golden redness .

虽然天气晴朗，阳光柔和，但这并不是一个明亮灿烂的夏夜。干草机正在沿路工作，天空虽然远非万里无云，但却预示着美好的未来--它的蓝，蓝得清晰可见，温和而稳定，云层又高又薄。西边也是温暖的，没有一丝寒冷的水光，仿佛点燃了一团火--在大理石般的水汽屏风后燃烧着一座祭坛，从光圈中闪耀出金色的红光。

I felt glad as the road shortened before me — so glad that I stopped once to ask myself what that joy meant , and to remind reason that it was not to my home I was going , or to a permanent resting - place , or to a place where fond friends looked out for me and waited my arrival . “ Mrs . Fairfax will smile you a calm welcome , to be sure ,” said I ; “ and little Ad è le will clap her hands and jump to see you ; but you know very well you are thinking of another than they , and that he is not thinking of you .”

当我眼前的路越来越短时，我感到很高兴--高兴得我停了下来，问自己这种高兴意味着什么，并提醒自己，我要去的不是我的家，不是一个永久的安息之地，也不是一个有深情厚谊的朋友眷顾我、等待我到来的地方。"费尔法克斯太太一定会微笑着欢迎你，"我说，"小阿黛尔见到你也会拍手雀跃；但你很清楚，你想的不是她们，而是另一个人，他想的不是你。

But what is so headstrong as youth — what so blind as inexperience ? These affirmed that it was pleasure enough to have the privilege of again looking on Mr . Rochester , whether he looked on me or not ; and they added —“ Hasten ! hasten ! be with him while you may ; but a few more days or weeks , at most , and you are parted from him forever !” And then I strangled a new - born agony — a deformed thing which I could not persuade myself to own and rear — and ran on .

但是，还有什么比年轻更任性，还有什么比缺乏经验更盲目呢？他们肯定地说，能有幸再次见到罗切斯特先生，无论他是否在看我，都已足够高兴；他们还说："快点！快点！趁现在还来得及，和他在一起吧；但最多再过几天或几周，你就要和他永别了！"然后，我掐死了一只刚出生的小雏鸟--一只我无法说服自己拥有和抚养的畸形儿--然后跑了。

They are making hay , too , in Thornfield meadows ; or , rather , the laborers are just quitting their work , and returning home with their rakes on their shoulders , now , at the hour I arrive . I have but a field or two to traverse , and then I shall cross the road and reach the gates . How full the hedges are of roses ! but I have no time to gather any ; I want to be at the house . I pass a tall brier , shooting leafy and flowery branches across the path ; I see the narrow stile with stone steps ; and I see — Mr . Rochester sitting there , a book and pencil in his hand . He is writing .

Well , he is not a ghost — yet every nerve I have is unstrung ; for a moment I am beyond my own mastery . What does it mean ? I did not think I should tremble in this way when I saw him , or lose my voice or the power of motion in his presence . I will go back as soon as I can stir ; I need not make an absolute fool of myself ; I know another way to the house . It does not signify if I knew twenty ways , for he has seen me .

他不是鬼魂，但我的每一根神經都繃緊了；剎那間，我超越了自己的掌控。这意味着什么？我没想到见到他时会如此颤抖，也没想到在他面前会失声或失去行动能力。我一有力气就会回去；我不需要出丑；我知道另一条路去那所房子。我知道二十条路并不重要，因为他已经看见我了。

“ And this is Jane Eyre ! Are you coming from Millcote , and on foot ? "我一直和我姨妈在一起，先生，她已经去世了"

"一个真正的雅尼安人的回答！善良的天使在守护着我她来自另一个世界--来自已死之人的居所--当她在阴暗中与我独处时告诉我的！如果我敢的话，我会摸摸你，看看你到底是实体还是影子，你这个精灵！但我宁愿在沼泽里抓住一束蓝色的火光。逃学！逃学！"他停了一会儿，又说，"整整一个月不在我身边，把我忘得一干二净，我敢发誓！"

I knew there would be pleasure in meeting my master again , even though broken by the fear that he was so soon to cease to be my master , and by the knowledge that I was nothing to him ; but there was ever in Mr . Rochester ( so , at least , I thought ) such a wealth of the power of communicating happiness , that to taste but of the crumbs he scattered to stray and stranger birds like me , was to feast genially . His last words were balm . They seemed to imply that it imported something to him whether I forgot him or not . And he had spoken of Thornfield as my home — would that it were my home !

我知道再次见到我的主人会很高兴，即使因为担心他很快就不再是我的主人，也因为知道我对他来说什么都不是而伤心；但罗切斯特先生身上（至少我是这么认为的）永远都有一种传递幸福的力量，只要尝尝他撒给像我这样的流浪鸟和陌生鸟的面包屑，就能享受到美味的盛宴。他的最后一句话是一剂良药。他的遗言似乎在暗示，我是否忘记他，对他来说并不重要。他说桑菲尔德是我的家--但愿那是我的家！

Mr . Rochester had sometimes read my unspoken thoughts with an acumen to me incomprehensible ; in the present instance he took no notice of my abrupt vocal response , but he smiled at me with a certain smile he had of his own , and which he used but on rare occasions . He seemed to think it too good for common purposes ; it was the real sunshine of feeling — he shed it over me now .

罗切斯特先生有时会以一种我无法理解的敏锐读懂我未曾言明的心思；而这次，他并没有注意到我突如其来的回应，而是对我露出了他自己的某种微笑，这种微笑他只在极少的场合才会使用。他似乎觉得这样的微笑太美好了，不适合一般的场合；这是真正的阳光般的感觉--他现在把它洒在了我的身上。

“ Pass , Janet ,” said he , making room for me to cross the stile ; “ go up home , and stay your weary little wandering feet at a friend ’ s threshold .”

"过去吧，珍妮特，"他说，给我让出了过栅栏的空间；"回家去吧，在朋友家的门槛上停留一下你那双疲惫的流浪的小脚。"

All I had now to do was to obey him in silence ; no need for me to colloquize further . I got over the stile without a word , and meant to leave him calmly . An impulse held me fast — a force turned me round ; I said — or something in me said for me , and in spite of me —

我现在要做的就是默默地顺从他，没有必要再多说什么。我一言不发地跨过栅栏，打算平静地离开他。

“ Thank you , Mr . Rochester , for your great kindness . I am strangely glad to get back again to you ; and wherever you are is my home — my only home .”

"谢谢您，罗切斯特先生，谢谢您的好意。能再次回到您身边，我感到非常高兴；您在哪里，哪里就是我的家，我唯一的家。

I walked on so fast that even he could hardly have overtaken me had he tried . Little Ad è le was half wild with delight when she saw me . Mrs . Fairfax received me with her usual plain friendliness . Leah smiled , and even Sophie bid me “ bon soir ” fu with glee . This was very pleasant ; there is no happiness like that of being loved by our fellow - creatures , and feeling that your presence is an addition to their comfort .

我走得很快，即使他想追上我也很难。小阿黛尔看到我，高兴得半疯了。费尔法克斯太太一如既往地友好地接待了我。莉娅笑了，连索菲也高兴地向我道谢，说 "晚上好"。这让我感到非常愉快；没有什么比被我们的同胞所爱，并感觉到你的到来给他们带来了更多的安慰更幸福的了。

I that evening shut my eyes resolutely against the future ; I stopped my ears against the voice that kept warning me of near separation and coming grief . When tea was over , and Mrs . Fairfax had taken her knitting , and I had assumed a low seat near her , and Ad è le , kneeling on the carpet , had nestled close up to me , and a sense of mutual affection seemed to surround us with a ring of golden peace , I uttered a silent prayer that we might not be parted far or soon ; but when , as we thus sat , Mr . Rochester entered , unannounced , and looking at us , seemed to take pleasure in the spectacle of a group so amicable — when he said he supposed the old lady was all right now that she had got her adopted daughter back again , and added that he saw Ad è le was “ pr ê te à croquer sa petite maman Anglaise ” fv — I half ventured to hope that he would , even after his marriage , keep us together somewhere under the shelter of his protection , and not quite exiled from the sunshine of his presence .

那天晚上，我坚决地闭上眼睛，不去想未来；我竖起耳朵，不去听那不断警告我即将分离和即将到来的悲伤的声音。茶會結束後，費爾法克斯太太拿起編織的織物，我在她身旁坐下，阿黛勒跪在地毯上，緊緊依偎著我，一種相互的親切感似乎在我們周圍繞著一圈金色的安寧，我默默祈禱，希望我們不要分開得太遠，也不要分開得太快。罗切斯特先生不请自来，看着我们，似乎很高兴看到我们如此和睦相处--他说老夫人现在应该没事了，因为她的养女又回来了，还说他看到阿黛尔 "准备和她的英国小妈妈结婚 "fv--我半信半疑地希望他即使在婚后，也会让我们在他的庇护下在某个地方相聚，而不是完全远离他的阳光。

A fortnight of dubious calm succeeded my return to Thorn field Hall . Nothing was said of the master ’ s marriage , and I saw no preparation going on for such an event . Almost every day I asked Mrs . Fairfax if she had yet heard anything decided ; her answer was always in the negative . Once , she said , she had actually put the question to Mr . Rochester , as to when he was going to bring his bride home ; but he had answered her only by a joke , and one of his queer looks , and she could not tell what to make of him .

在我回到荆棘田庄园之后，又过了两个星期令人怀疑的平静期。主人的婚事只字未提，我也没有看到任何准备工作。我几乎每天都问费尔法克斯太太是否听说有什么决定；她的回答总是否定的。她說有一次她真的問過羅徹斯特先生，什麼時候帶新娘回家，但他只用一個笑話和古怪的眼神回答了她，她也不知道該怎麼回答。

One thing specially surprised me , and that was , there were no journeyings backward and forward — no visits to Ingram Park . To be sure it was twenty miles off , on the borders of another county ; but what was that distance to an ardent lover ? To so practised and indefatigable a horseman as Mr . Rochester , it would be but a morning ’ s ride . I began to cherish hopes I had no right to conceive — that the match was broken off — that rumor had been mistaken — that one or both parties had changed their minds . I used to look at my master ’ s face to see if it were sad or fierce ; but I could not remember the time when it had been so uniformly clear of clouds or evil feelings . If , in the moments I and my pupil spent with him , I lacked spirits and sunk into inevitable dejection , he became even gay . Never had he called me more frequently to his presence — never been kinder to me when there — and , alas ! never had I loved him so well .

有一件事让我特别吃惊，那就是他没有前后左右的旅行，也没有去英格拉姆公园。当然，英格拉姆公园离这里有二十英里远，在另一个郡的边界上；但对于一个热恋中的情人来说，这点距离又算得了什么呢？對於羅徹斯特先生這位騎術精湛、鍥而不捨的騎士來說，也不過是一上午的路程。我開始抱有無可厚非的希望，希望這場戀愛已經破滅，希望傳言是錯誤的，希望其中一方或雙方已經改變了主意。我常常看着主人的脸，看它是悲伤还是狰狞；但我不记得主人的脸何时如此清澈，没有一丝阴云或邪恶的情绪。在我和徒弟与他共处的时光里，如果我缺乏精神，陷入不可避免的沮丧，他甚至会变得很开心。他从未如此频繁地把我叫到他的面前，从未如此亲切地对待我，唉！我从未如此爱过他。
